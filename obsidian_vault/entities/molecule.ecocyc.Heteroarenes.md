## Enriched Summary

An aromatic compound contains one or more aromatic rings. The pi orbitals of the ring's atoms form a conjugated system and are in resonance, giving increased stability compared to saturated compounds having a similar structure. As a result aromatic rings are very stable and do not break apart easily. Similar structures in which at least one of the ring's carbon atoms is replaced by one of the heteroatoms oxygen, nitrogen, or sulfur, are called heteroarenes.

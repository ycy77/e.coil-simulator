## Enriched Summary

Rhodoquinone (RQ) is a modified ubiquinone, where the methoxyl group in position 3 is replaced by an amino group. The compound was originally isolated from TAX-1085 , where is it used for the reduction of fumarate to succinate under anaerobic conditions. Rhodoquinone is not limited to prokaryotes. It was soon discovered that some eukaryotic parasites such as the nematodes TAX-6253 and TAX-6252 , as well as parasitic helminths also contain rhodoquinone, which is essential for fumarate reduction under anaerobic conditions . Later it was shown that rhodoquinone is present in all eukaryotes that reduce fumarate during anoxia, including freshwater snails, mussels, lugworms, and oysters . More recently, rhodoquinone has been detected in mitochondria purified from certain mouse and human tissues and it has been suggested that RQ is produced by all mammals . .

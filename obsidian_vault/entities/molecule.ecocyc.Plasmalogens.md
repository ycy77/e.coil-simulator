## Enriched Summary

Plasmalogens are a type of a glycerophospholipid characterized by the presence of a vinyl ether linkage at the sn-1 position and an ester linkage at the sn-2 position. They are found in animals and some anaerobic bacteria, but not in plants, fungi, or most aerobic bacteria, except myxobacteria. In mammals, the sn-1 position is typically derived from C16:0, C18:0, or C18:1 fatty alcohols while the sn-2 position is most commonly occupied by polyunsaturated fatty acids. When the group at the sn-1 position is saturated, the compound is called a plasmanyl-phospholipid. When the compound is unsaturated, it is known as plasmenyl-phospholipid, or plasmalogen. The most common head groups present in mammalian plasmalogens are ethanolamine (designated Alkyl-enyl-acyl-gly-P-EtOH-amines plasmenylethalomines) or choline (designated PLASMENYLCHOLINE plasmenylcholines).

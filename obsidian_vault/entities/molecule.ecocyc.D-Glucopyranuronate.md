## Enriched Summary

GLUCURONATE is a reducing acidic sugar that often forms a hexapyranose ring structure. It is common in carbohydrate chains of proteoglycans and is part of mucous animal secretions (such as saliva), cell glycocalyx and intercellular matrix The hexapyranosic form CPD-12521 is found in the glycosaminoglycans Chondroitin-sulfates, Dermatan-Sulfate, HEPARIN, Heparan-Sulfate and Hyaluronan.

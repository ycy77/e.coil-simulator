## Enriched Summary

Galactolipids are the main lipids of the membranes of chloroplasts and photosynthetic microorganisms. The main galactolipids in these membranes are D-Galactosyl-12-diacyl-glycerols (MGDG) and Galactosyl-galactosyl-diacyl-glycerols (DGDG). These compounds have been found in the X-ray structures of photosynthetic complexes, and are believed to assume a direct role in photosynthesis . The galactolipids D-galactosylceramide galactocerebrosides and Cerebroside-3-sulfates "galactocerebrosides 3-sulfate" are abundant components in myelin, the membrane around the axons in the nervous system of vertebrates . In addition, some galactolipids act as secondary metabolites and herbivore deterrents in plants and algae .

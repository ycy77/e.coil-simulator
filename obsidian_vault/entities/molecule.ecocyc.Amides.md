## Enriched Summary

Amides are derivatives of oxoacids in which an acidic hydroxy group has been replaced by an amino or substituted amino group. Most amide compounds fall under Carboxamides carboxamides, where the nitrogen atom is bonded with a carbon atom. However, the term amide also refers to compounds where the nitrogen is bonded to a non-carbon atom (see for example Sulfonamides).

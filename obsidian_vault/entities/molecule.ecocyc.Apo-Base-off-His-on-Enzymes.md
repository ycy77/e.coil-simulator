## Enriched Summary

Adenosylcobalamin-dependent enzymes fall into one of three groups: carbon-skeleton mutases (e.g. EC-5.4.99.1, EC-5.4.99.4, EC-5.4.99.13, EC-5.4.99.64, EC-5.4.99.2, and EC-5.4.99.63); eliminases (e.g. EC-4.3.1.7, EC-4.2.1.28, EC-4.2.1.30, and EC-1.17.4.2), and aminomutases, which also require pyridoxal phosphate as a cofactor (e.g. EC-5.4.3.5 and EC-5.4.3.3). In the carbon skeleton mutases and aminomutases an HIS residue, part of a conserved DxHxxG motif from the protein , replaces the dimethylbenzimidazole tail as the lower ligand to cobalt, which is instead extended down into the core of the protein. This mode of binding has been termed "base-off/His-on"" . In contrast, in the eliminases the adenosylcobalamin is bound with the dimethylbenzimidazole base coordinated to cobalt, as it is in free solution. This mode is known as "dmb-on" . Why different proteins use different binding mode of their adenosylcobalamin cofactor remains unclear .

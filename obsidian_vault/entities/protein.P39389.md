## Enriched Summary

Uncharacterized HTH-type transcriptional regulator YjiR YjiR, which contains a helix-turn-helix motif to bind DNA in its N-terminal domain, appears to belong to the DeoR family of transcription factors, and it was predicted to regulate genes related to antibiotic sensibility . YjiR may have a positive role in csgD expression . Upstream of the yjiR gene, a sequence was found that could work as a transcriptional promoter, and the transcription of the yjiR gene is affected by DNA damage . The YjiR binding landscape was globally mapped in vivo by standardized ChIP-seq and quantitatively modeled using BoltzNet, a biophysically informed neural network that infers binding energy from sequence data . The resulting ChIP-seq binding regions are available in RegulonDB (Galagan collection), and the corresponding energy matrix and genomic binding profiles can be accessed at boltznet.bu.edu .

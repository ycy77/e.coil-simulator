## Enriched Summary

Heparosan "Heparosan" (also known as heparan) is a member of the Glycosaminoglycans "glycosaminoglycan" family. It consists of a repeating disaccharide unit composed of CPD-12521-(1,4)-CPD-12541. The disaccharide units are linked by a (1-4) linkage to each other. Heparosan is a bacterial glycosaminoglycan produced and found in the capsule of some pathogenic bacteria such as Escherichia coli K5 and Pasteurella multocida. The heparosan skeleton serves as a starting point in the biosynthesis of several other glycosaminoglycan such as Heparan-Sulfate and HEPARIN, and bacterial heparosan is sometimes used for the synthesis of non-animal sourced heparin-like products.

## Enriched Summary

tolR (gene.b0738) is a gene entity. It encodes tolR (protein.P0ABV6). Encoded protein function: FUNCTION: Part of the Tol-Pal system, which plays a role in outer membrane invagination during cell division and is important for maintaining outer membrane integrity (PubMed:1683466, PubMed:17233825). Required, with TolQ, for the proton motive force-dependent activation of TolA and for TolA-Pal interaction (PubMed:11722743). The Tol-Pal system is also required for polar localization of chemoreceptors clusters (PubMed:24720726). The system also appears to be required for the activity of several outer membrane-localized enzymes with cell wall remodeling activity (PubMed:32152098). Modeling suggests that non-covalent binding of OmpA (from the outer membrane) and TolR (from the inner membrane) to peptidoglycan maintains the position of the cell wall in the periplasm, holding it approximately equidistant from both the inner and outer membranes.

## Enriched Summary

Arabinoses Arabinose is an aldopentose - a monosaccharide containing five carbon atoms. Unlike many other aldoses, which are more abundant in nature as the "D"-form, L-ARABINOSE is far more common in nature than D-ARABINOSE. Free L-ARABINOSE rarely exists in the nature (though it has been found in the core of coniferous trees ). However, it is widely distributed as a component of complex nonstarch polysaccharides in plant cell walls, including many plants common in the human diet such as maize, wheat, rye, rice, sugar beets, and plant gums, and in bacterial polysaccharides. L-arabinose has many biomedical and health effects, especially intestinal sucrase inhibition effect . In recent years it has been used as a low-caloric sweetener, with approximately 50% of the sweetness of sucrose .

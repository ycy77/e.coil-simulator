## Enriched Summary

Monosaccharides are polyhydroxy aldehydes or polyhydroxy ketones with three or more carbon atoms. The generic term monosaccharide denotes a single unit, without glycosidic connection to other such units. Monosaccharides with an aldehydic carbonyl group are called aldoses, while those with a ketonic carbonyl carbonyl group are called ketoses. In addition to aldoses and ketose, the term 'monosaccharide' also includes additional forms, such as dialdoses, alduloses, and diketoses, as well as deoxy and amino monosaccharides, and their derivatives, provided that the parent compound has a (potential) carbonyl group.

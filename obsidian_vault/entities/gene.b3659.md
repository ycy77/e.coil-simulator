## Enriched Summary

setC (gene.b3659) is a gene entity. It encodes setC (protein.P31436). Encoded protein function: FUNCTION: Involved in the efflux of sugars. The physiological role may be the detoxification of non-metabolizable sugar analogs. EcoCyc product frame: B3659-MONOMER. EcoCyc synonyms: yicK. Genomic coordinates: 3836953-3838137. EcoCyc protein note: SetC has significant sequence similarity with the sugar efflux transporters SetA and SetB from the major facilitator superfamily; glucose, galactose and lactose transport activities are not observed in everted membrane vesicles prepared from cells overexpressing setC . Deletion of setC increases intracellular arabinose concentration (measured indirectly using a fluorescent transcriptional reporter) and overexpression of setC reduces intracellular arabinose concentration compared to wild type . A SetC-GFP fusion protein localizes in a helical pattern in the cell . In the Transporter Classification Database , SetC is a member of the Sugar Efflux Transporter (SET) family within the Major Facilitator Superfamily.

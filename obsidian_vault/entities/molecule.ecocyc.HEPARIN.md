## Enriched Summary

HEPARIN "Heparin" is a glycosaminoglycan polymer with a molecular weight ranging from 3 to 30 kDa. It is composed of a variable sulfated repeating disaccharide units composed of hexuronic (either iduronic or glucuronic) acid and glucosamine residues, in which the 2 position of the hexuronic acid and the 2, 3, or 6 positions of glucosamine may be sulfated. The two residues within a disaccharide are linked by a (1->4) bond, as are the disaccharides to each other. The main disaccharide unit consists of CPD-12518 and CPD-12533 [it is often referred to as IdoA(2S)-(1->4)-GlcNS(6S)]. However, CPD-12518 can be substituted by CPD-12521, and CPD-12533 can be replaced by N-ACETYL-BETA-D-GLUCOSAMINE, N-sulfo-D-glucosamine, CPD-12545 or CPD-12565. The basic IdoA(2S)-(1->4)-GlcNS(6S) disaccharide accounts for at least 85% of heparin isolated from beef lung and about 75% of heparin from porcine intestinal mucosa . ## Annotation

HEPARIN "Heparin" is a glycosaminoglycan polymer with a molecular weight ranging from 3 to 30 kDa. It is composed of a variable sulfated repeating disaccharide units composed of hexuronic (either iduronic or glucuronic) acid and glucosamine residues, in which the 2 position of the hexuronic acid and the 2, 3, or 6 positions of glucosamine may be sulfated. The two residues within a disaccharide are linked by a (1->4) bond, as are the disaccharides to each other. The main disaccharide unit consists of CPD-12518 and CPD-12533 [it is often referred to as IdoA(2S)-(1->4)-GlcNS(6S)]. However, CPD-12518 can be substituted by CPD-12521, and CPD-12533 can be replaced by N-ACETYL-BETA-D-GLUCOSAMINE, N-sulfo-D-glucosamine, CPD-12545 or CPD-12565. The basic IdoA(2S)-(1->4)-GlcNS(6S) disaccharide accounts for at least 85% of heparin isolated from beef lung and about 75% of heparin from porcine intestinal mucosa . Variations in the extent and location of sulfation, as well as the conformation of the alternating hexuronic acid residues, lead to a high degree of heterogeneity. By convention, heparin refers to molecules which possess a high sulfate content and large amounts of iduronic acid. The structure of HEPARIN is very similar to that of Heparan-Sulfate (also known as heparitin sulfate). Heparan sulfate contains lower amounts of sulfate and less iduronic acid than heparin . In addition, heparan sulfates disaccharide units are organized into distinct sulfated and non-sulfated domains, while heparin disaccharides are sulfated more uniformly .

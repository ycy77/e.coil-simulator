## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: May play a role, directly or indirectly, in the biosynthesis of colanic acid (PubMed:15183873). Overexpression confers a mucoid colony phenotype to E.coli strain CM2555 (PubMed:15183873). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15183873}. YpdI is a putative membrane lipoprotein which has been shown to play a role in colonic acid synthesis. Colanic acid is the main extracellular polysaccharide in Escherichia coli and its synthesis involves at least two pathways of induction and 20 different genes . Capsular polysaccharide production is effected by both a two-component signal transduction system, RcsC and RcsB and a positive transcription regulator, RcsA . YpdI plasmid overexpression was shown to confer a mucoid colony phenotype to E. coli . Extracts of plasmid-bearing cells were subjected to SDS-PAGE analysis and an excess of a protein corresponding to the predicted size of the ypdI gene product was observed .

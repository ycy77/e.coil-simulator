## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Modulates intracellular Mg(2+) levels to maintain cellular integrity upon Mg(2+) limitation. Acts by binding and stabilizing the Mg(2+) transporter MgtA, thereby leading to increased intracellular level of Mg(2+). May inhibit FtsH proteolysis of MgtA. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:28512220}. MgtS is a small, inner membrane protein which interacts with the Mg2+ transporter, MGTA-MONOMER "MgtA" and the PITA-MONOMER "PitA low-affinity phosphate transporter" to promote intracellular Mg2+ accumulation; the interactions are implicated in regulatory cross-talk between Mg2+ and phosphate homeostasis When grown under Mg2+ limitation and compared to the wild-type, a ΔmgtS mutant has lower intracellular Mg2+ levels and displays a growth defect; MgtS and MgtA co-purify in a reciprocal manner . MgtA accumulates normally in a ΔmgtS strain that overexpresses a known FtsH substrate (phage lambda cII protein), indicating that MgtS may act to stabilize MgtA by inhibiting FtsH proteolysis of MgtA . Overexpression of mgtS activates the PWY0-1483 "PhoRB two-component system" during growth in rich media and this activation requires the PITA-MONOMER "PitA low-affinity phosphate transporter"; MgtS interacts physically with PitA and this interaction contributes to an increase in intracellular Mg2+ levels...

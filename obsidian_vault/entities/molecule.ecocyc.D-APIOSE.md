## Enriched Summary

Apiose is a unique branched-chain pentose found principally in plants. It is a key component of structurally complex cell wall polysaccharides such as CPD-14581 "rhamnogalacturonan-II" (RG-II) in the cell walls of higher plants and in apigalacturonan in the cell walls of aquatic monocots, and is a building block of a large number of naturally occurring secondary metabolites. The name "apiose" is derived from the flavonoid glycoside 7-O-BETA-D-APIOFURANOSYL-12-BETA-D-GLU apiin, from which it was first obtained .

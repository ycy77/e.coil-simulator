## Enriched Summary

PEP synthetase regulatory protein (PSRP, PpsR) catalyzes both the Pi-dependent activation and ADP/ATP-dependent inactivation of PEPSYNTH-MONOMER (PEPS, PpsA). PEPS is protected from inactivation by the presence of pyruvate . In a proteome-wide screen, PpsR was found to bind fructose-1,6-bisphosphate (FBP). In the presence of PpsR, addition of FBP to an assay measuring PEP synthetase activity of PpsA yields a 15% reduction of activity . The function of PSRP was predicted based on membership in the DUF299 protein family, which also contains the previously characterized pyruvate, orthophosphate dikinase regulatory protein (PDRP) from maize and Arabidopsis . Knockout of ppsR together with mutations in four additional genes are sufficient for an E. coli strain with an engineered Calvin-Benson-Bassham cycle to be able to produce sugar from CO2 .

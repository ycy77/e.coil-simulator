## Enriched Summary

Phytochelatins are the principal heavy-metal-detoxifying components in plant, fungi, and nematodes . They are formed from GLUTATHIONE by EC-2.3.2.15 (better known as phytochelatin synthase) and consist of repeating units (2-11) of γ-glutamylcysteine followed by a C-terminal Gly, Ser, or beta-Ala residue (although CPD-24346 and CPD-24347 are the most common). Phytochelatins play a pivotal role in heavy metal, primarily Cd2+, tolerance by chelating these substances and decreasing their free concentrations.

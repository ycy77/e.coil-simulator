## Enriched Summary

Camphor is produced by several trees, including the camphor tree (TAX-13429) as well as hundreds of other plants, including TAX-38868 (garden sage), TAX-39367 (rosemary), TAX-39169 (lavender), TAX-35608 (sweet wormwood), and TAX-13328 . A study of enzymes from the camphor tree found multiple terpene synthases, two that lead to production of CPD-862 and one that leads to production of S-CAMPHOR . ## Annotation

Camphor Camphor is a bicyclic monoterpene with a characteristic pungent odor and taste. Two enantiomers occur naturally - CPD-862, the predominant natural isomer, and S-CAMPHOR. Camphor is produced by several trees, including the camphor tree (TAX-13429) as well as hundreds of other plants, including TAX-38868 (garden sage), TAX-39367 (rosemary), TAX-39169 (lavender), TAX-35608 (sweet wormwood), and TAX-13328 . A study of enzymes from the camphor tree found multiple terpene synthases, two that lead to production of CPD-862 and one that leads to production of S-CAMPHOR . Camphor can be synthesized chemically, as an racemic mixture of the two enantiomers, from CPD-4892, which is obtained from oil of turpentine. Today most camphor is synthetic. It has a wide range of uses, including as a plasticizer for modified celluloses in lacquers, varnishes, and plastics; as a moth repellente; and as a preservative in cosmetics and embalming fluids. It is also used in medicine as an ingredient in topical creams and ointments to treat itching, irritation, and joint pain. Although it is found in essential oils used in perfumery (such as those of lavender), it significantly diminishes the quality of these oils and reduces their market value. Oils that contain elevated camphor content are usually used as insect repellents and for other non-perfumery uses . Even though camphor has been traditionally used in foods and medicines, it is toxic to humans and is especially dangerous to young children. Contact can lead to skin and eye irritation. Ingestion of camphor can cause irritability, seizures, cardiac arrhythmias, and cardiopulmonary arrest . Camphorated oil was removed from the US market in the 1980s due to accidental poisonings.

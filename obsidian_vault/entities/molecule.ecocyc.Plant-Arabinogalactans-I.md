## Enriched Summary

Arabinogalactans (AGs) are widely spread throughout the plant kingdom. Many edible and inedible plants are rich sources of these polysaccharides. AGs are associated with the pectin cell-wall component by physical bonds, and some of them are covalently linked to the complex pectin molecule as neutral side chains . AGs have been classified into two general forms, named Plant-Arabinogalactans-I "type I" and Plant-Arabinogalactans-II "type II". AGs of type I have a linear β-(1→4)-D-Galp backbone, while those of type II have a β-(1→3)-D-Galp backbone. 20-40% of the type I AGs consist of short chains of α-L-Araf residues, mostly (1→5)-linked at position 3. It is commonly found in pectins from citrus, apple and potato. Type I AG has been isolated from the skin of TAX-371859 (prickly pear) fruits .

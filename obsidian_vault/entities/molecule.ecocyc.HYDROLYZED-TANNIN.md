## Enriched Summary

A hydrolyzable tannin is a type of tannin that, upon heating with hydrochloric or sulfuric acids, yields CPD-183 or CPD-17769. At the center of a hydrolyzable tannin molecule there is a carbohydrate core (usually D-glucose, although sometimes it is a cyclitol like QUINATE or SHIKIMATE). The hydroxyl groups of the carbohydrate core are partially or totally esterified with phenolic groups such as CPD-183 in Gallotanins gallotannins or CPD-17769 in Ellagitannins ellagitannins.

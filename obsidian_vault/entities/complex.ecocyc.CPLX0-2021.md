## Annotation

The HU protein is a small DNA-binding protein that is considered a global regulatory protein and shares properties with histones, which play an important role in nucleoid organization and regulation . HU localizes preferentially to the loop regions of chromosomal hairpin compact structures, which are transcriptionally silent, AT-rich regions enriched in horizontally transferred genes (HTGs) and primarily assembled by H-NS and StpA . The HU protein has been shown to be involved in DNA replication and the formation of transcription foci , and it modulates the binding of IHF to oriC and stabilizes the DnaA oligomer bound to oriC . As another example, HU is required for the expression of σS factors . Recently, Oberto et al. showed that HU also plays an important role in the regulation of many genes in response to environmental changes and adaptation to stress, including changes in osmolarity, acid stress, SOS induction, and anaerobiosis . HU is a heterodimer formed by an α-subunit and a β-subunit, which are encoded by the hupA and hupB genes, respectively , and are differentially stimulated during cold shock . HU is a transcriptional dual regulator. HU-1 (HupB) and HU-2 (HupA) are closely related but differ by 28 residues, and they have a high content of hydrophobic residues represented mostly by alanine. There is no sequence homology between the proteins HU-1 and HU-2 compared to any of the five histones of different eukaryotes studied . HU forms high-affinity complexes with DNA containing sharp bends, kinks, branched and bulged structures, or single-strand breaks and loops . Three dimeric forms of HU exist in Escherichia coli: two homodimers, EcHUα2 and EcHUβ2, and a heterodimer, HUαβ .

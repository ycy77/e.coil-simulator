## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: Salicin; Salicoside CPD-1142 Salicin is a phenolic glycoside (PG) that makes an important contribution to plant defense against herbivores in TAX-3689 and TAX-40685 species . It is the most prevalent salicylate compound in certain northern willows . Studies related to this compound have been undertaken in a number of species including TAX-339964, TAX-77065, TAX-75715, and TAX-3691 . In general, a number of different phenolic glycosides, including CPD-13173 and CPD-1142 can accumulate to extremely high levels in leaves of TAX-3689 and TAX-40685 species, with exact levels of specific PGs varying based on genotype, environment, time of day, and developmental stage . Interestingly, there is evidence that the creation of large amounts of these compounds may impose a metabolic cost that can negatively impact plant growth , suggesting that plants must finely tune PG biosynthesis to maximize overall fitness. CPD-1142 Salicin may deter herbivore attack when it is hydrolyzed by β-glucosidases to CPD-173 in insect guts, possibly leading to the formation of gut lesions . The common drug CPD-524 has been generated by a chemical modification of CPD-1142.

## Enriched Summary

Glucuronoarabinoxylans Glucuronoarabinoxylans (GAX) and Arabinoglucuronoxylans arabinoglucuronoxylans (AGX) are components of Hemicelluloses that have a similar structure - a β(1→4)-D-xylopyranose backbone with single 4-O-methylglucuronate and α-L-arabinofuranose residues attached at position 2 and 3, respectively. However, Glucuronoarabinoxylans Glucuronoarabinoxylans consist of Arabinoxylan backbone that contains about ten times more α-L-arabinofuranose side chains than glucuronate ones, while Arabinoglucuronoxylans arabinoglucuronoxylans are Glucuronoxylans with a small proportion of the xylopyranose residues of the backbone disubstituted by two α-L-arabinofuranose residues. Another difference is that Glucuronoarabinoxylans glucuronoarabinoxylans contain some xylopyranose units that are doubly substituted by both 4-O-methylglucuronate and α-L-arabinofuranose . Some of the arabinofuranose side chains in AGX are further substitued by either a β-D-xylopyranose attached by a (1→2) linkage. This sugar moiety has been found usually esterified by ferulate at position 5 of the arabinofuranose unit and occur as a widespread component of grass cell walls . The AGX type polymers occur in appreciable quantity in coniferous species, although it is usually not the dominant hemicellulosic component (an exception is the hemicellulose of the tropic conifer TAX-1030197 )...

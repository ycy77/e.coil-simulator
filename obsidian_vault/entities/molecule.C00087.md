## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: Sulfur; S; Elemental sulfur EcoCyc common name: sulfur(0). Elemental sulfur appears in a number of different allotropic modifications: rhombic, monoclinic, polymeric, and others. The rhombic structure is the most common. Both the rhombic and monoclinic forms of sulfur are made up of eight sulfur atoms arranged in a puckered-ring structure. At one atmosphere pressure and temperatures less than 95.4°C, rhombic is the stable crystalline form. Above 95.4°C to the melt temperature of 118.9°C, the monoclinic crystalline structure is dominant . In addition, elemental sulfur always contains some polymeric sulfur consisting of chain-like macromolecules. The S-S bond energy is lower in polymeric sulfur than in ring sulfur .

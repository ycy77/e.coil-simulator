## Enriched Summary

Ceramides are found in high concentrations within the cell membrane and are one of the component lipids that make up Sphingomyelins sphingomyelin, one of the major lipids in the lipid bilayer. This type of ceramide, containing an additional double bond at position 8 of the sphingoid base and a methyl group at position 9, is found in some fungi .

## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Component of the AZY copper uptake system (PubMed:40108346). YebY negatively regulates activity of the copper importer YebZ (PubMed:40108346). At high copper concentrations, YebY may function as a safety valve, preventing toxic copper overload by inhibiting the import activity of YebZ and YobA (PubMed:40108346). The reduced form of YebY binds Cu(+) and Cu(2+) with low affinity (PubMed:34822841). It shows low specificity, and can also bind Co(2+), Ni(2+) and Ag(+) (PubMed:34822841). The low affinity of copper binding along with the stabilization imparted by the disulfide suggests that this metal binding is likely not physiologically relevant (PubMed:34822841). The AZY proteins link copper import to multiple antibiotic resistance (PubMed:40108346). They are necessary for the copper-dependent activation of the mar operon, thus leading to multidrug antibiotic resistance via the mar pathway (PubMed:40108346). The AZY proteins may also be involved in copper delivery to membrane proteins, but they are not involved in copper tolerance or antioxidant defense (PubMed:34822841). YebY is required for the activity of the copper-related NADH dehydrogenase II (NDH-2) (PubMed:34822841). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:34822841, ECO:0000269|PubMed:40108346}...

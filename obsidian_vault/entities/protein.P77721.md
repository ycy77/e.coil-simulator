## Enriched Summary

Uncharacterized HTH-type transcriptional regulator YdjF YdjF appears to belong to the DeoR family of transcription factors, and it was predicted to regulate genes related to metabolism . Upstream of the ydjF gene, a sequence was found that could work as a transcriptional promoter, and the transcription of the ydjF gene is affected by cadmium, stress, and glucose-lactose shift . The YdjF binding landscape was globally mapped in vivo by standardized ChIP-seq and quantitatively modeled using BoltzNet, a biophysically informed neural network that infers binding energy from sequence data . The resulting ChIP-seq binding regions are available in RegulonDB (Galagan collection), and the corresponding energy matrix and genomic binding profiles can be accessed at boltznet.bu.edu .

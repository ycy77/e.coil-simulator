## Enriched Summary

Bilins (also known as bilanes or bile pigments) are pigments formed in many organisms as a metabolic product of certain porphyrins. They consist of linear arrangements of four pyrrole rings (tetrapyrroles). Bilin (also called bilichrome) received its name since it was first identified as a bile pigment of mammals. However, billins are found in many organisms, including lower vertebrates, invertebrates, green plants, some algae and cyanobacteria. Bilins can range in color from red, orange, yellow or brown to blue or green.

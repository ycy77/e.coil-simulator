They are laid out in the cell wall at a high density and can constitute up to 50% of the algae dry mass. The basic units of Carrageenans carrageenans are repeats of alternating α-1,3-linked D-galactose and β-1,4-linked D-galactose units, which are often substituted by sulfate. Carrageenans are classified according to the number and the position of sulfated ester . In Carrageenoses carrageenoses the α-1,3-linked galactose units are modified by having a 3,6-anhydro bridge. These bridges, which form a double helix during aggregation, are responsible for the gelation that is typical to these compounds.

## Enriched Summary

RraB binds to the C-terminal half (CTH) of RNase E and interferes with the cleavage of a subset of RNase E target transcripts in vitro and in vivo. RraB interacts with RNase E at a distinct epitope within the CTH, while high-affinity binding of RraA, a second modulator of RNase E function, requires the CTH, but can not be mapped to a single epitope. Binding of RraA or RraB differently affect the composition of the degradosome . The regulatory interaction between RraB and RNase E and their orthologs appears to be evolutionarily conserved .

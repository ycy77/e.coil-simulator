## Enriched Summary

The Ubi complex is a soluble metabolon (transient protein complexes (or "microreactors") of sequential enzymes that channel substrates from one protein to the next without releasing the substrates to the cytosol) that mediates the enzymatic conversion of 2-octaprenylphenol (OPP) to ubiquinone-8. It contains the five enzymes necessary for catalysis, UbiE, UbiF, UbiG, UbiH, and UbiI, as well as two accessory factors, UbiJ and UbiK. The hydrophobic pathway intermediates OPP and C6-demethoxy-ubiquinone-8 co-elute with the Ubi complex . Computational modelling using Alpha-Fold2 indicates a single copy of each Ubi metabolon subunit (UbiG,E,I,H,F,J,K) makes up the core assembly; ligand binding was also analyzed . It is interesting to note that aside from UbiG, the catalytic activities of the enzymes UbiE, UbiF, UbiH and UbiI are only deduced from the mutant phenotypes of their respective genes, suggesting that their activities require the environment provided by the Ubi complex.

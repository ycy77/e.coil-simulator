## Enriched Summary

GnsA and its homolog G6823-MONOMER affect unsaturated fatty acid abundance and membrane fluidity . GnsA was isolated as a multicopy suppressor of a cold-sensitive secG mutant . It is also able to suppress fabA6, a temperature-sensitive unsaturated fatty acid auxotroph ; the suppressor was originally thought to localize to the ymcE gene, which is located immediately upstream of gnsA . GnsA is also predicted to be the toxin of a proposed GnsA-YmcE toxin-antitoxin system . A crystal structure of GnsA has been solved at 1.8 Å resolution . Overexpression of GnsA causes an increase in the unsaturated fatty acid content . At 20°C, overexpression of GnsA inhibits phophatidylethanolamine synthesis and thus causes a proportional increase of acidic phospholipids; cells become filamentous . A null mutation in gnsA has no effect on growth or phospholipid composition . GnsA and GnsB are able to rescue the growth defect and to restore the relative proportion of unsaturated to saturated fatty acids in various (p)ppGpp-deficient strains (e.g. deletion mutants of EG10281, EG10835 and EG10966) at low temperatures . Wild-type expression of GnsA is below the limit of detection .

## Enriched Summary

Ceramides Ceramides are found in high concentrations within the cell membrane and are one of the component lipids that make up Sphingomyelins sphingomyelin, one of the major lipids in the lipid bilayer. This type of ceramide with a sphingoid base with three double bonds has been found in the marine diatom TAX-35128 , as well as in the spermatozoa of the starfish TAX-7602 . A related compound that contains a methyl branch was reported from a squid neuron sphingomylein .

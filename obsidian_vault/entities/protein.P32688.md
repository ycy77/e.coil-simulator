## Enriched Summary

Uncharacterized protein YjbG The yjbEFGH operon is involved in the production of an extracellular polysaccharide . Overexpression of yjbEFGH alters colony morphology and leads to increased binding of toluidine blue-O and Congo red, indicating increased production of an EPS that is distinct from colanic acid . Expression of yjbG is positively regulated by RcsC . Disruption of yjbE has a polar effect on expression of yjbFGH, indicating that these genes form an operon; however, a full-length mRNA could not be detected, possibly due to strong secondary structure in the yjbE-yjbF intergenic region . Expression of yjbEFGH is highest at slow growth rates, low cell density and high oxygen availability; expression is higher in an rpoS mutant and induced by 0.7 M NaCl . A ΔyjbG rpoS::Tn10 mutant does not show extreme mucoidy on LB plates, but does produce increased amounts of extracellular polysaccharides as measured by a pelleting assay .

## Enriched Summary

Glyphosate is a broad-spectrum systemic herbicide used to kill weeds. It was initially patented and sold by Monsanto Company in the 1970s under the tradename Roundup, and has become the most used herbicide in the world . Glyphosate's mode of action is to inhibit the enzyme EC-2.5.1.19, which catalyzes the transfer of the enolpyruvyl moiety of PHOSPHO-ENOL-PYRUVATE to SHIKIMATE-5P to form 3-ENOLPYRUVYL-SHIKIMATE-5P. This is a key reaction in the ARO-PWY "shikimate pathway", leading to synthesis of aromatic amino acids via CHORISMATE in plants, fungi, bacteria, and apicomplexan parasites .

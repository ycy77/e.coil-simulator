## Enriched Summary

dcuD (gene.b3227) is a gene entity. It encodes dcuD (protein.P45428). Encoded protein function: FUNCTION: May have a role in the transport of dicarboxylates from the periplasm across the inner membrane. Does not seem to be essential for growth. It is likely to be a cryptic gene, as no or low expression has been found under a number of growth conditions and when tested with a large number of potential substrates. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10525738}. EcoCyc product frame: YHCL-MONOMER. EcoCyc synonyms: yhcL. Genomic coordinates: 3374869-3376236. EcoCyc protein note: In the Transporter Classification Database, DcuD is a member of the C4-dicarboxylate Uptake C (DcuC) family . DcuD has high sequence similarity to the anaerobic C4-dicarboxylate transporter, DcuC . DcuD is not essential for C4-dicarboxylate transport during aerobic or anaerobic growth and a dcuD null mutant strain shows no significant growth impairment when tested with a large number of substrates; expression of dcuD is very low or absent under most conditions (see also ). DcuD may be active during glycerol fermentation ; DcuD is implicated in the generation of PMF during glucose fermentation at pH7.5

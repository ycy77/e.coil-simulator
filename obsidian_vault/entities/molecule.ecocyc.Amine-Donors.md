## Enriched Summary

Amine-Donors "An amino group donor" is a compound that can donate an amino group to another compound in a reaction catalyzed by enzymes that belong to the EC-2.6.1 transaminase group" (often called aminotransferases). Prior to donating the amino group the compound is Aminated-Amine-Donors and after donating it the compound is Deaminated-Amine-Donors. Since tranmination reactions are reversible, the distinction between an amino group donor and an amino group acceptor is not determined by the actual chemical entity, but by the role it plays in a particular reaction. However, some compounds, such as GLT and L-ALPHA-ALANINE, are often used as amino group donors by the aminotransferases in reactions that are physiologically unidirectional. This particular entry is used when the identity of the amino group donor (or acceptor) is not known or cannot be defined.

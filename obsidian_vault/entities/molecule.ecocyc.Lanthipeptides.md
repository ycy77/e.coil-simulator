## Enriched Summary

Lanthipeptides are a family of RiPPs "ribosomally synthesized and post-translationally modified peptides" (RiPPs) that is characterized by the presence of multiple CPD-3736 (Lan) and CPD-25467 (MeLan) residues. These residues are formed during the cyclization of these compounds, which is catalyzed by lanthipeptide synthases. These enzymes catalyze dehydration of Ser/Thr residues, followed by intramolecular addition of the thiol group of a Cys residue to the dehydro amino acids, which results in the formation of the thioether crosslinks of (methyl)lanthionine . Members of the lanthipeptides that have antimicrobial activity are known as lantibiotics (note that lanthipeptides used to be called lantipeptides, but their name was revised to reflect the presence of lanthionine groups ). ## Annotation

Lanthipeptides are a family of RiPPs "ribosomally synthesized and post-translationally modified peptides" (RiPPs) that is characterized by the presence of multiple CPD-3736 (Lan) and CPD-25467 (MeLan) residues. These residues are formed during the cyclization of these compounds, which is catalyzed by lanthipeptide synthases. These enzymes catalyze dehydration of Ser/Thr residues, followed by intramolecular addition of the thiol group of a Cys residue to the dehydro amino acids, which results in the formation of the thioether crosslinks of (methyl)lanthionine . Members of the lanthipeptides that have antimicrobial activity are known as lantibiotics (note that lanthipeptides used to be called lantipeptides, but their name was revised to reflect the presence of lanthionine groups ). Lantibiotic peptides have high affinity for their various targets, which is typically a small molecule . For example, CPD-12301 binds with high affinity to the bacterial cell wall precursor NAcMur-Peptide-NAcGlc-Undecaprenols "lipid II" , and CPD-27028 specifically recognizes L-1-PHOSPHATIDYL-ETHANOLAMINE "phosphatidyl ethanolamine" . Five classes of lanthipeptides have been defined, based on the type of lanthipeptide synthase involved. The biosynthesis of class I lantipeptides involves separate Ser/Thr dehydratases (LanB) and cyclases (LanC), while formation of (methyl)lanthionine in class II compounds is carried out by a single bifunctional enzyme carrying both a dehydratase and a cyclase domain (LanM) . Both class III and class IV involve single three-domain enzymes consisting of an N-terminal lyase, a central kinase and a C-terminal cyclase domains.

## Enriched Summary

Lipid A disaccharide synthase (LpxB) catalyzes the condensation of BISOHMYR-GLUCOSAMINYL-1P and UDP-2,3-bis(3-hydroxymyristoyl)glucosamine and release of UDP to form lipid A disaccharide, an intermediate in the NAGLIPASYN-PWY pathway . The β,1'-6 linked disaccharide generated by LpxB is characteristic of all lipid A molecules . LpxB belongs to the glycosyltransferase family 19. The enzyme is membrane-associated and binds phospholipids, and its activity is dependent on the surface concentration of its substrates . LpxB elutes as an octamer from a size exclusion column in the absence of detergent . In the presence of detergent, LpxB elutes as a dimer . GlpD (AERGLYC3PDEHYDROG-CPLX) specifically copurifies with affinity-tagged LpxB . Crystal structures of an LpxB mutant that improved its solubility reveal an extensively intertwined domain-swapped dimer. The structures were obtained using LpxB protein from E. coli BL21, which is 99% identical to that of E. coli K-12 . A temperature-sensitive lpxB mutant shows a 100-300 fold increase in lipid X, a 50-100 fold increase in UDP-2,3-bis(3-hydroxymyristoyl)glucosamine, and an absence of phosphatidylglycerol . The structures were obtained using LpxB protein from E. coli BL21, which is 99% identical to that of E. coli K-12 . The highly conserved residues D98 and R201 are essential for LpxB activity . The mutations introduced to improve LpxB solubility for crystallization severely reduce catalytic activity, suggesting that the hydrophobic nature of this surface region is required for extraction of lipid substrates from detergent micelles . Overexpression of LpxB results in the accumulation of aberrant tubular membranes at the cytoplasmid surface of the inner membrane . lpxB is an essential gene .

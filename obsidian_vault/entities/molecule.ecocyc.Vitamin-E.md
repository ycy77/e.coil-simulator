## Enriched Summary

The term Vitamin-E (tocochromenols) is a collective term for the eight forms of Tocotrienols tocotrienols and Tocopherols tocopherols, each existing as α, β, γ, and δ species. Structurally tocotrienols and tocopherols differ only in the degree of unsaturation of their side-chain. The tocotrienol chain comprises three trans-double bonds, whereas the tocopherol chain is fully saturated. In addition, the α, β, γ, and δ species of both compound groups vary in the methylation pattern of the chromanol ring . Vitamin E compounds are synthesized only by photosynthetic organisms, although they are essential dietary components for animals (including humans), where they act primarily as antioxidants. Their presumed role in plants involve protection of chloroplast lipids and chlorophylls against oxidative damages. The tocotrienols are mostly found in monocots and a few dicots , whereas tocopherols are wider spread in the plant kingdom and dominate in the dicots .

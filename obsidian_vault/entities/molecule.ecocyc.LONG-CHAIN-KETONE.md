## Enriched Summary

A ketone is a compound with the structure RC(=O)R', where R and R' can be a variety of atoms and groups of atoms. It features a carbonyl group (C=O) bonded to two other carbon atoms. Ketones differ from Aldehydes aldehydes in that the carbonyl is placed between two carbons rather than at the end of a carbon skeleton.

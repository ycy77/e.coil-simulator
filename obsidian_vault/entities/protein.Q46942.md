## Enriched Summary

Uncharacterized protein YqeI YqeI appears to pertain to the CadC family of transcription factors, and it was predicted to regulate genes involved in processes related to phages and LPS synthesis | CITS: [35880217]|. Upstream of the yqeI gene, a sequence was found that could work as a transcriptional promoter, and the transcription of the yqeI gene is affected by conditions that lead to biofilm formation and UV exposure . yqeI is located within a remnant of an ETT2 (type III secretion system) pathogenicity island that is fully present in pathogenic strains such as E. coli O157:H7 and at least partially present in most sequenced E. coli and Shigella strains . ETT2 has been shown to contribute to pathogenesis . YqeI is predicted to be a bitopic inner membrane protein . YqeI affects the expression of the genes involved in bacterial localization, locomotion, and biological adhesion in avian pathogenic Escherichia coli (APEC) . It decreases flagella formation, motility ability, serum resistance, cell adhesion ability, and bacterial colonization ability, supporting that the transcriptional regulator YqeI plays an important role in the pathogenicity of APEC . YqeI was transcriptionally upregulated in iron limitation over iron excess at 63% dissolved oxygen as determined by RNA-seq .

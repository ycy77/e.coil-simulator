## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Required for the use of extracellular DNA as a nutrient (PubMed:16707682). Has been suggested to be involved in gluconate metabolism (PubMed:9871335). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16707682, ECO:0000305|PubMed:9871335}. The yhgH gene product is required for utilization of DNA as the sole source of carbon and energy; yhgH is homologous to genes involved in natural competence and genetic transformation in other bacteria . Based on complementation experiments, YhgH/GntX is also thought to be involved in high-affinity gluconate transport . A yhgH null mutant has a normal growth phenotype in complex media, but has a stationary phase competition-defective (SPCD) phenotype in co-culture with the wild-type strain. A yhgI null mutant can not utilize DNA as the sole source of carbon and energy .

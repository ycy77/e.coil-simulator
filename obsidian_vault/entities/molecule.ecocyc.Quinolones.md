## Annotation

A quinolone is a compound that contains the Quinolines quinoline structure modifed by an oxo group on the pyridine ring. The first quinolone compound to be discovered was CPD-25979, identified in 1962 as an impurity in the chemical manufacturing of the antimalarial agent CPD-23333. That compound lead to the development of the antibiotic CPD-21025 "nalidixic acid" . Since then more than 10,000 quinolone derivatives have been patented or published. Early on the quinolones were found to have antibiotic activity. They directly inhibit DNA synthesis by binding to the polymerase-DNA complex. Quinolones also stabilize DNA strand breaks created by DNA gyrase and topoisomerase IV, and ternary complexes of drug, enzyme, and DNA block progress of the replication fork. Fluoroquinolones are WHO-approved second-line drugs to treat tuberculosis. Quinolones were later found to exhibit a variety of biological activities, including antimalarial, antiviral and antitumor activities . Many compounds demonstrate anticancer activity through DNA intercalation. Most of these drugs are currently used in the treatment of human malignancies targeting topoisomerase (types II) enzymes . The quinolones stabilize covalent enzyme—cleaved DNA complexes that are intermediates in the catalytic cycle of the enzyme . Quinolones also have antiviral activities. They were shown to protect cells from HIV-mediated cytotoxicity . Some of the compounds target the viral protein integrase .

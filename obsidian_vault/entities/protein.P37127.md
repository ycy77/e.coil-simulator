## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Involved in formate-dependent uric acid degradation under microaerobic and anaerobic conditions. May reduce the enzymes necessary for uric acid degradation. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:30885932}. Under anaerobic and microaerobic conditions, E. coli is able to utilize urate as its sole source of nitrogen. AegA or YgfT play a role in the formate-dependent catabolism of urate . AegA contains an N-terminal Fe-S cluster domain and C-terminal similarity to the GltD subunit of glutamate synthase . An aegA mutant does not have obvious phenotypes with respect to respiration or fermentation and shows no defect in metabolizing ammonium or various amino acids (L-alanine, L-arginine, L-glutamate, glycine, or DL-serine) as a source of nitrogen, or in use of various sources of carbon . An aegA ygfT double mutant lacks urate degradation activity . Expression of aegA is regulated by Fnr, NarXL, and NarQ (but not ArcA) , and is induced under anaerobic and microaerobic conditions . Results differ on whether expression of aegA under anaerobic conditions is repressed in the presence of nitrate , or whether there is no effect of nitrate addition; differences may be due to differences in the presence or absence of regulatory elements . AegA: "anaerobically expressed gene A"

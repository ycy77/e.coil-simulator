## Enriched Summary

Lipid A is a lipid component of the Lipopolysaccharides "lipopolysaccharides" (LPS) of Gram-negative bacteria. Its hydrophobic nature allows it to anchor the LPS to the outer membrane. Lipid A consists of two glucosamine units attached by a β(1→6) linkage and decorated with four to seven acyl chains and one or two phosphate groups. Up to four acyl chains, often with a β hydroxy group and usually 10 to 18 carbons in length, are attached directly to the glucosamine sugars at positions N-2 and O-3 (and N'-2 and O'-3, respectively), while additional acyl chains are attached to the β hydroxy groups of some of those primary acyl chains. Phosphate groups can be attached at position O-1, O'-4, or both. Many variations are found in the structures of lipid A molecules. Further modifications are commonly observed, such as the addition or removal of phosphate groups (see EC-2.7.4.29, EC-3.1.3.M9, and EC-3.1.3.M11), the addition of PHOSPHORYL-ETHANOLAMINE (see EC-2.7.8.43, EC-2.7.8.42, and EC-2.7.8.M9), the addition of 4-AMINO-4-DEOXY-L-ARABINOSE units (see EC-2.4.2.43), and further addition or removal of fatty acyl units (see EC-2.3.1.251 and EC-3.1.1.77). ## Annotation

Lipid A is a lipid component of the Lipopolysaccharides "lipopolysaccharides" (LPS) of Gram-negative bacteria. Its hydrophobic nature allows it to anchor the LPS to the outer membrane. Lipid A consists of two glucosamine units attached by a β(1→6) linkage and decorated with four to seven acyl chains and one or two phosphate groups. Up to four acyl chains, often with a β hydroxy group and usually 10 to 18 carbons in length, are attached directly to the glucosamine sugars at positions N-2 and O-3 (and N'-2 and O'-3, respectively), while additional acyl chains are attached to the β hydroxy groups of some of those primary acyl chains. Phosphate groups can be attached at position O-1, O'-4, or both. Many variations are found in the structures of lipid A molecules. Further modifications are commonly observed, such as the addition or removal of phosphate groups (see EC-2.7.4.29, EC-3.1.3.M9, and EC-3.1.3.M11), the addition of PHOSPHORYL-ETHANOLAMINE (see EC-2.7.8.43, EC-2.7.8.42, and EC-2.7.8.M9), the addition of 4-AMINO-4-DEOXY-L-ARABINOSE units (see EC-2.4.2.43), and further addition or removal of fatty acyl units (see EC-2.3.1.251 and EC-3.1.1.77). Many bacterial species produce lipid A molecules that contain CPD-24440 (see PWY-8273). Since the fully acylated lipid A is assembled only when attached to the core oligosaccharide, a "stand alone" lipid A, as depicted here, does not occur naturally.

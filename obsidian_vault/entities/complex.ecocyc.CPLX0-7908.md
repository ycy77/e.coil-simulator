## Annotation

The assembly of iron-sulfur clusters requires complex biosynthetic machinery. E. coli encodes two sets of proteins, the Isc and the Suf system, to achieve this task. IscA is the type II A-type carrier (ATC-II) of the Isc system . IscA acts as an iron chaperone . In the presence of the thioreductase system, IscA has been shown to bind iron with an association constant of 2x1019 M-1 . In vitro in the presence of IscS and L-cysteine, IscA can provide iron to the scaffold protein IscU , and IscA mutants unable to bind iron are unable to perform this function . IscA was shown to bind iron in vivo . In the presence of hydrogen peroxide, IscA loses its iron binding activity due to oxidation of its thiol groups . IscA can utilize iron bound by FtnA . Crystal structures of IscA are presented at 2.3 Å resolution. IscA forms a dimer of dimers . IscA and HscA physically interact . The [2Fe-2S] cluster-bound form of IscA interacts with ferredoxin, resulting in transfer of the [2Fe-2S] cluster to form holoferredoxin . Under physiologically relevant conditions, IscA acts as an iron chaperone rather than an iron-sulfur cluster assembly scaffold protein . Mutagenesis of the conserved C99 or C101 residues abolishes the iron binding and transfer activities of IscA . Overexpression of a gene cluster containing the iscA region results in increased formation of iron-sulfur clusters in various overexpressed ferredoxins . Conversely, an iscA mutation limits iron-sulfur cluster assembly in overexpressed ferredoxin . An iscA insertion mutant does not have a significant growth defect , but an iscA sufA double mutant has a severe growth defect under aerobic conditions in minimal or rich medium due to lack of [4Fe-4S] clusters in metabolically essential enzymes such as IlvD, ThiC , and IspG/H .

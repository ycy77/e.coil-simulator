## Enriched Summary

The "Propionate Regulator," PrpR, is a transcription factor that controls expression of genes involved in catabolism of propionate . It is negatively autoregulated, sensitive to catabolite control, and coordinately activates transcription of a divergent operon related to propionate catabolism . Synthesis of this operon is induced when E. coli is grown in the absence of glucose or glycerol. PrpR belongs to the NtrC family of activators, which interact directly with the RpoN RNA polymerase . PrpR is a σ54-dependent activator and belongs to the AAA+ superfamily of mechano-chemical ATPases. It consists of three domains, an amino-terminal domain involved in effector recognition, a catalytic domain, and a carboxy-terminal DNA-binding domain . This regulator binds to a palindromic sequence (5'-GTTTCAT-10n-ATGAAAC-3') that overlaps prpRp . PrpR was transcriptionally upregulated in iron excess over iron limitation at 63% dissolved oxygen as determined by RNA-seq .

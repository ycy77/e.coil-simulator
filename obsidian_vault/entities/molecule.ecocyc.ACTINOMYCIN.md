## Enriched Summary

The ACTINOMYCIN actinomycins are a family of bicyclic chromopeptide lactones with strong antineoplastic activity that are produced by various streptomycete strains . The first actinomycin was isolated from TAX-1890 in 1940, and was the first antibiotic obtained in pure form from an actinomycete . ACTINOMYCIN-D "Actinomycin D" was the first antibiotic shown to have anti-cancer activity , and was approved as an anticancer drug in 1964, making it one of the oldest anticancer drugs. Actinomycin D inhibits transcription by binding DNA at the transcription initiation complex and preventing elongation of RNA chain by RNA polymerase . A common feature of all actinomycins is the chromophoric moiety CPD-18447, which is a unique phenoxazinone dicarboxylic acid to which two pentapeptide lactone rings are attached with amide linkages .

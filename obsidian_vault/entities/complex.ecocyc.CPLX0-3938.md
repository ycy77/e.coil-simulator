## Enriched Summary

CutA is a copper-binding protein that is also required for copper tolerance. Mutants lacking CutA function show increased copper sensitivity and uptake. CutA is also involved in tolerance of zinc, nickel, cobalt and cadmium. It is induced by the presence of copper, zinc, nickel and cobalt, and to a lesser extent cadmium, manganese and silver . The crystal structure of CutA has been determined to 1.7 Å resolution. It forms a trimer . CutA can bind copper in vitro . The crystal structure of the E. coli CutA trimer is similar to those of CutA trimers from the hyperthermophile Pyrococcus horikoshii and the extreme thermophile Thermus thermophilus, although the monomer from P. horikoshii is more stabilized by ion pairs, hydrogen bonds, and hydrophobic interactions . Rational protein design was used to enhance the heat stability of E. coli CutA and additional crystal structures were solved for wild-type, single (E61V), double (S11V/E61V) and triple (C16A/C39A/C79A) mutants. The stability of the already stable native protein could be further improved by making certain amino acid substitutions . In addition to X-ray crystallography, the solution structure of CutA was determined by NMR, its internal dynamics studied, and its trimeric structure in solution was confirmed. The dissociation constant of the highly stable trimer was estimated to be less than 1 μM...

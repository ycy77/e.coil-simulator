## Annotation

Dps is a highly abundant protein in stationary-phase E. coli and is required for the normal starvation response and long-term stationary phase viability . The protein was shown to be involved in protection from multiple stresses, including oxidative stress caused by treatment with hypochlorous acid or hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) , the stress of exposure to visible light in a seawater environment , fatty acid starvation , N-ethylmaleimide (NEM) treatment , heat shock , and acid stress . Dps binding to DNA also contributes to acid tolerance in the pathogenic E. coli O157:H7 . Dps was reported to act as a molecular chaperone, protecting aggregation-prone proteins during heat shock . Dps binds tightly to DNA in a non-sequence-specific manner , forming a DNA-protein crystal . In the presence of significant stress signals Dps can consolidate the nucleoid, creating a physical barrier for DNA protection . Dps is able to bind to DNA, folding it in the presence of low concentrations of KCl and MgCl2 as well as in low pH, and is not affected by temperature changes into the physiological range . Dps can co-crystallize with DNA of different lengths (from ~3000 to ~10,000 base pairs) and nucleotide sequences, with one Dps molecule contacting a ~6 nm long DNA segment . Dps-DNA complexes form one-dimensional crystals or filament-like assemblies, which transform into weakly ordered complexes with triclinic unit cells. Under certain pH and salt conditions the complexes form cylindrical structures . Dps is the major nucleoid protein at stationary phase , showing evenly distributed localization within the nucleoid .

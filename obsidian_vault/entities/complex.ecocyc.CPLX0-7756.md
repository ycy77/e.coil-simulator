## Annotation

NsrR, the "nitrite-sensitive repressor" , regulates genes involved in cell protection against nitric oxide (NO) . A microarray analysis showed that this protein regulates directly or indirectly at least 30 genes, some of which are involved in protection against reactive nitrogen species that can be generated by NO . A ChIP-chip analysis (chromatin immunoprecipitation and microarray analysis) revealed that NsrR regulates, in addition, genes involved in motility and/or biofilm development . NsrR also appears to regulate σE-dependent genes in an RseA-independent manner ; NsrR also regulates genes involved in tellurate reduction . Inhibition of nsrR expression by CRISPRi reduced cellular fitness under treatment with the antibiotics verapamil or erythromycin . NsrR belongs to the Rrf2 family , which includes among others the [2Fe-2S]-containing transcription factor IscR and the iron regulator RirA . These proteins have orthologs in a wide range of gram-negative and gram-positive bacteria . For DNA binding, NsrR has a helix-turn-helix motif close to the N terminus and three cysteine residues to ligate a [2Fe-2S] cluster close to the C terminus . Some proteins, such as SufA, SufB, IscU, EprA, and IscA, are involved in the assembly and transfer of the [2Fe-2S] cluster to NsrR .

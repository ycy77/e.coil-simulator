## Enriched Summary

yhhS (gene.b3473) is a gene entity. It encodes yhhS (protein.P37621). Encoded protein function: FUNCTION: Confers high-level resistance to glyphosate when overexpressed (PubMed:22089966). Overexpression has no effect on intracellular arabinose concentrations (PubMed:22952739). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22089966, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22952739}. EcoCyc product frame: YHHS-MONOMER. Genomic coordinates: 3610516-3611733. EcoCyc protein note: The YhhS protein is an uncharacterised member of the major facilitator superfamily (MFS) of transporters . Overexpression of both a full-length yhhS and a truncated yhhS (containing 7 of the putative 12 membrane spanning domains) confers resistance to the herbicide, glyphosate and permits growth of cells in the presence of 15mM glyphosate . Overexpression of yhhS reduces the intracellular accumulation of labeled glyphosate . YhhS has been implicated in arabinose efflux . Accumulation of the cationic fluorescent dye 3,3'-dipropylthiadicarbocyanine iodide (diS-C3) is greater in yhhS knockout cells compared to wild type . This partially characterized enzyme was found to interact with the PGK enzyme in glycolysis in a large assessment of protein-protein interactions (PPIs) .

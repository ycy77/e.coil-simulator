KEGG compound: L-Adrenaline; (R)-(-)-Adrenaline; (R)-(-)-Epinephrine; (R)-(-)-Epirenamine; (R)-(-)-Adnephrine; 4-[(1R)-1-Hydroxy-2-(methylamino)ethyl]-1,2-benzenediol EcoCyc common name: (R)-adrenaline. L-EPINEPHRINE is a hormone and a neurotransmitter produced in some neurons of the central nervous system and in the chromaffin cells of the adrenal medulla that acts on drenergic receptors.It is called epinephrine in the US and adrenaline everywhere else. L-EPINEPHRINE has many functions, depending on the nature of the receptor it binds to in the different cells. Among other roles it regulates heart rate, blood vessel and air passage diameters, and metabolic shifts.

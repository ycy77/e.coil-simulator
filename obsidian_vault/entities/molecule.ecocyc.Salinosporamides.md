## Enriched Summary

The marine bacterium TAX-168695 produces a family of potent 20S-proteasome inhibitors. Originally three members of the family were isolated - CPD-12674, CPD-12685, and CPD-27917 . Later additional compounds - CPD-12686 through CPD-27921 and CPD-27922 . These related compounds possess a unique bicyclic structural element, composed of a tightly fused Gamma-Lactams "γ-lactam" and Beta-lactones "β-lactone" rings. The salinosporamides bind irreversibly to the proteosome with great affinity, and CPD-12674 is used as an anticancer agent. It entered phase I human clinical trials for the treatment of multiple myeloma only three years after its discovery .

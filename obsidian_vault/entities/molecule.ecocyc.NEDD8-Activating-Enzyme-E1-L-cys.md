## Enriched Summary

NEDD8-activating enzymes, also known as E1 enzymes, catalyze the first step in the neddylation process. The E1 enzyme, which is a herodimeric complex, binds ATP-Mg2+ and NEDD8 and catalyzes C-terminal acyl adenylation of NEDD8. A catalytic L-cysteine residue of the E1 enzyme then attacks the NEDD8-AMP complex through acyl substitution, creating a thioester bond and an AMP leaving group. This active form of NEDD8, bound to the E1 enzyme, is transferred to NEDD8-carrier-proteins-E2 through a transthioesterification reaction, in which an E2 catalytic L-cysteine attacks the backside of the E1-NEDD8 complex.

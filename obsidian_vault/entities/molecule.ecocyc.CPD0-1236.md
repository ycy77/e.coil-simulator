## Enriched Summary

N3-(4-methoxyfumaroyl)-L-2,3-diaminopropanoate is a synthetic molecule that is able to inactivate the bacterial EC-2.6.1.16. The molecule is likely attacked by the thiolate side chain of the active site Cys in the glutaminase domain of the enzyme. The alkylation of this residue blocks generation of the ammonia, which is required to convert FRUCTOSE-6P into D-GLUCOSAMINE-6-P .

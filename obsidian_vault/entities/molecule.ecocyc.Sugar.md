## Enriched Summary

The term sugar is used as a generic name for sweet-tasting, soluble carbohydrates. The term is not a specific chemical definition, but is frequently applied to Monosaccharides monosaccharides and Disaccharides disaccharides. Strictly speaking, sugars, like other carbohydrates, contain only carbon, hydrogen and oxygen atoms. When one or more of those atoms is substituted by an amino group, a sulfhydryl group, or a similar heteroatomic groups, the resulting compound is considered Modified-Sugars.

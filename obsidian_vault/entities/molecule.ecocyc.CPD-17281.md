## Enriched Summary

CPD-8121 Icosatetraenoate (also spelled eicosatetraenoate, or ETA) is a polyunsaturated fatty acid that contains four cis double bonds at positions 8, 11, 14 and 17. The last double bond is located at the third carbon from the end of the molecule, making it an ω-3 fatty acid. CPD-8121 Icosatetraenoate is an intermediate between CPD-12653 and 5Z8Z11Z14Z17Z-EICOSAPENTAENOATE. It appears to act as as a dual inhibitor of ARACHIDONIC_ACID oxygenation by both the cyclooxygenase (COX) and lipoxygenase pathways, and may be responsible for the anti-inflammatory effect of TAX-38949 (green-lipped mussels), in which it is found in high concentrations . Like other fatty acids, CPD-8121 is rarely found in its free form. It is usually found as either CPD-14394 or CPD-17281 "incorporated into a lipid".

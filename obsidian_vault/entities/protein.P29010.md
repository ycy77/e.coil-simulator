## Enriched Summary

Uncharacterized protein YdfD YdfD is a lysis protein encoded by the Qin prophage. The protein contains a predicted N-terminal hydrophobic domain, but lacks a recognizable signal peptide . YdfD has been shown to interact directly both in vitro and in vivo with EG10370 and may be a specific inhibitor of IspG; through this interaction YdfD compromises cell wall biosynthesis leading to cell lysis . Inducing expression of YdfD leads to lysis of 99.9% of cells within 2 hours. The predicted cytoplasmic domain, residues 23-63, retains this activity. Both the co-expression of dicB or of SulA, another protein that inhibits cell division, abolishes this phenotype, suggesting that the lytic effect of YdfD is dependent on cell division . A ydfD deletion exhibits aggravating genetic interactions with genes involved in mismatch repair . Deletions of specific N- and C-terminal residues reveals that the N-terminal domain of YdfD is responsible for binding to the N-terminal domain of IspG and may also recruit its C-terminal domain to IspG . Gene expression levels are influenced by several environmental stressors, increasing in response to higher pH, increased oxidative stress (H2O2) and high salinity (% NaCl) .

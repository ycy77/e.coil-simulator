## Enriched Summary

The FtsL, FtsB and FtsQ cell division proteins form a complex independently of its location at the cell division site . This complex is thought to function as a molecular scaffold that enables proper formation of the divisome. A structural model of the periplasmic component of the FtsLBQ complex has been generated, indicating a trimeric or hexameric coiled-coil structure . A current hypothesis is that a dimer of FtsB recruits FtsL into a higher-order complex with 1:1 stoichiometry . A crystal structure of the heterotrimeric complex has been obtained; it has an inverted V-shaped confirmation. One branch consists of the rod-shaped FtsQ with its α1 domain attaching to the transmembrane helix and its two globular domains in the periplasm; the other branch consists of the coiled-coil of FtsB-FtsL dimer and extends from the inner membrane to the periplasm where its Β domain intefaces with FtsQ . A model of the complex provides more insight into the interface of the 3 subunits as well as dynamics of each .

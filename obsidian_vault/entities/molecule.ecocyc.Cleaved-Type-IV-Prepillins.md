## Enriched Summary

Precursors of the type IV pilins of a number of bacterial pathogens, as well as related proteins involved in extracellular protein export and DNA uptake, are synthesized with short basic leader sequences. During maturation of these proteins the leader sequence is proteolytically removed by specialized endopeptidases, of which the prototype is encoded by the pilD gene of TAX-287, which cleaves preferably at a Gly|Phe site, leaving a PHE at the N-terminus of the cleaved type IV pilin. The enzyme than N-methylates the N-terminal amino acid .

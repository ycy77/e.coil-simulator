## Enriched Summary

InsAB' is the transposase for the insertion element IS2. InsAB' binds to both terminal repeats of IS2 and, when overexpressed, generates minicircles containing IS2 and one or two base pairs taken from sequence adjacent to the left inverted repeat of IS2 . InsAB' is most likely the product of a -1 translational frameshift that occurs at an AAAAAAG sequence in the latter portion of insA, allowing translational readthrough past the insA stop codon . The cleavage step that initiates transposition and produces these minicircles always starts on the right inverted repeat before proceeding through a joined dimer intermediate . The process of insertion yields a duplication of 5 base pairs at the insertion site, leaving IS2 flanked by duplicated sequence . IS2 is an insertion sequence present in 0-12 copies in various strains of E. coli . Though it can cause activation and suppression of adjacent genes, activation appears to be due to generation of novel promoters during the insertion process rather than to the presence of promoters carried within IS2 .

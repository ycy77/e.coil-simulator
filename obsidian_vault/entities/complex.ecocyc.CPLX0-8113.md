## Annotation

Early experiments using the heavily mutagenised, E. coli K-12 derived strain, K10 suggested that the PhoU protein was a negative regulator of the pho (phosphate) regulon . This was later supported by experimental work using E. coli K-12 . PhoU negatively regulates expression of the PHOB-MONOMER . phoU may encode a repressor of PhoB or it may act as an activator of the repressor of PhoB . A missense mutation in phoU and a phoU deletion mutation both result in the production of high levels of alkaline phosphatase when strains are grown in excess Pi . PhoU may be directly involved in Pi uptake in association with the ABC-27-CPLX "PstSCAB" phosphate transporter . PhoU has no direct role in Pi uptake . phoU null mutants show a severe growth defect which can be alleviated by compensatory mutations in phoB or pstSCAB leading to the proposal that PhoU is required for normal growth in the presence of the PstSCAB transporter . Cells lacking PhoU, transport phosphate at a higher rate and accumulate higher levels of phosphate suggesting that PhoU modulates the activity of the PstSCAB transporter . PhoU interacts with PhoR and PstB. PhoU, PhoR and PstSCAB may form a Pi signaling complex The PhoU mutations, G29D and A83T, result in increased intracellular levels of Long-Chain-Polyphosphate. Disruption of phoU with a kanamycin cassette also results in accumulation of polyphosphate in the cytoplasm . Revertants with compensatory mutations in the promoter region of pstSCAB or in the phoB coding region, lose the elevated polyphosphate phenotype and are readily isolated after 12-18 hours growth in rich medium .

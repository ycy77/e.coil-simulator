## Enriched Summary

Rhamnogalacturonan type II (RG-II) is a relatively small polysaccharide that makes up about 10% of Pectin pectin. Despite its small size, it, is the most structurally complex pectin component. Its α-1,4-linked D-galacturonate backbone is decorated with four side branches (named A-D) consisting of 12 different types of sugars in over 20 different linkages . Some of the sugars are quite rare, including CPD-13046, CPD-20946 (3-C-carboxy-5-deoxy-L-xylose), and CPD-20949 (Dha). RG-II exists in the primary wall as a dimer that is cross linked by a 1:2 borate-diol diester . The borate diester cross link is formed between the apiosyl residue in side chain A of each monomer subunit . Despite this complexity the structure of RG-II is largely conserved across plant species. It has been reported that the structure is virtually the same in members of the Brassicacea, Cucurbitaceae, Leguminoseae, Apiaceae, Liliaceae, Araceae, Amaryllidaceae, Gramineae, and more families . However, later work reported some variation in the side chains and methylation status within and across different plant speces .

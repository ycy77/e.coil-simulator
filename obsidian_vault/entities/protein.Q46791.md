## Enriched Summary

Uncharacterized response regulatory protein YgeK No information about this protein was found by a literature search conducted on July 10, 2018. YgeK is a predicted member of the NarL family of response regulators of two-component regulatory systems. The protein lacks the receiver domain, which usually contains an Asp residue that is phosphorylated by the cognate histidine kinase. However, expressed and purified YgeK can be phosphorylated in vitro by the noncognate histidine kinases BarA and UhpB . On the other hand, it was suggested that YgeK belongs to the LuxR family of transcription factors . This protein contains a helix-turn-helix motif to bind DNA in its C-terminal domain . Upstream of the ygeK gene, a sequence was found that could work as a transcriptional promoter, and the transcription of the ygeK gene is affected by conditions that generate general transcriptional termination and it is also affected by hupA, hupB and rpoS mutants .

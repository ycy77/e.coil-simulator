## Enriched Summary

"Fucose regulator," FucR, acts as an activator of operons involved in fucose transport and degradation . During induction by fuculose 1-phosphate, FucR binds to DNA, positively autoregulating itself and simultaneously activating transcription of the divergent fucAO operon. Expression of this operon requires growth on L-fucose or L-rhamnose under anaerobic conditions . The repressor gene of this operon is positioned at the 3' end of the operon itself . As a member of the DeoR family, FucR features an N-terminal domain containing a putative helix-turn-helix motif . A positive correlation between cellular growth and the copy number of the protein FucR has been reported . The FucR binding landscape was globally mapped in vivo by standardized ChIP-seq and quantitatively modeled using BoltzNet, a biophysically informed neural network that infers binding energy from sequence data . The resulting ChIP-seq binding regions are available in RegulonDB (Galagan collection), and the corresponding energy matrix and genomic binding profiles can be accessed at boltznet.bu.edu .

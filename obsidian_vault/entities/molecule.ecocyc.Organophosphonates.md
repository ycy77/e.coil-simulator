## Enriched Summary

Organophosphonates are organic molecules containing a direct carbon—phosphorus (C—P) bond in the form of C-PO(OH)2 or C-PO(OR)2 (where R can be either an alkyl or an aryl group). Phosphonates are quite common among different organisms, including both prokaryotes and eukaryotes. They occur primarily as components of phosphonolipids (CPD-1106 is a common phosphonate constituent of phospholipids, analogous to PHOSPHORYL-ETHANOLAMINE). In addition, phosphonates are constituents of polysaccharides, glycoproteins, glycolipids, and several antibiotics. While phosphonates are chemically sturdy compounds, they can be degraded by environmental microorganisms.

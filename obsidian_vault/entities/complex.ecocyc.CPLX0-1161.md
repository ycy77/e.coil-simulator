## Enriched Summary

HslU is the ATPase component of the HslVU protease, which is composed of HslU and HslV . This ATP-stimulated protease exhibits activity similar to that of the chymotrypsin-like activity of the eukaryotic proteasome . HslU also exhibits protein chaperone activity . HslU is required for growth under conditions of elevated temperature . HslU is involved in wild-type cell size control under some conditions . The relationship between HslU and the cell division inhibitor SulA is complicated; HslVU degrades the Lon substrate SulA , whereas HslU also exhibits chaperone activity toward SulA . A similar relationship may exist between HslU and DnaA . The HslVU protease plays a role in clearing the defective peptides produced in the presence of puromycin . HslVU is capable of filling the role of the Lon protease under some conditions . Crystal structures of HslU and of the HslVU complex are presented . HslU and HslV form ring shaped complexes of protein hexamers that stack into a four-ring cylinder with HslU rings on each end and HslV rings in the center . HslU appears to be homodimeric in the absence of ATP and to form a higher-order complex in the presence of ATP .

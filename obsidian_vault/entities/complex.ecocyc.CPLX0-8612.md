## Enriched Summary

mreC is implicated in the formation of cell shape . Cells depleted of MreC become spherical and eventually lyse; MreC is associated with the inner membrane; MreC interacts with EG10608-MONOMER "MreB" and EG10610-MONOMER "MreD" , and with EG10015-MONOMER RodZ . MreC is implicated in an activation pathway that controls peptidoglycan synthesis by the elongasome . MreC interacts directly with EG10606-MONOMER PBP2; MreC modulates the interaction between EG10607-MONOMER MrdB and EG10606-MONOMER PBP2 (the SEDS-bPBP pair of the elongasome/Rod system). MreC contains a predicted N-terminal transmembrane helix and a coiled coil domain (involved in protein dimerization or multimerization); MreC is predicted to be a transmembrane dimer or higher oligomer with an N-terminal cytoplasmic region and a dimeric periplasmic region . The Mre proteins are conditionally essential; MreC- cells are viable under slow growth conditions and divide as small spheres, their death under fast growth conditions can be prevented by an increase in ppGpp levels or an increase in the FtsZ division protein.

## Enriched Summary

This class describes a CPD-8117 group that is incorporated within a lipid molecule CPD-8117 (GLA) is a polyunsaturated (18:3) ω-6 fatty acid (the 6 refers to the position of the first double bond from the methyl end of the fatty acid). It is synthesized from LINOLEIC_ACID by Δ6 desaturase enzymes. Unlike its precursor, GLA is not an essential fatty acid for animals, since almost all animals possess a Δ6 desaturase enzyme. GLA is of major importance in animal cells, being the precursor for the synthesis of several Δ6-desaturated fatty acids, such as ARACHIDONIC_ACID and 5Z8Z11Z14Z17Z-EICOSAPENTAENOATE (see PWY-7592). Δ6-desaturated fatty acids have roles in the maintenance of membrane structure and function, in the regulation of cholesterol synthesis and transport, in the prevention of water loss from the skin, and as precursors of eicosanoids, including prostaglandins and leucotrienes . Like other fatty acids, CPD-8117 is rarely found in its free form. It is usually found either as GAMMA-LINOLENOYL-COA or Gamma-linolenoyl-groups "incorporated into a lipid".

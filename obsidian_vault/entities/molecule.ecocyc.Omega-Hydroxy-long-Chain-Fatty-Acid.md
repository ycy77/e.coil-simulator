An ω-hydroxy fatty acid is Fatty-Acids with a hydroxy group at the end of the chain that is farthest from the carboxylated end. Fatty acids are often classified into four groups by their lengths: Short-chain-fatty-acids "Short-chain fatty acids" (SCFA) are fatty acids with aliphatic chains of fewer than six carbons. Long-Chain-Fatty-Acids "Long-chain fatty acids" (LCFA) are fatty acids with aliphatic chains of 13-22 carbons.

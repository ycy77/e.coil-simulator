## Enriched Summary

A unique feature of mycobacterial cell wall lipids is the presence of very long chain fatty acids with multiple methyl branches toward the carboxyl end of the carbon chain. Many types of such acids exist, including Mycosanoic-Acids "mycosanoic acids", Mycolipanoic-Acids "mycolipanoic acids", Mycolipanolic-Acids "mycolipanolic acids", Mycolipenic-Acids "mycolipenic acids", Mycolipodienoic-Acids "mycolipodienoic acids", Phthioceranic-Acids "phthioceranic acids", and Mycocerosic-Acids "mycocerosic acids". Mycocerosic acids are 2,4,6-trimethyl- and 2,4,6,8-tetramethyl-2-alkanoic acids present in many pathogenic TAX-1762, including TAX-1773, TAX-1765, TAX-1777, TAX-29311, TAX-1768, TAX-1769 . Unlike the other types of fatty acids found in mycobacteria, the chiral centers bearing the methyl groups have an L (levorotatory) stereo configuration. The name "mycocerosic acid" was suggested by Ginger and Anderson in 1945 . The mycocerosic acids of the mycobacteria are found mainly as CPD-18612 (DIM A) waxes or in phenolic glycolipids. In TAX-1773 the common mycoceroic acids are C30 and C32 . In TAX-1765 the common mycoceroic acids are mainly C26, C27, C29, and C30 .

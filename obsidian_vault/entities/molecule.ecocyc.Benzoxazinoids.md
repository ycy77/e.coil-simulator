## Enriched Summary

Benzoxazinoids were identified in the early 1960s as secondary metabolites of the grasses (including the major agricultural crops maize (TAX-4577), wheat (TAX-4565), and rye (TAX-4550)) that function as natural pesticides and exhibit allelopathic properties . Benzoxazinoids are synthesized in two subfamilies of the TAX-4479 and sporadically in single species of the dicots . They play an important role in defense against pests such as insects, pathogenic fungi and bacteria . The most common benzoxazinoids are CPD-6365 and CPD-6366. Since the aglycone benzoxazinoids are autotoxic, they are stored in glucosylated forms that have reduced chemical reactivity . The glucosides are stored in the vacuoles . This allows the plants to store the toxic compounds in anticipation of pathogen attacks, prompting the suggestion to name these compounds "phytoanticipins".

## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: N-terminal pseudogene fragment of this gene. If the reading frame is restored, catalyzes the first step in the biosynthesis of branched-chain amino acids. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P0DP90, ECO:0000305|PubMed:7015336}. ilvG is not expressed in the canonical, sequenced E. coli K-12 strain due to an internal frameshift mutation that effectively cuts it in half . Mutations that remove a single nucleotide or add two nucleotides can revert a strain to allow wild-type production of IlvG . Based on a similar example in S. typhimurium, it has been suggested that this disabling mutation in ilvG is specific to the lab isolate, and is not representative of natural E. coli strains . E. coli K-12 with the ilvG mutation undergo oscillating cycles of isoleucine starvation due to aberrant regulation of isoleucine biosynthesis. This is alleviated by restoration of ilvG expression . ilvG expression increases during growth on acetate . ptsN knockout phenotypes relating to nitrogen source utilization regulation were observed only in strains lacking functional ilvG . In non-K-12 strains, site-directed mutagenesis and crystallographic studies of IlvG have helped define the catalytic mechanism .

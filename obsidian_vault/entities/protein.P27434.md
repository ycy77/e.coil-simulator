## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Cytoskeletal protein that is involved in cell-shape control through regulation of the length of the long axis. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19008860}. RodZ is a bitopic inner membrane protein that is involved in the maintenance of cell shape through interaction with the EG10608-MONOMER "MreB" cytoskeleton . RodZ links MreB to cell wall synthesis proteins (possibly EG10606-MONOMER "MrdA" and /or EG10607-MONOMER "RodA"); this interaction mediates MreB processive motion as the cell wall is synthesized . Regulation of RodZ modulates MreB localization patterns to drive changes in cell shape; RodZ binding is predicted to alter the biophysical properties of MreB filaments . RodZ colocalizes to the division site with MreB; RodZ localizes to the division site in a EG10347-MONOMER "FtsZ"-dependent way; RodZ physically interacts with FtsZ; RodZ is involved in formation of the Z ring during the early stage of cell division . GFP-RodZ fusion proteins distribute in a spiral-like or banded manner along the length of bacterial cells . RodZ and MreB fluorescent fusion proteins colocalise in a helical manner along the inner membrane . BACTH (bacterial two-hybrid) assays suggest that RodZ interacts with MreB and MreC in vivo . RodZ is required for the proper formation of MreB spirals . RodZ and MreB colocalize and rotate together around the cell circumference...

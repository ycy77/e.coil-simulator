## Enriched Summary

The sialyl Lewis-X (SLeX) antigen is expressed on glycolipids and glycoproteins on the cell surfaces. In adults, SLeX antigen is expressed principally on the surfaces of granulocytic cells. It plays an important role during early steps of inflammatory response as a ligand of the three known selectins - E-selectin (also known as endothelial leucocyte adhesion molecules, ELAM-1), P-Selectin, and L-selectin, resulting in the capture of leucocytes from the plasma and their attachment to blood endothelial cells . The antigen is expressed in high frequency in tumor tissue where it plays a role in adhesion between tumor cells and blood endothelial cells during tumor metastases . The SLeX antigen is synthesized from B-Gal-14-NacGlc-R by the action of two enzymes, EC-2.4.3.6 and EC-2.4.1.152 .

## Enriched Summary

The aminoglycosides are a class of clinical antibiotics that interact with bacterial ribosomal RNA and inhibit protein synthesis. The majority of the aminoglycosides consist of a unique aminocyclitol CPD-10151 core, which is decorated with various aminosugars, leading to diverse structural molecules,including Kanamycins kanamycin, CPD-14154 tobramycin, Gentamycins gentamycin, CPD-14143 ribostamycin, CPD-14140 butirosin, Neomycins neomycin, CPD-14177, Lividomycins lividomycin, CPD-14157 apramycin, and HYGROMYCIN-B. Other groups of aminoglycosides contain cyclitols either derived from either MYO-INOSITOL or from D-SEDOHEPTULOSE-7-P.

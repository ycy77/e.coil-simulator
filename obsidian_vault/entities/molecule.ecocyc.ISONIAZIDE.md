## Enriched Summary

ISONIAZIDE Isoniazid is a clinically approved antituberculosis agent. It was first described in 1912 and has been used as an anti-tuberculosus drug since 1952 . Its activity is specific to the mycobacteria genus . Its high bactericidal activity, low cost, high bioavailability, excellent intracellular penetration, and narrow spectrum of action have made ISONIAZIDE one of the best antimicrobial agents Isoniazid is a prodrug and must be activated within TAX-1773 by the CPLX-9239 . KatG forms CPD-20007, which spontaneously couples with NAD+/NADH to form an CPD-20008 . The precise mechanism of ISONIAZIDE action on TAX-1773 is still poorly understood . However, one well-documented primary target is EC-1.3.1.118, which is encoded by the G185E-5668 gene. The CPD-20008 binds tightly to this enzyme, thereby blocking the action of fatty acid synthase and the synthesis of mycolic acids . ## Annotation

ISONIAZIDE Isoniazid is a clinically approved antituberculosis agent. It was first described in 1912 and has been used as an anti-tuberculosus drug since 1952 . Its activity is specific to the mycobacteria genus . Its high bactericidal activity, low cost, high bioavailability, excellent intracellular penetration, and narrow spectrum of action have made ISONIAZIDE one of the best antimicrobial agents Isoniazid is a prodrug and must be activated within TAX-1773 by the CPLX-9239 . KatG forms CPD-20007, which spontaneously couples with NAD+/NADH to form an CPD-20008 . The precise mechanism of ISONIAZIDE action on TAX-1773 is still poorly understood . However, one well-documented primary target is EC-1.3.1.118, which is encoded by the G185E-5668 gene. The CPD-20008 binds tightly to this enzyme, thereby blocking the action of fatty acid synthase and the synthesis of mycolic acids .

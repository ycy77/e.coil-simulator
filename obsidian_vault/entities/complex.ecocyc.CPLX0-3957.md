## Annotation

SbcCD is an ATP dependent, structure specific DNA nuclease that is required for the completion of chromosome replication and is implicated in the response to palindrome induced genetic instability. Purified SbcCD has ATP-Mn2+-dependent double strand DNA exonuclease activity and ATP-independent single-strand DNA endonuclease activity . SbcCD cleaves hairpin structures that cause DNA replication to stall; cleavage does not require free 3' or 5' DNA ends and generates products with 3'-hydroxyl and 5'-phosphate termini . SbcCD can insert double strand breaks into a duplex DNA substrate; SbcCD can remove protein bound to a DNA end by introducing a double strand break . SbcCD can cleave an interrupted DNA palindrome in vivo generating a two-ended double strand break; cleavage is dependent on replication and repair of the break occurs via the RecBCD recombination pathway .

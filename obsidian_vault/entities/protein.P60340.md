## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Responsible for synthesis of pseudouridine from uracil-55 in the psi GC loop of transfer RNAs. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_01080, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11142385, ECO:0000269|PubMed:7489483}. TruB is a pseudouridine synthase that catalyzes the pseudouridylation of U55 in tRNA . The base modification generated by TruB activity contributes to the wild-type thermal stability of tRNA . In addition to its catalytic activity, TruB also acts as a tRNA chaperone ; early mutagenesis results had already suggested such a role . tRNAs carry multiple modifications, often in nearby nucleotide residues. The preferred substrate for the TruB methyltransferase and tRNA binding activities is unmodified tRNA . Crystal structures of TruB have been solved . TruB recognizes the folded T-loop structure of tRNAs and undergoes significant conformational changes upon tRNA binding . Recognition of the tRNA substrate requires the 53:61 base pair of the T-loop stem as well as several conserved nucleotides within the loop . TruB binds both folded and misfolded tRNAs . The substrate specificity of TruB has been described . Site-directed mutagenesis of the conserved amino acid residue D48 and Y76 yields catalytically inactive enzymes.

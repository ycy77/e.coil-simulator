## Enriched Summary

Amines are derivatives of AMMONIA, with one or more of the hydrogen atoms replaced by organic substituents such as alkyl and aryl groups. When only one of the three hydrogen atoms in ammonia is replaced by an alkyl group, the compound is called Primary-Amines. When two, three, or four of the hydtogen atoms are replaced, the compound is called Secondary-Amines, Tertiary-Amines, or Quaternary-Amines, respectively.

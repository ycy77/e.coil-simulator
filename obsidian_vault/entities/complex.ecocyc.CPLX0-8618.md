## Enriched Summary

SymE belongs to the AbrB superfamily of proteins, which also contains the antitoxin MazE . SymE binds DNA; ectopic expression of SymE leads to nucleoid condensation, which blocks DNA replication and transcription . Purified SymE oligomerizes, potentially forming homotetramers . SymE co-purifies with certain ribosomal proteins . SymE exhibits properties of the toxin of a toxin-antitoxin system. Overexpression of SymE reduces colony-forming ability and protein synthesis and leads to mRNA degradation . Ectopic expression of SymE leads to the expression of DNA damage-inducible genes; an assay for dsDNA break formation showed that SymE accumulation in fact causes DNA damage . It was initially thought that SymE itself degrades mRNAs ; however, SymE has no detectable mRNA cleavage activity in vivo . Expression of SymE is regulated at the level of transcription, translation, mRNA- and protein stability. Transcription of symE is induced by DNA-damaging agents such as mitomycin C , chloroacetaldehyde and styrene oxide , and is regulated by LexA . Translation of symE is repressed by the small RNA SymR, which is encoded on the antisense strand of the 5' region of the symE mRNA. SymR also appears to affect stability of the symE mRNA.

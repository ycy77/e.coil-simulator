Current data suggests that HypC delivers Fe-CO2 to HypD, where CO2 is reduced to CO . HypD alone has low intrinsic ATPase activity; the purified HypC-HypD complex catalyzes hydrolysis of ATP with a 5-fold higher kcat of ~ 0.85 s-1 . HypE catalyzes the ATP hydrolysis-dependent dehydration of the carbamate group to a cyanate group which is transferred to the HypCD complex . The HypCD complex serves as the scaffold for assembly of the Fe(CN)2(CO) ligand of [NiFe]-hydrogenase . HypD is the scaffold protein for assembly of the Fe-(CN)2CO cofactor of [NiFe] hydrogenases . Current data suggests that HypC delivers Fe-CO2 to HypD, where CO2 is reduced to CO . In addition to the four iron ions contained in the [4Fe-4S] cluster of HypD, the HypCD complex contains two labile iron ions that are lost during aerobic purification . A purified HypCD complex containing all cofactors was subjected to a variety of experimental techniques, showing that the [4Fe-4S] cluster and the mononuclear iron site of the HypCD complex are redox active .

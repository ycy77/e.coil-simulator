## Enriched Summary

The pungency of chili or hot peppers comes from phenolic alkaloids called Capsaicinoids capsaicinoids. These compounds are exclusively produced in fruits of plants belonging to the TAX-4071 genus. A quarter of the world population consumes hot peppers in some form daily . Structurally capsaicinoids consist of a common aromatic moiety, CPD-9327, modified by a short chain fatty acid that may have variable length and different degrees of saturation . The accumulation of Capsaicinoids capsaicinoids is strictly correlated with developmental stages of the fruit and usually spans the ripening stages after fruit size increase has ceased . The capsaicinoids are localized in the epidermal cells of the fruit placenta . Pungency differs considerably in species ranging from very hot to sweet peppers and has a positive correlation with the level of capsaicinoids biosynthetic gene expression . CAPSAICIN Capsaicin and CPD-9185 are the most common types. Other naturally occurring capsaicinoids include CPD-9182, CPD-9333, CPD-9331, CPD-9181, and CPD-9330, which differ in the fatty acid chain length (n=7-10) and the presence and/or position of a double bond .

## Annotation

The transcriptional regulator Fur, for "Ferric Uptake Regulation", controls the transcription of genes involved in iron homeostasis and a PD00440 "minor sigma factor" that initiates transcription of ferric citrate transport genes in response to the presence of periplasmic iron(III) dicitrate . Fur also regulates transcription of many other genes involved in different cellular functions, including the following: flagellum chemotaxis , methionine and L-threonine biosynthesis , acid and oxidative stresses , metal ion stress , resistance to cobalt and nickel , the tricarboxylic acid cycle , glycolysis and gluconeogenesis , respiration , porins , purine metabolism , 2,3-dihydroxybenzoate biosynthesis , and phage DNA packaging . Fur is capable of controlling its own synthesis . It was observed that in the absence of Fur, E. coli K12 did not exhibit a transcriptional response to iron, suggesting that Fur serves as the sole regulatory factor governing the direct iron response . The manner in which Fur regulates E. coli motility has been shown to vary depending on the specific strain . It has been suggested that the regulation of Fur over carbon metabolism may contribute to facilitating enterobactin biosynthesis and citrate accumulation . Based on transcriptome analysis, expression levels of a set of genes regulated by Fur change substantially in response to antibiotic treatment . On the other hand, inhibition of fur expression by CRISPRi enhanced cellular fitness under treatment with the antibiotic polymyxin B, but reduced it under treatment with carbonyl cyanide 3-chlorophenylhydrazone (CCCP) or pyocyanin .

## Enriched Summary

YbiB is a member of the TrpD2 protein family and represents a new class of non-specific DNA binding proteins. The TrpD2 protein family is part of the phosphoribosyl transferase III superfamily, but YbiB did not show phosphoribosyltransferase or nucleoside phosphorylase activity under the conditions tested . A crystal structure of YbiB has been solved at 1.94 Å resolution. The structure shows a head-to-head homodimeric architecture in which each monomer consists of an N-terminal α-helical domain and a C-terminal α/β domain. The YbiB dimer has two highly positively charged grooves on its surface with which it binds to nucleic acids in a sequence-independent manner . YbiB is part of a EG10533 LexA-regulated operon, together with the EG11357 helicase gene, suggesting that it may play a role in the SOS stress response . YbiB interacts with the G7656-MONOMER. The interaction occurs between the positively charged grooves on the surface of YbiB and the intrinsically disordered and highly negatively charged C-terminal domain of ObgE. Binding by ObgE efficiently inhibits the binding of DNA to YbiB. YbiB binding does not influence the intrinsic GTP hydrolysis activity of ObgE (which is slow) . The ybiB mRNA belongs to a group of transcripts whose levels at different growth rates are under predominantly degradational control...

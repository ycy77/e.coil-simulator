## Enriched Summary

SecB is the core chaperone of the Sec secretion pathway. SecB maintains newly synthesized secretory pre-proteins in their unfolded, translocation-competent state and delivers them to the membrane-embedded SECE-G-Y-CPLX Sec translocon. SecB also functions as a general chaperone - it is able to assist the folding of cytosolic proteins . Protein purification studies have shown that SecB codes for a cytoplasmic homotetramer required for the translocation of preproteins into inverted inner membrane vesicles . SecB has an affinity for a number of periplasmic and outer-membrane proteins in their unfolded forms.

## Enriched Summary

Aldoses "An aldose" is a monosaccharide containing one aldehyde group per molecule and having a chemical formula of the form CnH2n-1On where n>=3. Aldoses can form cyclical structures by a reaction between the hydroxyl group on carbon 5 with the aldehyde at carbon 1, forming a a six-membered ring consisting of five carbon atoms and one oxygen atom. These cyclical molecules are referred to as aldopyranoses, since the ring is reminiscent of Pyrans pyran (although with no double bonds).

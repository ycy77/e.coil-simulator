## Enriched Summary

β-1,2-Glucan is a homopolymer composed of glucose residues connected by β(1,2) linkages. This type of glucan has been reported from some Gram-negative bacteria, such as TAX-357, TAX-379, TAX-28105, and TAX-234, where it exists mainly in cyclic form . Cyclic β-1,2-glucans have been reported to play a role in osmotic regulation , but were also reported to act as commensal factors in TAX-379 and as infectious factors in TAX-235 . Cyclic β-1,2-glucans are usually found with a degree of polymerization (DPs) around 20, although a much longer glucan (DPs of around 40) has been reported from TAX-382 .

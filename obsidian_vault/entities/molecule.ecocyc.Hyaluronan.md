Hyaluronan Hyaluronan is an anionic, nonsulfated Glycosaminoglycans "glycosaminoglycan" distributed widely throughout connective, epithelial, and neural tissues, including the vitreous humor of the eye, the aorta, blood, brain, liver and skin . It is produced by almost all members of the animal kingdom as well as by certain members of streptococci . Hyaluronan can be very large, with its molecular weight often reaching the millions. It forms a strikingly viscous solution and functions primarily as a joint lubricant and shock absorber, although it is also thought to be involved in cell proliferation, migration and differentiation.

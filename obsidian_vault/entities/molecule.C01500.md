## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: Geraniol Natural essential oils contain monoterpenes as one of their major constituents. The oil accumulates in glands found in leaves, petals and trichomes. GERANIOL "Geraniol" is an acyclic monoterpene alcohol that is found in the essential oils of many plant species, including nutmeg, TAX-94328 (ginger) , TAX-39350 (basil) , rosemary, sage, cardamom, grapefruit, and TAX-66014 (lemon grass) . It is one of the three terpene compounds principally responsible for muscat aroma, and is emitted from flowers of many plant species with an intense rose scent. GERANIOL Geraniol makes up to 86-98% of the essential oil in leaves of the camphor tree (TAX-192326) . GERANIOL "Geraniol" has cancer antiproliferative properties due to its inhibitory effect on polyamine metabolism. It is known to decreases the activity of EC-4.1.1.17, a key polyamine biosynthesis enzyme that is enhanced during cancer growth .

## Enriched Summary

O-Linked glycans are polysaccharide or oligosaccharide that are linked to a protein via the oxygen atom in the side chain of an SER or THR residue. Most commonly O-glycosylation begins with the attachment of CPD-3604 to the free hydroxyl group of the amino acid, although O-linkages to fucose, glucose, mannose, xylose and other sugars are known. In eukaryotes, O-linked glycans are assembled in the Golgi apparatus one sugar at a time, while attached to the core protein. Mucin-type O-glycosylation, consisting of glycans attached via O-linked CPD-3604, is one of the most abundant forms of protein glycosylation in animals. Eight core-type structures of mucin-type O-glycans have been proposed, including Core1 "core 1", Core2 "core 2", GLUCOSAMINYL-13-N-ETCETERA-GALACTOSAMINY "core 3", GLUCOSAMINYL-16-ETCETERA-GALACTOSAMINYL- "core 4", Core5 "core 5", Core6 "core 6", Core7 "core 7" and Core8 "core 8" . Note that core 1, also known as the T antigen, corresponds to the beta-Gal-13-alpha-GalNac-R "type 3 histo-blood group precursor disaccharide".

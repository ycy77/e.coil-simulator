## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: Dextran; 1,6-alpha-D-Glucan; (1,6-alpha-D-Glucosyl)n; (1,6-alpha-D-Glucosyl)n+1; (1,6-alpha-D-Glucosyl)m; (1,6-alpha-D-Glucosyl)m+1; Dextran 40; Dextran 70; Dextran 75 EcoCyc common name: a dextran. Dextran is a complex branched polysaccharide made of many glucose molecules joined into chains of varying lengths. The main chain is a linear polymer of α1->6 linked D-glucose molecules, with branches beginning from α-(1,2), α-(1,3), and α-(1,4) linkages. Dextrans produced by different organisms differ in their yields, structures, and physico-chemical properties. The most widely used dextran, which is produced by TAX-1245, is composed of approximately 97% α-1,6 linkages, with most of the remaining linkages being α-(1,3) . On the other hand, more than 30% of the linkages in the dextran produced by TAX-33964 NM105 are α-(1,2) Earlier studies of dextran structure reported the existence of longer branches of up to 50 residues . However, later studies of dextran from TAX-1245 found much shorter side chains, with about 40% of them containing only one D-glucose residue, at least 45% containing two D-glucose units, and the rest (max. 15%) longer than two units. The branching chains also consist of mostly α1->6 linkages .

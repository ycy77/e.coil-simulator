## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: Globoside; Globoside I; N-Acetyl-galactosaminyl-1,3-D-galactosyl-1,4-D-galactosyl-1,4-D-glucosylceramide; N-Acetyl-D-galactosaminyl-1,3-D-galactosyl-1,4-D-galactosyl-1,4-D-glucosylceramide EcoCyc common name: a globotetraosylceramide. Originally, the name "globoside" was used as a generic name for the mucolipids present on human erythrocytes . Later it was used for mucolipids possessing hexosamines as the only nitrogen-containing sugars. A structural analysis of the main component of the mucolipids identifed it as N-ACETYL-D-GALACTOSAMINYL-13-D-GALACT globotetraosylceramide and today the term globoside is a synonym for that compound. Globotetraosylceramide was characterized as a surface antigen produced by peripheral T cells (lymph node lymphocytes), previosuly known as T lymphocyte macrophage-associated antigen, or TLMA .

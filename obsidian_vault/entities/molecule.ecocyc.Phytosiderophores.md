## Enriched Summary

Siderophore Siderophores are small, high-affinity iron chelating compounds usually produced and secreted by microorganisms. Once outside the cell, siderophores bind FE+3 with high affinity, and the iron-siderophore complexes are imported back into the cell by active-transport mechanisms. Some grasses are known to make siderophores as well. These Phytosiderophores "phytosiderophores" are also known as mugineic acids.

## Enriched Summary

The glycation reaction, also known as the Maillard reaction, as first identified by the French chemist L. Maillard, is ubiquitous in nature. The reaction involves the formation of Schiff's bases between reducing sugars (such as glucose) and biological amines such as an amino acid, which then rearrange to form stable ketoamine products, or amadori compounds.

## Enriched Summary

The RdcA GTPase interacts with the MASE1 (Membrane-Associated SEnsor) domain of DgcE and controls its activity . The N-terminal domain of RdcA resembles the dynamin family of GTPases, but lacks the typical membrane-binding motif . The conserved threonine residue T103, which is part of the dynamin GTPase active site, is required for activation of DgcE, while a mutation in K82, predicted to be part of the GTP binding site of RdcA, has no effect. The T103D variant is no longer able to interact with DgcE, but does interact with RdcB . In vitro, RdcA/CrfC binds to the EG10242-MONOMER clamp (DnaN) component of the replisome and promotes the colocalization of nascent DNA strands in sister replication forks. It contains a clamp-binding consensus motif in its N-terminal region and can bind multiple clamp molecules . RdcA/CrfC localizes to nucleoid poles . A mutation in rdcA partially suppresses the motility defect of a ΔpdeH mutant . A ΔrdcA mutant shows a defect in biofilm formation and chromosome positioning and segregation . RdcA is expressed throughout the growth cycle .

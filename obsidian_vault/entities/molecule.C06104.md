## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: Adipate; Adipic acid; Hexanedioate; Hexan-1,6-dicarboxylate; Hexanedioic acid General Background ADIPATE Adipate is a dicaboxylic acid that is present in the juice of sugar beet and sugar cane, though the biosynthetic pathway in these plants is still unknown. Adipate has also been identified as a metabolic intermediate in the biodegradation pathways of cyclic alkanes, cyclic alcohols, and long chain aliphatic dicarboxylic acids . Adipate is used in the chemical manufacturing industry, mostly for the production of nylon. Adipate monomers are condensed with hexamethylene diamine, forming Nylon-6, the most common form of nylon. Other uses for adipate include the production of polyurethane, formation of plasticizers and lubricant components, and as a flavorant and gelling aid in the food industry. Most of the adipate in commercial processes is produced from petrochemicals, specifically by oxidation of KA oil (CYCLOHEXANOL and CYCLOHEXANONE) catalyzed by nitric acid .

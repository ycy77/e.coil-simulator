## Enriched Summary

A nucleotide is composed of Bases (nitrogenous base), a five-carbon sugar (either CPD0-1110 ribose or 2-DEOXYRIBOSE), and one to three phosphate groups attached to one of the carbons of the ribose or deoxyribose moiety. When joined together, nucleotides make up the structural units of RNA and DNA. The nucleotides are called Ribonucleotides when the sugar is CPD0-1110 ribose, and Deoxynucleotides when it is 2-DEOXYRIBOSE. In the absence of phosphate, the combination of Bases (nitrogenous base) and a five-carbon sugar is called Nucleosides.

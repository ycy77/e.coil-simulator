## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Involved in peptide bond synthesis. Allows translation of proteins with poly-acidic sequences in the middle of the protein, which otherwise cause intrinsic ribosome destabilization (IRD) due to difficulties in maintaining an intact ribosome. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:38661232}. YbiT belongs to the YbiT subfamily of the ABCF family of ATP-binding cassette (ABC) proteins . There are four ABCF family proteins within E. coli K-12: YheS, YJJK-MONOMER "EttA", UUP-MONOMER "Uup" and G6423-MONOMER "YbiT", all of which are involved in noncanonical translation of 'hard-to-translate' nascent peptides getting them through the ribosome exit tunnel. YheS represses poly-acidic sequence-dependent intrinsic ribosome destabilization, stalling and premature termination . Overexpression of an EQ2 mutant of YbiT (carrying substitutions in the predicted ATPase active sites) of E. coli CFT073 inhibits protein synthesis . Accumulation of the cationic fluorescent dye 3,3'-dipropylthiadicarbocyanine iodide (diS-C3) is greater in ybiT knockout cells compared to wild type . The binding of the transcription factor CPLX0-226 CRP to a predicted CRP DNA binding site around the start of the ybiT gene has been demonstrated; however an effect of this binding on gene expression has not been analyzed...

## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Integrase is necessary for integration of the phage into the host genome by site-specific recombination. In conjunction with excisionase, integrase is also necessary for excision of the prophage from the host genome. IntS is the integrase required for both integration and excision of the prophage KpLE1 . A narrow concentration range of IntS enables excisive recombination . IntS binds DNA at the KplE1 attachment site attL . In the presence of their DNA target sites, IntS oligomers are stabilized . The attL site overlaps with the intS promoter region, thereby enabling negative autoregulation of intS expression by IntS .

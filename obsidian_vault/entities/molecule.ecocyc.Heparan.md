## Enriched Summary

This compound class describes a Heparosan polymer, attached to a serine residue in a glycoprotein, prior to any subsequent modifications. In animal tissues heparosan is synthesized attached to cell surface or extracellular matrix proteins, known as "core proteins". Heparosan consists of a repeating disaccharide unit composed of CPD-12521-(1,4)-CPD-12541. The disaccharide units are linked by a (1-4) linkage to each other. The polymer chain is attached to the core proteins via a tetrasaccharide linkage region that differs in composition from the rest of the polymer. The heparosan skeleton serves as a starting point in the biosynthesis of several other glycosaminoglycan such as Heparan-Sulfate and HEPARIN, and bacterial heparosan is sometimes used for the synthesis of non-animal sourced heparin-like products.

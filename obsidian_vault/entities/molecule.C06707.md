## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: Teichoic acid EcoCyc common name: a teichoic acid. Teichoic acids were first reported in 1958 , after being discovered serendipitously as "contaminants" in bacterial extracts from TAX-1590 . As they make up a significant portion of the cell walls (about 60% in TAX-1423) , they were named based on the Greek word teikhos, which means a fortification wall. The teichoic acids are found in the cell walls of a wide range of Gram-positive bacteria . There are two main types of teichoic acids: the lipoteichoic acids (LTAs), which are anchored to the plasma membrane and extend from the cell surface into the peptidoglycan layer , and the wall teichoic acids (WTAs), which are covalently attached to peptidoglycan and extend through and beyond the cell wall. In TAX-224308 the main form of teichoic acid is a polymer of CPD-75 "poly(glycerol phosphate)", while in TAX-655816 the major variety is a polymer of CPD-19310 "poly(ribitol phosphate)" . In both cases, the repeating units are joined together by phosphodiester linkages, and are anchored via 2-3 glycerol phosphate units to a N-acetyl-β-D-mannosaminyl-(1→4)-N-acetyl-α-D-glucosamine disaccharide. The glycerol and ribitol units of the polysaccharides are modified with D-alanine esters and/or monosaccharides such as glucose or N-acetylglucosamine...

## Enriched Summary

An oligosaccharide is a saccharide polymer containing a small number (typically three to six) of component monosaccharides (also known as simple sugars). Oligosaccharides are often found either O- or N-linked to compatible amino acid side chains in proteins or to lipid moieties.

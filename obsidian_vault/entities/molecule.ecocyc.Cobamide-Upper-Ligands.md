## Enriched Summary

Cobamides "A cobamide" is a corrinoid molecule based on the structure of CPD-20843, a cobalt-corrinoid hexaamide that is formed from COBINAMIDE by the attachment of an α-D-ribofuranose 3-phosphate residue at the 2 position of the aminopropanol chain. Cobamides serve as cofactors for a number of important enzymes. A key structural feature of cobinamides is a corrin ring with a cobalt ion in its center. Four of the six coordination sites of the cobalt ion are provided by the corrin ring, while the other two are provided by Cobamide-Upper-Ligands upper (β) and Cobamide-Lower-Ligands lower (α) ligands, which are located above and below the ring plane, respectively. The best known cobamide, Cobalamins cobalamin, contains DIMETHYLBENZIMIDAZOLE as the lower ligand and either a methyl group or 5'-deoxyadenosyl group as the upper ligand.

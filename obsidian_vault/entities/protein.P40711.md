## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Rescues stalled ribosomes (PubMed:21051357, PubMed:21418110, PubMed:22922063). Can hydrolyze peptidyl-tRNA on ribosomes stalled by both non-stop mRNAs and mRNAs that contain rare codon clusters (PubMed:21051357) or ribosomes stalled in the middle of mRNA (PubMed:22922063). First identified as a complementary ribosome rescue system when the stalled ribosome cannot be rescued by the SsrA(tmRNA)-SmpB quality control system or the alternative ribosome-rescue factor A (arfA) (PubMed:21418110). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:21051357, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21418110, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22422986, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22922063}. ArfB is a protein involved in the rescue of stalled ribosomes. The protein is able to hydrolyze peptidyl-tRNAs generated by translation of non-stop mRNAs or mRNAs containing clusters of rare codons . ArfB has similarity to domain III of EG10761-MONOMER "RF1" and EG10762-MONOMER "RF2", including the GGQ motif required for peptidyl-tRNA hydrolase activity, but not to domain II, which contains the stop codon recognition domain of the release factors . The crystal structure of the ArfB-ribosome complex suggests that the ArfB tail functions as a sensor probing the occupancy of the mRNA entry channel, thereby distinguishing between stalled and translating ribosomes . ArfB appears to be associated with the 70S ribosome and polysome fractions...

## Annotation

MinC acts to ensure correct placement of the septum during cell division by suppressing formation of septa at aberrant loci within the cell. MinC-MinD has been reported to interfere with EG10347-MONOMER "FtsZ" ring assembly and with FtsA assembly into the FtsZ ring . MinC is involved in both MinC-MinD-MinE and in EG10227-MONOMER "DicB"-MinC mediated cell division inhibition. MinC homomultimerizes . The MinC-MinD complex inhibits septum formation at any site within the cell, and MinE acts to localize and restrict inhibition to the inappropriate septation sites . MinC interacts with FtsZ; MinC inhibits FtsZ polymerization but not FtsZ GTPase activity . MinC promotes depolymerization of FtsZ bundles by enhancing the detachment rate of FtsZ-GDP and reducing the attachment rate of FtsZ-GTP monomers . MinD ATPase activity is required for the MinC-mediated inhibition pathway , and MinD ATPase activity is positively regulated by MinE and phospholipid . MinD affects the affinity of MinC for the septal ring . The location of the MinC-MinD complex oscillates from pole to pole within the cell; MinE causes complex dissociation that is followed rapidly by complex formation at the opposite pole . Early

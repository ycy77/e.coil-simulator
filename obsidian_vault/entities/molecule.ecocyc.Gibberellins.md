## Enriched Summary

Gibberellins (GAs) are plant hormones that regulate growth and influence various developmental processes, including stem elongation, germination, dormancy, flowering, sex expression, enzyme induction, and leaf and fruit senescence. All known gibberellins are diterpenoid acids that are synthesized by the terpenoid pathway in plastids and then modified in the endoplasmic reticulum and cytosol until they reach their biologically-active form (see PWY-5052). The gibberellins are named GA1 through GAn in order of discovery. All gibberellins have either 19 or 20 carbon units grouped into either four or five ring systems (see CPD1F-95 and CPD1F-134, respectively). The fifth ring is a lactone ring. GAs containing a tetracyclic ent-gibberellane structure (CPD1F-95) are called C20-GAs, whereas GAs containing a pentacyclic 20-nor-ent-gibberellane structure (CPD1F-134) are called C19-GAs. C20-GAs do not usually have biological activity but can be metabolized to active C19-GAs (note that not all C19-GAs are bioactive).

## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Endonuclease IV plays a role in DNA repair. It cleaves phosphodiester bonds at apurinic or apyrimidinic (AP) sites, generating a 3'-hydroxyl group and a 5'-terminal sugar phosphate. It preferentially attacks modified AP sites created by bleomycin and neocarzinostatin. Endonuclease IV is an inducible apurinic/apyrimidinic (AP) site endonuclease. Early studies purified and characterized Endonuclease IV (Endo IV) from K-12 strains . Endo IV is induced when cells are grown in the presence of the DNA damaging agents, methyl viologen, and to a lesser extent, plumbagin, phenazine methosulfate and menadione . The purified enzyme catalyses AP endonuclease activity on plasmid DNA containing AP sites; it also exhibits 3'-phosphomonesterase and 3'-phosphodiesterase activity - releasing a variety of fragments from DNA 3'-termini, including phosphate, phosphoglycoaldehyde and deoxyribose 5-phosphate . The enzyme may or may not have 3'-5' exonuclease activity. Purified Endo IV is a metal-dependent endonuclease . Endo IV can incise the DNA backbone 5' of the oxidative lesion Î±-deoxyadenosine (see further ). Endo IV is involved in the (glycosylase independent) nucleotide incision repair (NIR) pathway which is able to process a number of lesions generated by oxidative stress including 6-dihydrouracil, 5,6-dihydrothymidine, Î±-deoxyadenosine, and 5-hydroxyuridine...

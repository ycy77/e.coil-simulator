## Enriched Summary

The TusBDC complex functions as a sulfur mediator during synthesis of 2-thiouridine of the modified wobble base mnm5s2U in tRNA. Activated sulfur appears to be transferred from TusA to a cysteine residue in TusD and subsequently transferred to TusE. TusE stimulates the transfer of sulfur from TusA to TusD. The TusBCD complex does not appear to bind tRNA directly in an in vitro assay, but it does interact with TusE . A crystal structure of the TusBCD complex has been determined at 2.15 Å resolution. All three subunits show structural similarity to a protein of unknown function, YchN .

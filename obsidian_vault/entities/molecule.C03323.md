KEGG compound: (2,1-beta-D-Fructosyl)n; (2,1-beta-D-Fructosyl)n+1; Inulin; (1,2-beta-D-Fructosyl)n; (1,2-beta-D-Fructosyl)m; (1,2-beta-D-Fructosyl)n+1; (1,2-beta-D-Fructosyl)m-1 EcoCyc common name: an inulin. Inulins are polymers mainly comprising fructose units joined by a β-(2-1) glycosidic bond. They are typically terminated by a single glucose unit. Inulin serves as a storage polysaccharide accumulating in the underground organs of some members of the TAX-4210 and TAX-4479, including TAX-2067293, Jerusalem artichoke (TAX-4233) and chicory (TAX-13427) . It was originally isolated from TAX-55635 roots by the German scientist Valentin Rose in 1804, hence its name. Plant inulins vary in size, but typically contain between 2-60 fructose units .

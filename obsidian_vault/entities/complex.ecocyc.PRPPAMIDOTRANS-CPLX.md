## Enriched Summary

Amidophosphoribosyl transferase (PurF) catalyzes the committed step in purine de novo biosynthesis. In this reaction an amino group derived from glutamine is added to the C-1 of 5-phospho-α-D-ribose 1-diphosphate (PRPP), the diphosphoryl group is displaced, and glutamate is produced . There is kinetic evidence for channeling of the reaction product phosphoribosylamine (PRA) from PurF to PurD, the next enzyme in the pathway ; a docking and channeling model for the transfer of PRA has been proposed . PurF can use ammonia in place of glutamine ; in the absence of other substrates, it was also shown to have a low level of glutaminase activity which is inhibited by GMP and AMP . Affinity labeling studies showed the amino-terminal cysteine to be the active site residue necessary for glutamine amide transfer .

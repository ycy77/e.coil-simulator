## Enriched Summary

Some organisms produce phospholipids in which the fatty acyl chains contain cyclopropane rings. These rings are formed by tenzymes that transfer a methylene moiety from S-adenosylL-methionine to double bonds of unsaturated fatty acyl chains within the lipids. In most organisms the cyclopropane groups are cis-configured and are formed by the action of EC-2.1.1.79, which acts only on cis double bonds. The MYCOLIC-ACIDS "mycolic acids" of mycobacteria often contain additional trans-configured cyclopropane groups that are formed by EC-2.1.1.M101 . The physiological role of cyclopropane-containing fatty acyl chains (CFA) is to stabilize the membranes by increased ordering of the chains without sacrificing membrane fluidity. This can decrease the proton permeability of phospholipid bilayers, helping the bacteria survive acid shock . trans-configured CFA in mycolic acids were reported to be correlated with the persistence of the pathogen and to modulate the innate immune response of the host .

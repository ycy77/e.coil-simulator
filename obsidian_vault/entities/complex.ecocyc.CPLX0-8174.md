## Enriched Summary

Cas1 and Cas2 are the only proteins universally conserved between CRISPR-Cas systems and form a stable complex that is required for spacer acquisition but not for pre-existing spacers . Cas1 and Cas2 together are required for efficient adaptation of the CRISPR array . Mutations that affect the formation of a stable Cas1-Cas2 complex are defective in spacer acquisition, possibly due to lack of recognition of the leader-repeat sequence of the CRISPR locus . In the presence of Cas2, Cas1 catalyzes a concerted cleavage-ligation reaction inserting new spacer DNA at the leader proximal repeat . The reaction requires supercoiled target DNA and protospacer DNA with free 3'-OH ends . The Cas1-Cas2 complex is predominantly heterotetrameric in solution, although the crystal structure of the complex indicates that a hexameric state consisting of two Cas1 dimers and one Cas2 dimer is possible .

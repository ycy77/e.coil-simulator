## Enriched Summary

Poly-β-1,6-N-acetyl-D-glucosamine, better known as Polysaccharide intercellular adhesin (PIA), is a homoglycan of β-1,6-linked 2-acetamido-2-deoxy-β-D-glucopyranosyl residues, of which about 15% are non-N-acetylated. This polymer is central to biofilm accumulation in Staphylococci.

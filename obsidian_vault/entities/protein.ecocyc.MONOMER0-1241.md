## Enriched Summary

AlaB catalyzes a glutamate-pyruvate aminotransferase reaction, generating alanine from pyruvate with glutamate as the amino donor. This activity has been assayed in crude cell extracts, and the gene encoding AlaB had been isolated on a plasmid . Expression of alaB from a multicopy plasmid partially suppresses the growth defect of an ilvE alaA mutant strain . More recently, in an effort to identify the location of the alaB gene in the E. coli genome, the physical map of alaB shown in was compared to that of all eight genes encoding minor alanine aminotransferases. Only one good, but not precise match, to the EG10066 argD gene, was found. The authors also concluded that alaB encodes a minor alanine transaminase which likely has a different primary activity .

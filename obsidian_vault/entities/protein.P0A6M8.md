Elongation factor G (EF-G) is an essential protein that facilitates the translocation of the ribosome by one codon along the mRNA molecule, shifting the resulting deacylated-tRNA from the ribosomal P-site to the E-site and the peptidyl-tRNA from the A-site to the P-site. The activity requires GTP hydrolysis . EF-G is also involved in ribosome disassembly and recycling . Acetylation site K618 was found to be the key site influencing translational elongation rate but not growth rate .

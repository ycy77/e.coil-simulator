## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: 1-Phosphatidyl-1D-myo-inositol 5-phosphate; 1,2-Diacyl-sn-glycero-3-phospho-(1'-myo-inositol-5'-phosphate) EcoCyc common name: a 1-phosphatidyl-1D-myo-inositol 5-phosphate. The phosphorylated derivatives of L-1-phosphatidyl-inositols "phosphatidylinositol" (PtdIns), collectively called phosphoinositides (PIs), are eukaryotic cell membrane phospholipids whose inositol head group is phosphorylated at position D3, D4, or D5, singly or in all combinations, to yield seven PI metabolites. The seven phosphoinositides found in animals include: Phosphatidylinositol monophosphates: CPD-177, CPD-1108 and 1-PHOSPHATIDYL-1D-MYO-INOSITOL-5-PHOSPHA. Phosphatidylinositol bisphophosphates: 1-PHOSPHATIDYL-1D-MYO-INOSITOL-34-BISPH, 1-PHOSPHATIDYL-1D-MYO-INOSITOL-35-BISPH, and PHOSPHATIDYL-MYO-INOSITOL-45-BISPHOSPHA. Phosphatidylinositol trisphophosphate: PHOSPHATIDYLINOSITOL-345-TRIPHOSPHATE. About phosphatidylinositol 5-phosphate (PI5P): PI5P represents only 0.5% of the PI pool present in most cells. However, its levels strongly fluctuate due to external stimuli such as TCR activation, insulin treatment, oxidative stress, and pathogen cellular invasion . Several studies found changes in intracellular PI5P levels during cell cycle progression, as well as upon thrombin treatment and osmotic stress...

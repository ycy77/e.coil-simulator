## Enriched Summary

CPD-17257 trans-Vaccenate is a naturally occurring trans-fatty acid produced by mammals. The name is derived from the Latin vacca (cow) since it is the main trans fatty acid in cow's milk. It is also the predominant trans fatty acid in human milk. CPD-17257 trans-Vaccenate was discovered in 1928 in animal fats and butter . It is formed with a conjugated isomer of linoleate known as CPD-15704 rumenic acid" by biohydrogenation of dietary polyunsaturated fatty acids in the rumen . Another isomer, CPD-9247, is produced from CPD-9245 by bacteria and plants. It is one of only two unsaturated fatty acids in TAX-562 (see PWY-5973). Some plant species such as TAX-48545 and TAX-73871 (Doxantha unguis-cati L.) produce relatively high levels of CPD-9245 and CPD-9247 in their seed oils .

## Enriched Summary

Anaerobic sulfatase-maturating enzyme homolog AslB (AnSME homolog) AslB is a member of the anaerobic sulfatase maturation enzyme subfamily of the Radical SAM superfamily of enzymes . AslB from E. coli BL21(DE3), which is 96% identical to AslB of E. coli K-12 MG1655 (shown here), has been studied experimentally. Co-expression of AslB with recombinant human sulfatases results in increased sulfatase activity . Both an aslB single and aslB ydeM double null mutant is still able to mature a heterologous sulfatase from Clostridium perfringens. Maturation of the C. perfringens enzyme appears to be dependent on a different enzyme that is present only under aerobic conditions . Expression of aslB is activated by the transcriptional regulator Rob .

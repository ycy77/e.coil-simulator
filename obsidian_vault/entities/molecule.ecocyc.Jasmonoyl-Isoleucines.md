## Enriched Summary

Jasmonoyl-isoleucine (JA-Ile) has been detected in Arabidopsis thaliana and a number of other plant species . There are two different naturally occurring stereoisomers of this compound, CPD-11232 and CPD-11259, that can spontaneously interconvert through epimerization . In Arabidopsis thaliana, JA-Ile promotes binding between the COI1 F-box protein and the JAZ1 repressor involved in jasmonic acid-mediated signaling . Therefore, JA-Ile promotes the degradation of JAZ1. Initial studies suggested that CPD-11232 "(-)-JA-Ile" was a biologically active phytohormone . However, later work shows that CPD-11259 is the biologically active form of the compound . This compound exists at low levels in most commercial preparations of CPD-11232 and may explain the observed biological activity originally associated with CPD-11232 "(-)-JA-Ile". A pure preparation of that stereoisomer does not promote JAZ/COI1 binding in vitro . JA-Ile has also been detected in TAX-49451 and plays a role in defense responses but it is unclear which form of JA-Ile is active in this process.

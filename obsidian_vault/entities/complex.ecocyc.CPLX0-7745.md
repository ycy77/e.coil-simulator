## Annotation

MurR "N-acetyl muramic acid repressor," represses genes involved in the catabolism of cell wall sugars . It represses transcription from the murQ promoter and also interferes with its own transcription. murR and murQ are unique and now well-known genes that are regulated by MurR. They are divergently oriented and expressed from nonoverlapping face-to-face (convergent) promoters, and their transcripts are complementary at their 5' ends . MurR is a member of the RpiR/AlsR family of transcriptional regulators, whose members contain two domains, a highly conserved N-terminal HTH domain and a C-terminal SIS (sugar phosphate isomerase/sugar phosphate-binding) domain . It forms a stable multimer (most likely a tetramer) and binds to two adjacent inverted repeats within the murR/murQ operator region, preventing the recruitment of RNA polymerase. It has been demonstrated that MurR forms tetramers in solution . The C-terminal SIS domain of MurR is involved in N-acetylmuramic acid-6-phosphate (MurNAc-6-P) binding, which is the effector molecule of this protein. The binding of this effector to MurR weakens the DNA-binding affinity of MurR, causing an induction of the genes repressed by this protein . Another molecule that is able to bind to MurR is N-acetylglucosamine-6-phosphate (GlcNAc-6-P), but this binding is weaker than the binding of MurNAc-6-P-MurR, and GlcNAc- 6-P is to unable to remove MurR from DNA as MurNAc-6-P does . The crystal structures of the MurR associated with its ligands have revealed the contacts of the protein with such compounds . S146, H171, S190, and S192 are the amino acids of MurR that are highly conserved in the members of the family of transcriptions factors to which the protein belongs .

## Enriched Summary

CPD-15302 Platensimycin (PTM) is a potent and selective inhibitor of the elongation-specific condensing enzymes of type II fatty acid biosynthesis (EC-2.3.1.41 (FabB) and EC-2.3.1.179 (FabF)). The compound, produced by TAX-58346, was discovered in 2006 by researchers at Merck, using an antisense differential sensitivity screening focused on selective inhibitors of fatty acid biosynthesis . PTM interacts specifically with the acyl-enzyme intermediate of the FabF enzyme. Crystalographic studies have shown that it binds in the malonyl subsite of FabF, with its benzoic acid ring positioned to interact with important residues of the catalytic machinery. Because of its unique mode of action, platensimycin is efficient against notorious antibiotic-resistant strains such as methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA), vancomycin-intermediate S. aureus, and vancomycin-resistant enterococci. ## Annotation

CPD-15302 Platensimycin (PTM) is a potent and selective inhibitor of the elongation-specific condensing enzymes of type II fatty acid biosynthesis (EC-2.3.1.41 (FabB) and EC-2.3.1.179 (FabF)). The compound, produced by TAX-58346, was discovered in 2006 by researchers at Merck, using an antisense differential sensitivity screening focused on selective inhibitors of fatty acid biosynthesis . PTM interacts specifically with the acyl-enzyme intermediate of the FabF enzyme. Crystalographic studies have shown that it binds in the malonyl subsite of FabF, with its benzoic acid ring positioned to interact with important residues of the catalytic machinery. Because of its unique mode of action, platensimycin is efficient against notorious antibiotic-resistant strains such as methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA), vancomycin-intermediate S. aureus, and vancomycin-resistant enterococci. PTM is not active against wild-type TAX-562 due to efflux mechanisms, has no antifungal activity, and has low mammalian cell toxicity . The utility of CPD-15302 as a drug is hampered by poor pharmacokinetic properties and negligible oral bioavailability. Clinical trials have been delayed pending the development of chemical analogues which have more favorable drug-like properties .

## Enriched Summary

Cas1 and Cas2 are the only proteins universally conserved between CRISPR-Cas systems and form a stable complex that is required for spacer acquisition . Cas1 and Cas2 together are required for efficient adaptation of the CRISPR array. Induced expression of Cas1 and Cas2 leads to acquisition of new spacer elements in the CRISPR repeat . Cas2 homologs in other organisms were shown to have sequence-specific endoribonuclease activity . Site-directed mutagenesis of the signature catalytic residue E9 does not abolish spacer acquisition, indicating that Cas2 plays a nonenzymatic role. Conversely, deletion of the C-terminal region that interacts with Cas1 prevents spacer acquisition .

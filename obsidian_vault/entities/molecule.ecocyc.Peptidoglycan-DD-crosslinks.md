## Enriched Summary

Transpeptidases cross link glycan strands within newly synthesized peptidoglycan (PG) polymers and between newly made material and preexisting peptidoglycan; they also link peptidoglycan to Braun's lipoprotein (Lpp) in the outer membrane and catalyze the exchange of carboxy terminal D-alanine residues with glycine in the peptide stem. D,D-transpeptidases generate 4-3 cross-links, typically between the D-alanine at position 4 of a donor peptide stem and the meso-diaminopimelyl or L-lysyl residue at position 3 of an acceptor peptide stem; L,D-transpeptidases generate 3-3 cross-links from PG tetrapeptide substrates (see ).

## Enriched Summary

RutR regulates genes directly or indirectly involved in the complex pathway of pyrimidine metabolism and also plays a role in regulation of the genes for degradation of purines and genes for maintenance of pH homeostasis . Inhibition of rutR expression by CRISPRi reduced cellular fitness under treatment with the antibiotic pyocyanin . This protein belongs to the TetR family, whose members have an N-terminal DNA-binding domain that contains a helix-turn-helix motif and a C-terminal domain for dimerization and ligand binding . RutR appears to bind DNA as a dimer . RutR is acetylated at lysines K7, K11, K52, and K62 through two mechanisms: non-enzymatic and enzymatic reactions by the enzymes G7350-MONOMER PatZ/YfiQ and EG12270-MONOMER YiaC . RutR recognizes and binds a 16-bp (7-2-7) inverted repeat consensus sequence , where the central A/T nucleotide in each half-site appears to be the most important for the site . This binding is inhibited when uracil and thymine bind to RutR, although the effect is greater for uracil than for thymine . Based on the crystal structure of an unliganded mutant RutR protein, a structural basis for the use of uracil as the sole effector molecule was determined . RutR shows a binding site that is rich in aromatic and hydrophobic residues .

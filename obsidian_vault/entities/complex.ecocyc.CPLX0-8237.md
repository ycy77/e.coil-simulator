## Enriched Summary

The RusA protein is an endonuclease that functions as a Holliday junction resolvase . Resolution of synthetic X junctions by dual strand incision occurs predominantly 5' of symmetrically located CC dinucleotides ; optimal activity at pH 7.5 requires the presence of Mn2+ . RusA binding alters the global structure of DNA junctions . Binding selectivity of RusA for specific DNA structures has been investigated . Although strand cleavage by the individual subunits of the RusA homodimer can be uncoupled in vitro, efficient cleavage in vivo requires simultaneous interactions of both subunits and is highly specific for Holliday junction intermediates . A D70N mutant binds normally to DNA junctions, but is catalytically inactive .

## Enriched Summary

The Jasmonates jasmonates are a group of important phytohormones, structurally similar to animal Prostaglandins prostaglandins and ubiquitous in the plant kingdom. Many forms exist, based on the structure of Jasmonic-Acids "Jasmonate" (jasmonic acid, JA). The jasmonates act as regulatory molecules in many developmental processes that include fertility, sex determination, root elongation and fruit ripening. They are also potent signals activating plant defences against pathogens, herbivory, wounding and abiotic stress. The jasmonate regulation system includes the COI1 receptor protein and several JAZ (JAsmonate ZIM domain) repressors, which, in the absence of jasmonate, are bound to the MYC2 transcription factors, preventing transcription. In the presence of active jasmonate molecules, COI1 interacts with the JAZ repressors, causing their PWY-7511 ubiquitylation. ## Annotation

The Jasmonates jasmonates are a group of important phytohormones, structurally similar to animal Prostaglandins prostaglandins and ubiquitous in the plant kingdom. Many forms exist, based on the structure of Jasmonic-Acids "Jasmonate" (jasmonic acid, JA). The jasmonates act as regulatory molecules in many developmental processes that include fertility, sex determination, root elongation and fruit ripening. They are also potent signals activating plant defences against pathogens, herbivory, wounding and abiotic stress. The jasmonate regulation system includes the COI1 receptor protein and several JAZ (JAsmonate ZIM domain) repressors, which, in the absence of jasmonate, are bound to the MYC2 transcription factors, preventing transcription. In the presence of active jasmonate molecules, COI1 interacts with the JAZ repressors, causing their PWY-7511 ubiquitylation. The ubiquitylated repressors are targeted for proteasome degradation, releasing the MYC2 transcription factors and activating transcription . Only two of the four stereoisomers of jasmonate occur naturally: CPD-731 and CPD-734 . These two forms are present in equilibrium within cells and can interconvert spontaneously through epimerization . The exact ratio of these stereoisomers within a cell has not been conclusively determined, and it may vary based on species, cell type, and physiological state. But, in the systems tested so far, CPD-734 is found at higher levels than CPD-731 . The biological activity of these stereoisomers can be quite different . In addition, enzymes involved in JA metabolism may have different preferences for the different stereoisomers .

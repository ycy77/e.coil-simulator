## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Involved in the biosynthesis of osmoregulated periplasmic glucans (OPGs). OpgH is a multifunctional inner membrane protein. OpgH, along with the periplasmic protein OpgG, is required for synthesis of the periplasmic oligosaccharides known as CPD-16398 osmoregulated periplasmic glucans (OPGs), formerly called membrane-derived oligosaccharides (MDOs); although exact details remain unclear, an OpgGH complex is suggested to function for synthesis and translocation of the polyglucose backbone structure of OPG in a process requiring CPD-12575 UDP-glucose and EG50003-MONOMER acyl carrier protein (and see ). OpgH also functions separately and individually as a UDP-glucose-activated inhibitor of cell division; under rich nutrient conditions, OpgH localizes to the division site by an unknown mechanism; OpgH interacts physically with EG10347-MONOMER FtsZ to inhibit cell division . OpgH is an inner membrane protein with 8 transmembrane domains; the N and C-termini are located in the cytoplasm, as is a third domain between the second and third transmembrane regions ; this latter cytoplasmic domain contains sequence features characteristic of inverting glycosyltransferases.

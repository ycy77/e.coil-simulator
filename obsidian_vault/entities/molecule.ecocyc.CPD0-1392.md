## Enriched Summary

CPD0-1392 Cisplatin is a chemotherapy medication used to treat a number of cancers including testicular cancer, ovarian cancer, cervical cancer, breast cancer, bladder cancer, head and neck cancer, esophageal cancer, lung cancer, mesothelioma, brain tumors and neuroblastoma. Following administration, one of the two of the chlorine atoms are slowly displaced by water to give the aquo complex CPD-21200. In the presence of DNA, the water molecule in CPD-21200 is easily displaced by the N-heterocyclic bases, particularly GUANINE. Subsequent displacement of the other chloride, typically by another guanine, results in crsslinking of the DNA. The damaged DNA elicits DNA repair mechanisms, and when repair proves impossible, leads to apoptosis. Cisplatin tends to preferentially kill the fastest proliferating cells, which presumably are cancer cells. CPD0-1392 Cisplatin is degraded via the PWY-4061 "glutathione-mediated detoxification" pathway .

## Enriched Summary

Agarans Agarans and Agaroses agaroses are sulfated polysaccharides that are extracted from sea weeds belonging to the TAX-2763 (red algae), mainly from TAX-2769, and species from the TAX-31444 and TAX-33664 genera. The basic units of Agarans agarans are repeats of β -1,3 linked D-galactose and α-1,4- linked L-galactose units, which are substituted by sulfate. Different types of agarans have sulfate groups at different positions (depicted as R3 in the structure) ( . In Agaroses agaroses the α-1,4- linked galactose units are modified by having a 3,6-anhydro bridge. These bridges are responsible for the gelation that is typical to these compounds, by forming a double helix during the process.

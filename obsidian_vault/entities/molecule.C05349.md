## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: Ciprofloxacin Ciprofloxacin is a fluoroquinolone antibiotic used to treat a number of bacterial infections including bone and joint infections, intra abdominal infections, certain types of infectious diarrhea, respiratory tract infections, skin infections, typhoid fever, and urinary tract infections, among others. It is on the World Health Organization's List of Essential Medicines. Ciprofloxacin was developed by Bayer and patented in 1980, following up on the success of CPD-21015. While it contains only one more carbon atom than CPD-21015 , ciprofloxacin is 2- to 10-fold more potent against most strains of Gram-negative bacteria. Ciprofloxacin was approved in 1987, just one year after the approval of norfloxacin .

## Enriched Summary

Ceramides are found in high concentrations within the cell membrane and are one of the component lipids that make up Sphingomyelins sphingomyelin, one of the major lipids in the lipid bilayer. This type of ceramide, containing an additional double bond at position 8 of the sphingoid base, is a common modification in plants and fungi . In plants, there is commonly a mixture of ceramides with the double bond at position 8 in both cis and trans configurations, whereas the double bond at the 4 position is generally in the trans configuration . In the diatom TAX-35128 both bonds are in the trans configuration .

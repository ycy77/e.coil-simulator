## Enriched Summary

Linalools Linalool (3,7-dimethyl-1,6-octadien-3-ol) is an acyclic monoterpene alcohol with a pleasant fragrance associated with a diverse array of plants . It is found in lavender, bergamot, jasmine, basil, rosemary, sage, star-anise, cinnamon, clove, nutmeg, coriander, cardamom, ginger, black pepper and mandarin, and is one of the three terpene compounds principally responsible for Muscat aroma. Linalool is a primary compound in floral scent research. Both enantiomeric forms, CPD-8996 and CPD-8997, occur in nature. Linalool is involved as an attractant in plant interactions with pollinators, herbivores and parasitoids . It has also been shown to be induced by artificial wounding and by jasmonic acid . The de novo synthesis of linalool has been demonstrated in floral tissues , barks and roots . Junipers, dicots and monocots emit linalool along with other monoterpenoids . Even though fungi typically do not produce monoterpenes, the de novo synthesis of linalool was demonstrate in some fungi, including the wine yeast strain, Saccharomyces cerevisiae M522, in the absence of grape derived precursors .

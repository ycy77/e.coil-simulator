## Enriched Summary

The Tol-Pal complex is important for maintaining outer membrane integrity via lipid homeostasis and this may represent its primary role . Early work (for example ) implicated the Tol-Pal proteins in phage infection and colicin uptake and this has since been widely investigated (see ). The Tol-Pal proteins are encoded within a seven gene cluster - ybgC, tolQ, tolR, tolA, tolB, pal, cpoB - expressed from two promoters, one located upstream of ybgC and the other upstream of tolB . TolQ, TolR and TolA are inner membrane proteins ; TolB is a periplasmic protein and Pal is a peptidoglycan associated outer membrane lipoprotein . ## Annotation

The Tol-Pal system is a group of 5 interacting proteins which span the cell envelope and play a role in outer membrane invagination and septal peptidoglycan processing during cell division (and see ). The Tol-Pal complex is important for maintaining outer membrane integrity via lipid homeostasis and this may represent its primary role . Early work (for example ) implicated the Tol-Pal proteins in phage infection and colicin uptake and this has since been widely investigated (see ). The Tol-Pal proteins are encoded within a seven gene cluster - ybgC, tolQ, tolR, tolA, tolB, pal, cpoB - expressed from two promoters, one located upstream of ybgC and the other upstream of tolB . TolQ, TolR and TolA are inner membrane proteins ; TolB is a periplasmic protein and Pal is a peptidoglycan associated outer membrane lipoprotein . Interactions between the Tol-Pal proteins have been characterised both in vivo and in vitro (see individual proteins for details). suggest that these interactions result in the formation of a complex that links the inner and outer membranes. The Tol proteins may constitute an energy transducing system (reviewed in ) and a speculative mechanism for Tol-Pal force transduction through the cell envelope has been described (and further ). TolQ and TolR share structural and functional homologies with ExbB and ExbD of the TonB system as well as with MotA and MotB of the flagellar motor system, both of which are ion potential-driven molecular motors . TolA conformation and interaction with Pal is dependent on the proton motive force; TolQR may constitute an ion channel for proton movement . TolQR provides the energy for correct localization of Pal during the cell cycle (and further ). The Tol-Pal system has been implicated in maintaining outer membrane stability.

## Enriched Summary

Gellan is a water-soluble polysaccharide produced by the bacterium TAX-179878. The repeating unit of the polymer is the tetrasaccharide [β-D-Glc(1→4)-βD-GlcA(1→4)-β-D-Glc(1→4)-α-L-Rha(1→3)]. Gellan is primarily used in microbiological cultures as a gelling agent, alternative to agar. It is also used as a food additive, as a thickener, emulsifier, and stabilizer.

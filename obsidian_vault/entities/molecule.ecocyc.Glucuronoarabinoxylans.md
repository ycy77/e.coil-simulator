## Enriched Summary

Glucuronoarabinoxylans Glucuronoarabinoxylans (GAX) and Arabinoglucuronoxylans arabinoglucuronoxylans (AGX) are components of Hemicelluloses that have a similar structure - a β(1→4)-D-xylopyranose backbone with single 4-O-methylglucuronate and α-L-arabinofuranose residues attached at position 2 and 3, respectively. However, GAX-type polymers consist of Arabinoxylan backbone that contains about ten times more α-L-arabinofuranose side chains than glucuronate ones, while AGX-type polymers are Glucuronoxylans with a small proportion of the xylopyranose residues of the backbone disubstituted by two α-L-arabinofuranose residues . Another difference is that GAX contain some xylopyranose units that are doubly substituted by both 4-O-methylglucuronate and α-L-arabinofuranose . Unlike glucuronoxylans, glucuronoarabinoxylans are not acetylated . Some of the arabinofuranose side chains in GAX are further substitued by either a β-D-xylopyranose attached by a (1→2) linkage or by a second arabinofuranose attached by a (1→3) linkage.

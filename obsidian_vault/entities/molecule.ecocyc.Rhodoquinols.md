## Enriched Summary

Rhodoquinone (RQ) is a modified ubiquinone, where the methoxyl group in position 3 is replaced by an amino group. The compound was originally isolated from TAX-1085 , where is it used for the reduction of fumarate to succinate under anaerobic conditions. The rhodoquinone/rhodoquinol standard redox potential is substantially lower than that of ubiquinone/ubiquinol/UQH2 (-63 vs. +110 mV). The lower redox potential drives the succinate dehydrogenase complex (complex II) in reverse to function as a fumarate reductase. Rhodoquinone is not limited to prokaryotes. It was soon discovered that some eukaryotic parasites such as the nematodes TAX-6253 and TAX-6252 , as well as parasitic helminths also contain rhodoquinone, which is essential for fumarate reduction under anaerobic conditions . Later it was shown that rhodoquinone is present in all eukaryotes that reduce fumarate during anoxia, including freshwater snails, mussels, lugworms, and oysters . More recently, rhodoquinone has been detected in mitochondria purified from certain mouse and human tissues .

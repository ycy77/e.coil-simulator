## Enriched Summary

Oxc is the E. coli ortholog of the oxalyl-CoA decarboxylase from Oxalobacter formigenes. The enzyme has oxalyl-CoA decarboxylase activity and may be used for oxalate detoxification . Crystal structures of the apoenzyme and in complex with ADP or the substrate analog acetyl-CoA have been solved. The crystal structure indicates that the enzyme is a tetramer assembled as a dimer of dimers . Oxc is required during the adaption phase of an oxalate-induced acid tolerance response .

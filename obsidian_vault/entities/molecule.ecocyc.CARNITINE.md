## Enriched Summary

CARNITINE is a zwitterionic, quaternary amine carboxylate that has essential and diverse roles in intermediary metabolism in all living kingdoms. Carnitine was first discovered in 1905 in muscle extracts, and the name is derived from the Latin word for flesh, Carnis . In 1962 it was found that the physiological enantiomer is CARNITINE, and since then the widespread distribution of L-carnitine has been established in higher and lower eukaryotes, plants, and many microorganisms . In animals and yeast carnitine is best known for its contribution to fatty acid intracellular trafficking . In plants L-carnitine is also associated with fatty acid metabolism, although its exact function is less well understood . Bacteria are not able to synthesize carnitine do novo, but are able to import the compound and use it as an osmoprotectant or to enhance thermotolerance, cryotolerance and barotolerance .

## Enriched Summary

A carboxyamide is a compound with the general formula RC(=O)NR'R", where R, R', and R" represent organic groups or hydrogen atoms. It is often formed as a derivative of a carboxylic acid ,where the hydroxyl group is replaced by an amine group. The amide core group -C(=O)N- is called a peptide bond when it is part of the main chain of a protein, and an isopeptide bond when it occurs in a side chain, such as in the amino acids ASN and GLN. According to IUPAC, amides with NH2, -NHR, or -NRR' groups, where R and R' are groups other than hydrogen, should not be distinguished by means of the terms primary, secondary, and tertiary.

## Enriched Summary

Retinoids are a group of compounds that are related to vitamin A, including all-trans forms such as CPD-13524, RETINAL and RETINOATE, cis forms such as CPD-882, CPD-881, CPD-13549 and CPD-13548, and Retinyl-Esters "retinyl esters" such as CHOCOLA_A. Retinoids have many important and diverse functions throughout the body including roles in vision, regulation of cell proliferation and differentiation, growth of bone tissue, immune function, and activation of tumor suppressor genes. Structurally, all retinoids possess a β-ionone ring and a polyunsaturated side chain, with either an alcohol, aldehyde, a carboxylic acid group or an ester group. The side chain is composed of four isoprenoid units, with a series of conjugated double bonds which may exist in trans- or cis-configuration.

## Enriched Summary

Mating in TAX-4932 results from the fusion of two haploid cells of opposite mating type, a and α, to form a diploid. The mating pheromones, CPD-10692 and Alpha-factor, produced by a and α haploid cells, respectively, stimulate receptors on cells of the opposite mating type to initiate responses that lead to cell fusion. The molecular structure of the α factor has been investigated. Four different oligopeptides exhibiting α factor activity have been isolated . These peptides, designated as α1, α2, α3, and α4 are structurally closely related, being composed of thirteen (α1 and α3) or twelve (α2 and α4) amino acids, respectively. The α3 and α4 peptides are oxidation products, containing an internal L-Methionine-sulfoxides instead of MET. The peptides were found to be composed of the following amino acids: 2 glutamate or glutamine, 2 proline, 1 glycine, methionine or methionine sulfoxide, 2 leucine, 1 tyrosine, 1 lysine, 1 histidine, 1 or 2 tryptophan. TYR is the C-terminal amino acid in all four peptides. The tridekapeptides differ from the dodekapeptides by an additional N-terminal TRP residue.

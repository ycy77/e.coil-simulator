## Enriched Summary

LysW is an amino-group-carrier protein used by many archaeal species for the synthesis of the amino acid LYS. The amino group of CPD-468, an intermediate in the PWY-3081 "archaeal L-lysine biosynthetic pathway" (which is also found in the hyperthermophilic bacterium TAX-274), is covalently linked to the γ-carboxyl group of the last C-terminal residue of LysW (Glu54 in TAX-274) by a dedicated enzyme encoded by the G-5207 gene. LysW not only protects the amino group of CPD-468, but also serves as a carrier protein, which is easily recognized by lysine biosynthesis enzymes. In this way all subsequent transformations leading to L-lysine occur when the amino acid is attached to LysW, and the CPD-468 side chain is converted to a C-terminal lysyl side chain in the LysW-conjugated form. Eventually, a dedicated enzyme encoded by the G-5222 gene releases the mature amino acid . It has been shown that in some species, such as members of the TAX-2284 genus and TAX-311400 LysW is also used as a carrier protein for GLT during the PWY-7400 "biosynthesis of L-arginine".

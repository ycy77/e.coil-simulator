## Annotation

ZraP is an accessory protein and modulator of the PWY0-1498 "Zra signaling pathway" - an envelope stress response pathway that contributes to antibiotic resistance . ZraP is a zinc responsive, periplasmic protein with chaperone activity . ZraP does not have a direct role in zinc tolerance . Purified ZraP binds Zn2+, Co2+, Ni2+ and Cu2+; it does not bind Cd2+ or Mn2+ ; purified ZraP binds Cu2+ with higher affinity than Zn2+ (see also ). Purified ZraP is able to protect malate dehydrogenase from denaturation at 45°C and pH 8.5 . ZraP is the main zinc containing protein under zinc stress conditions (growth in the presence of 0.24 mM 69mZn) . zraP has two zinc-binding motifs . Purified ZraP forms a tetramer of dimers; dimers are formed through a disulphide bridge involving cysteine residue 104; 4 copper ions bind per ZraP octomer . The Cys-104 residue is at least partially reduced in vivo . Heterologous expression of a putative zinc dependent transcriptional regulator from Proteus mirabilis in E. coli results in increased production of ZraP that corresponds with greater zinc tolerance, compared to wild type . Under conditions of envelope stress (treatment with benzethonium chloride, polymyxin B, or mecillinam), a ZraP deletion mutant (ΔzraP) shows enhanced membrane disruption and abnormal cell shape as compared to wild-type; deletion of zraP impacts resistance to a variety of antibiotics and zraP expression is induced by the antimicrobial compounds (benzethonium chloride, polymyxin B, tetracycline and lomefloxacin) to which ZraP deletion mutants are more sensitive; deletion of zraP confers sensitivity to acidic pH, the presence of bile salts and osmotic shock .

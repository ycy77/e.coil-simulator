## Enriched Summary

TusA TusA is a sulfur/thiosulfate-carrier protein involved in many different functions, depending on the organism. In the Gram-negative bacterium TAX-562 TusA has a well documented function as a sulfurtransferase in tRNA modification and molybdenum cofactor biosynthesis . In organisms that store sulfur globules such as TAX-1049 TusA functions in sulfur transfter during the globules degradation. It accepts a sulfur atom from a rhodanese-like protein and transfers it to DsrE-Protein . In the acidothermophilic sulfur- and tetrathionate-oxidizing archaeon TAX-1006005 TusA is also involved in thiosulfate transfer, accepting a thiosulfate group from MONOMER-19176 .

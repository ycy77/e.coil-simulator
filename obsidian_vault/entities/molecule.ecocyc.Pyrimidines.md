## Enriched Summary

PYRIMIDINE-RING Pyrimidine is a heterocyclic aromatic compound similar to benzene, containing two nitrogen atoms at positions 1 and 3 of the ring. The name pyrimidines is also used for compounds derived from the basic pyrimidine compound, including Pyrimidine-Bases "three of the bases" found in nucleic acids, namely CYTOSINE, THYMINE, and URACIL. This class stands for all compounds that have a pyrimidine as part of their structure.

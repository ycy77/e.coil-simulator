## Enriched Summary

An aromatic compound contains one or more aromatic rings (an aromatic ring is a planar ring in which every atom must have an occupied p orbital, which overlaps with p orbitals on either side). The pi orbitals of the ring's atoms form a conjugated system and are in resonance, giving increased stability compared to saturated compounds having a similar structure. As a result aromatic rings are very stable and do not break apart easily. The least complex aromatic hydrocarbon is benzene. The name stems from a time when compounds were characterized by their smell rather than their chemical structure. Similar structures in which at least one of the ring's carbon atoms is replaced by one of the heteroatoms oxygen, nitrogen, or sulfur, are called heteroarenes.

## Enriched Summary

The sLea-Antigen "sialyl-Lewis a epitope" (SLea) was discovered in 1979 with the use of a monoclonal antibody . While the antigen is believed to play an important role during embryogenesis , in adults only low amounts of the antigen are present on normal epithelial cells . When the selectin family of adhesion molecules was described, it was found that SLea, along with the sLex-Antigen "sialyl-Lewis x antigen", is a ligand for E-selectin. Higher expression of the SLea antigen in adults is strongly associated with cancer. It was found in high amounts on the surface of human pancreatic, colon and gastric cancer cell lines and in human adenocarcinomas of the colon, stomach, pancreas and gall-bladder . The expression of the antigen has been linked to tumor progression. A gradual increase in SLea was found in colon and rectum during neoplastic transformation and progression , and its presence was restricted to tumorigenic and invasive human urothelial cell lines .

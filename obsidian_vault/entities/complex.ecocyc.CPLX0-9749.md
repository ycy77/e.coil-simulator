## Enriched Summary

An isolated, purified YdiJ was found to be a metalloprotein that contains FAD as well as a 4Fe-4S cluster and shares similarity to a nonessential protein of Pseudomonas putida . YdiJ has been found to belong to a novel family of D-2-hydroxyglutarate dehydrogenases that metabolise R-2-HYDROXYGLUTARATE (D-2-hydroxyglutarate, D-2-HGA) to 2-KETOGLUTARATE. The enzyme has strict substrate specificity to D-2-HGA. Inactivation of the ydiJ gene results in accumulation of D-2-HGA in culture media. It is suggested that YdiJ is involved in recycling the D-2-HGA that is generated by EG10944 during SERSYN-PWY-1; the primary electron acceptor during oxidation of D-2-HGA is not known . A meta-analysis of E. coli K-12 MG1655 transcriptomic data sets in response to multiple stressors found 15 uncharacterized or partially characterized genes (yqfA, yhfG, yhhA, ybgS, arpA, yqaE, yfdY, yaiY, yebE, ydiE, yjcB, yiiX, ycjF, yihF, yidX) that were upregulated under all five stresses: heat, cold, oxidative stress, nitrosative stress, and antibiotic treatment, whereas 3 uncharacterized or partially characterized genes (ymfI, ydiJ, yedE) that were downregulated under all but nitrosative stress .

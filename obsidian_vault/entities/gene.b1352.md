## Enriched Summary

kilR (gene.b1352) is a gene entity. It encodes kilR (protein.P38393). Encoded protein function: FUNCTION: A Rac cryptic prophage-encoded toxic protein, which contributes to host survival during oxidative stress conditions (PubMed:29237698, PubMed:40778775). Dual inhibitor that affects cell division and cytoskeletal organization (PubMed:40778775, PubMed:8752325). Low levels of expression lead to cell filamentation, indicating the inhibition of cell division, and high levels of expression result in rod-shaped defects (PubMed:40778775, PubMed:8752325). Acts by perturbing the Z-ring formation and the MreB localization, affecting FtsZ-mediated cell division and MreB-mediated cell elongation (PubMed:40778775). This dual inhibition activity contributes to the high level of toxicity of KilR and to its function in SOS-independent DNA damage tolerance during oxidative stress (PubMed:40778775). Division inhibition by KilR can be relieved by overexpression of the cell division protein FtsZ (PubMed:8752325). However, overexpression of MreBCD cannot suppress KilR toxicity, even though it reduces the cell rounding effects associated with KilR expression (PubMed:40778775). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:29237698, ECO:0000269|PubMed:40778775, ECO:0000269|PubMed:8752325}.

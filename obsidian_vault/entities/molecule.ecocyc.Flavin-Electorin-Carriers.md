## Enriched Summary

Flavin compounds are based on the tricyclic heterocycle CPD-23399 structure. They are capable of undergoing oxidation-reduction reactions, and can accept either one electron in a two-step process or two electrons at once. Reduction is made with the addition of hydrogen atoms to specific nitrogen atoms on the isoalloxazine ring system. Flavin compounds participate in many reactions as electron acceptors or donors, and some of the compounds (particularly FAD and FMN) can also serve as enzyme cofactors. Enzymes that contain flavin cofactors are often referred to as flavoenzymes or flavoproteins. The flavin compounds are synthesized from GTP, via the precursor RIBOFLAVIN.

## Enriched Summary

Hypoxanthine phosphoribosyltransferase is a purine salvage enzyme which catalyzes the transfer of the ribosyl-5-phosphate group from PRPP to the N9 position of hypoxanthine to yield IMP . It can also act on guanine , and this activity appears to be sufficient to salvage guanine for guanine nucleotide synthesis in the absence of gpt . The enzymatic activity is inhibited by IMP ; ppGpp was shown to bind to the enzyme , but it appears that no biochemical data showing inhibition of the purified enzyme by ppGpp has been published. Hpt shares only 23% amino acid sequence identity with that of E. coli Gpt, despite their common function . This enzyme is one of three E. coli phosphoribosyltransferases (PRTases) that participate in purine salvage, Gpt, Hpt and Apt . Crystal structures of free and product-bound forms of the enzyme have been solved . A V157A Y173H double mutant generated by directed evolution shows promiscuous substrate specificity . Nucleoside monophosphate product analogs that inhibit the enzyme have been identified . An hpt mutant is resistant to 6-mercaptopurine . An hpt gpt double mutant strain is extremely sensitive to inhibition by adenine due to the role of guanine phosphoribosyltransferase in converting free guanine to GMP when the cell is depleted for guanine nucleotides...

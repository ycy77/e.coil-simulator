## Enriched Summary

CPD-19766 "Factor 430" (often referred to as coenzyme F430) is a nickel containing tetrapyrrole cofactor that is present in all methanogenic bacteria. It was initially observed in 1977 by Jean LeGall in extracts of TAX-145262. The name factor 430 (F430) was coined by Gunsalus and Wolfe who reported the absorbance maximumat 430 nm . The compound belongs to the corphins, a class of compounds that combine structural elements of both Corrins corrins and Porphyrins porphyrins . In 1982 Ellefson et al showed that CPD-19766 is a cofactor of the enzyme EC-2.8.4.1 (methyl-coenzyme M reductase) . This enzyme catalyzes the final step of methane production in all methanogenic bacteria: the reduction of the methyl group in Me-CoM to CH4. F430 exists in two distinct intracellular pools: either bound to EC 2.8.4.1 or free of protein in the cytosol. Early literature on F430 was confusing due to reports of heterogeneity in F430 samples that depended on the isolation and purification procedures used in different laboratories. Later work showed that F430 exists primarily in two isomeric forms. These forms differ in the relative stereochemical disposition of acid side chains at positions 12 and 13 .

## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: N-Glycoloyl-neuraminate; N-Glycolylneuraminate; Neu5Gc EcoCyc common name: N-glycolylneuraminate. CPD-273 (Neu5Gc) is a modified sialic acid. It is abundant in many species of the Deuterostome lineage, including simians, mice, rats, cows, pigs, and sheep, but is nearly absent from birds and reptiles . In cats, Neu5Gc distinguishes the blood groups A and B . Humans cannot synthesize Neu5Gc due to an irreversible inactivation of the CMAH gene on chromosome 6p21.32 encoding EC-3.1.4.40 . Despite the lack of this enzyme, Neu5Gc is found in certain tumors and in fetuses, suggesting the existence of an alternative pathway . As a component of glycoconjugates, Neu5Gc is known as the Hanganutziu-Deicher antigen .

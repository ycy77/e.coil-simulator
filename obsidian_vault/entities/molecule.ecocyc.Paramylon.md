## Enriched Summary

Paramylon is a storage glucan produced by Euglenoids, discovered in 1850 . It is a high-molecular-weight (between 100 and 500 kDa) linear polysaccharide made up of glucose units connected by β(1->3) linkages . It contains no more than 700 polymerized glucose molecules and exhibits a high degree of crystallinity (about 90 %) in its natural state . Paramylon molecules are arranged as an intermolecular triple helix forming microfibrils, which in turn make up fibres. Rectangular and wedge-shaped segments consisting of these fibres are arranged to form granules of different shapes throughout the cytoplasm, called paramylon bodies. The granules are surrounded by a biomembrane and show an unusual high degree of crystallinity. The paramylon bodies are often visible as colorless or white particles in light microscopy . Grown under optimal conditions, TAX-3039 can accumulate paramylon to approximately 80% of its dry weight . Under anaerobic conditions paramylon is promptly degraded and converted to Wax-Esters "wax esters" composed of saturated fatty acids and alcohols with chain lengths of 10-18. The major constituents are CPD-7836 and CPD-7875 . This phenomenon, which is named PWY-5885 "wax ester fermentation", provides the cells with ATP. The wax esters, which are insoluble in water, accumulate in the cells, and are converted back to paramylon when aerobic conditions return...

hsrA (gene.b3754) is a gene entity. It encodes hsrA (protein.P31474). Encoded protein function: Probable transport protein HsrA (High-copy suppressor of rspA) EcoCyc product frame: YIEO-MONOMER. EcoCyc synonyms: yieO. Genomic coordinates: 3939185-3940612. EcoCyc protein note: The HsrA protein is an uncharacterised member of the Drug:H+ Antiporter-2 (14 Spanner) (DHA2) Family within the Major Facilitator Superfamily (MFS) of transporters . Overexpression of cloned hsrA in a K-12 strain which lacks the major efflux pump permease AcrB (E. coli KAM3; see ) does not impact resistance to a range of toxic compounds (dyes, detergents, antibiotics and others) compared to the mutant parent . Potential substrate candidates identified using an untargeted metabolomics approach include SER serine, 2-HYDROXYGLUTARIC_ACID hydroxyglutarate, OROTATE, and others . Overexpression of hsrA relieves the effect of overexpression of rspA on sigmaS expression. HsrA is not a repressor of rspA expression or a direct activator of sigmaS expression .] hsrA (yieO) is one of 35 efflux-encoding genes that have been deleted or inactivated in 'Efflux KnockOut-35' (EKO-35) - a K-12 BW25133-derived strain that lacks 35 efflux pumps and can be used as a platform to study their function .

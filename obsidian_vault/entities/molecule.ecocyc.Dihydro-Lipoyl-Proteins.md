## Enriched Summary

LIPOIC-ACID "Lipoate" is an organosulfur compound that is used as an essential cofactor by many enzyme complexes. The functional group of lipoate is a ditholane ring, which is a cyclic disulfide. The enzyme complexes that require lipoate have a dedicated Lipoyl-Protein "lipoyl domain", and lipoate is actually produced on this domain. The precursor CPD-195 molecule is first attached to a specific LYS residue within the lipoyl domain (producing Octanoylated-domains), and is then converted to lipoate by the enzyme CPLX0-782, resulting in Lipoyl-Protein-N6-lipoyllysine. CPLX0-782 "Lipoate synthase" not only catalyzes the reaction, but is also the source of the two sulfur atoms.

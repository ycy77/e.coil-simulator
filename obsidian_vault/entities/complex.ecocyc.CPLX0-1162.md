## Enriched Summary

HslV is the peptidase component of the HslVU protease, which is composed of HslU and HslV . This ATP-stimulated protease exhibits activity similar to that of the chymotrypsin-like activity of the eukaryotic proteasome . HslV exhibits weak peptidase activity in the absence of HslU . HslV alone hydrolyzed tested proteins at approximately 5% of the rate observed in the presence of HslU . HslV alone can degrade some, but not all, unfolded proteins at varying rates . The HslVU protease plays a role in clearing the defective peptides produced in the presence of puromycin . HslVU is capable of filling the role of the Lon protease under some conditions . HslVU degrades the Lon substrate SulA and exhibits activity toward DnaA204 mutant protein . Crystal structures of HslV and of the HslVU complex are presented . HslU and HslV form ring shaped complexes of protein hexamers that stack into a four-ring cylinder with HslU rings on each end and HslV rings in the center .

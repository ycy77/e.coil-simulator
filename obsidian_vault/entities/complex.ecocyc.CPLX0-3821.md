## Enriched Summary

HypA and HypB form a heterodimer . The GDP-bound form of HypB promotes formation of the HypA-HypB complex . Within the HypA-HypB complex, the Ni2+ ion migrates from the GTPase domain binding site in HypB to HypA . Ni2+ transfer from HypB to HypA involves modification of the nickel coordination environment of HypA by HypB . When HypA is fully loaded with Ni2+, the complex dissociates . In competitive binding experiments, SlyD is able to block the interaction of HypB with HypA; thus, it appears that a hierarchy of binary protein-protein interactions supports the nickel delivery process .

## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: Capsular polysaccharide EcoCyc common name: a capsular polysaccharide. Polysaccharide capsules are ubiquitous structures found on the cell surface of a broad range of bacterial species. They are thick, mucous-like, layers of polysaccharides that form a cap around the bacterial surface.They are an important factor in the virulence of many animal and plant pathogens, where they protect the bacteria from opsonophagocytosis and complement-mediated killing by the host. Capsular polysaccharides can be linear or branched and consist of regularly repeating subunits of one to six monosaccharides. They have molecular weights on the order of 100-2000 kDa, and are linked to the cell surface of the bacterium via covalent attachments to phospholipids, lipid-A molecules, or peptidoglycan. The composition allows for a remarkable variability - over 80 different capsular polysaccharides (also known as K antigens) have been described for TAX-562 alone. Capsular polysaccharides are highly hydrated, and consist of over 95% water. Biosynthesis and assembly of capsular polysaccharides is a complex process.

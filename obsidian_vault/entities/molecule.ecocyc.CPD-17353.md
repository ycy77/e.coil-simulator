## Enriched Summary

Meroterpenoids are secondary metabolites that are produced in part from a terpenoid co-substrate by mixed biosynthetic pathways. They are hybrid compounds that combine terpenoid scaffolds with polyketides, alkaloids, phenols, or amino acids. They are widely produced by bacteria, algae, plants, and animals . The term meroterpenoid was first used by in 1986 to describe natural products bearing a mixed terpenoid biogenesis. The prefix mero- is derived from the Greek word "merus", which means "fragment or part" . Many meroterpenoids demonstrate biological activities of medical importance, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, anti-atherosclerotic, anti-adipogenic, anti-diabetic, anti-carcinogenic, and neuroprotective activities.

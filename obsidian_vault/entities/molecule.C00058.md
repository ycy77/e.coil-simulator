## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: Formate; Methanoic acid; Formic acid Formate is a one-carbon molecule that was first isolated from distillation of ant bodies, and it was subsequently named using the Latin word for ant, formica . Ants and other insects accumulate formate in secretory glands and release their content as a mechanism of defense. Formate serves as an intermediate in one-carbon (1C) metabolism in all organisms. It is also a key metabolite for many bacterial and archaeal species. Depending on the physiological conditions, formate serves various roles, such as to act as a reductant in anaerobic respiration, be oxidized aerobically, be secreted, or be converted to hydrogen and CO2. Formate is particularly common in anaerobic habitats, produced as a fermentation product of some species and used as a growth substrate for others. It is crucial compound in many syntrophic associations, in which organisms live close to the thermodynamic limit (for example, sulfate-reducing bacteria and methanogens) . Its redox potential is low (around -420 mV), which allows it to donate an electron for the reduction of fumarate. In eukaryotes formate is produced from a variety of nutrients, and can be fed into a number of biosynthetic pathways.

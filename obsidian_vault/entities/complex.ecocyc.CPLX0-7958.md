## Annotation

The exact structure of the carbon-phosphorus lyase (C-P lyase) system is not well characterized. The genes encoding proteins of this enzymatic system are located in a 14-gene phnCDEFGHIJKLMNOP operon that is involved in phosphonate uptake and metabolism and is a member of the phosphate regulon . A well defined core complex consists of the PhnG, PhnH, PhnI and PhnJ subunits . A crystal structure of the complex has been solved at 1.7 Å resolution, revealing a symmetrical hetero-octamer consisting of a compact central tetramer comprised of PhnI and PhnG subunits, each interacting individually with the distal PhnJ and PhnH subunits . Curiously, the site of the Fe4S4 cluster, which is likely required for radical formation, is located at a 30 Å distance from a glycine residue in PhnJ which is the location of a radical between enzymatic turnover cycles . A functionally important zinc binding site is shared between PhnI (His328 and His333) and PhnJ (His108) . The CP lyase core complex associates with a dimer of PHNK-MONOMER PhnK, which further associated with a dimer of PHNL-MONOMER PhnL. PhnK and PhnL are members of the ATP-binding protein family, and both are able to bind and hydrolyze ATP, which leads to opening of the core complex, exposing and rearranging the Zn2+ active site . In vitro analysis has shown that purified PhnI on its own has ribonucleoside 5'-triphosphate nucleosidase activity, liberating adenine from ATP and guanine from GTP, but in the presence of PhnG, PhnH, and PhnL the enzyme has EC-2.7.8.37 "ATP:methylphosphonate 5-triphosphoribosyltransferase" activity .

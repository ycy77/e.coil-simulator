## Enriched Summary

Saponins are a class of secondary metabolites that have been grouped together phenomenologically by the foaming they produce when shaken in aqueous solutions, and structurally by their composition of one or more hydrophilic glycoside moieties combined with a lipophilic triterpene derivative. They are particularly abundant in various plant species, but are also found in marine organisms. The aglycones (glycoside-free portion) of the saponins are termed sapogenins. They can be any one of a wide variety of polycyclic organic structures originating from the serial addition of ten-carbon terpene units to compose a C30 triterpene skeleton. The number and length of saccharide chains attached to the sapogenin can vary greatly.

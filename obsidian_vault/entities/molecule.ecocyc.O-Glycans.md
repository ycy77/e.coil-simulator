## Enriched Summary

O-Linked glycans are polysaccharide or oligosaccharide that are linked to a protein via the oxygen atom in the side chain of a SER or THR residue. Most commonly O-glycosylation begins with the attachment of CPD-3604 to the free hydroxyl group of the amino acid, although O-linkages to fucose, glucose, mannose, xylose and other sugars are known. Mucin-type O-glycosylation, consisting of glycans attached via O-linked CPD-3604, is one of the most abundant forms of protein glycosylation in animals. Eight core-type structures of mucin-type O-glycans have been proposed. In eukaryotes, O-linked glycans are assembled in the Golgi apparatus one sugar at a time, while attached to the core protein. Examples for O-linked glycoproteins include glycophorin, Mucin mucin, notch, thrombospondin, Factor-VII "factor VII" and Factor-IX "factor IX".

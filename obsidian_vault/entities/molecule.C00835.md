## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: Sepiapterin Early studies have suggested that the red eye pigments of the fruit fly TAX-7227 are pteridines . One of those compounds was a highly fluorescent dihydro pteridine . The compound is believed to be a precursor of the red eye pigment CPD-16457 (see PWY-7442 for the pathway). Its concentration was much higher in the sepia mutant, in which synthesis of the red pigment is blocked, giving it its original name sepia pteridine . Since EC-1.14.16.1, from liver was known to utilize a dihydro pterin cofactor, it was later tested whether sepiapterin could act as a cofactor. When the enzyme was tested with extracts made from sepia fly mutants, it activity was almost 1000 times higher than that with a rat liver extract (it was later shown that the natural cofactor of that enzyme was a different compound - 78-dihydrobiopterins 7,8-dihydrobiopterin ).

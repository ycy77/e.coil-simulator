## Enriched Summary

O-Linked glycans are polysaccharide or oligosaccharide that are linked to a protein via the oxygen atom in the side chain of an SER or THR residue. In the structure below, R1 is hydrogen when the modified amino acid is SER and carbon when the amino acid is THR. In eukaryotes, O-linked glycans are assembled in the Golgi apparatus one sugar at a time, while attached to the core protein. Examples for O-linked glycoproteins include glycophorin, Mucin mucin, notch, thrombospondin, Factor-VII "factor VII" and Factor-IX "factor IX". O-Linked glycans are polysaccharide or oligosaccharide that are linked to a protein via the oxygen atom in the side chain of a SER or THR residue. The most common O-glycosylation in eukaryotic organisms is mucin-type O-glycosylation, consisting of glycans attached via O-linked CPD-3604, although O-linkages to fucose, glucose, mannose, xylose and other sugars are known. Eight core-type structures of mucin-type O-glycans have been proposed, including Core1 "core 1", Core2 "core 2", GLUCOSAMINYL-13-N-ETCETERA-GALACTOSAMINY "core 3", GLUCOSAMINYL-16-ETCETERA-GALACTOSAMINYL- "core 4", Core5 "core 5", Core6 "core 6", Core7 "core 7" and Core8 "core 8" .

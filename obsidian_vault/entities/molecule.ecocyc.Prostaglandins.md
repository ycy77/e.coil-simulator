## Enriched Summary

The term prostanoid is used to describe a subclass of Icosanoids icosanoids that includes the Prostaglandins prostaglandins and the Thromboxanes thromboxanes. These lipid compounds are derived from the ω-6 essential fatty acid ARACHIDONIC_ACID. Every prostanoid contains 20 carbon atoms with an internal carbon ring structure. Prostaglandins are messenger molecules produced de novo by most tissues and organs that act in an autocrine or paracrine fashion to mediate both homeostatic and inflammatory reactions. The name prostaglandin derives from the fact that initially it was thought to be present mainly in prostate secretions. See also PWY66-374.

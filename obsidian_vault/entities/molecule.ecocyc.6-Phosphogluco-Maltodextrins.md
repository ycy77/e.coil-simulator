## Enriched Summary

Maltodextrins are linear polymers of glucose molecules joined by an O-glycosidic (α-1->4) bond. The term is usally used to describe a mixture of chains that vary from 3-17 glucose units long. Maltodextrin is commonly used as a food additive, and is commercially produced from corn and wheat Starch by partial hydrolysis. This 6-phosphogluco-maltodextrin has a single α-linked glucosyl residue that is phosphorylated at the O6 position . The phosphorylation can occur at residues other than that shown in this structure, but not on multiple O6 residues.

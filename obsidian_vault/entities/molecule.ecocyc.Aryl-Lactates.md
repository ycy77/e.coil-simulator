## Enriched Summary

Aryl-lactates are metabolites derived from gut microbes that impact human physiology, including growth, development, and immune function. The aryl-lactates CPD-5921, CPD-2222, and CPD-21492 are derived from PHE, TYR and TRP, respectively, via the enzymes EC-2.6.1.57, and microbial EC-1.1.1.110 . Aryl-lactates are present in nearly all fermented foods . Evidence suggests that they can bind immunomodulatory receptors, including the human hydroxycarboxylic acid receptor 3 (HCAR3), and aryl hydrocarbon receptor (AhR) .

## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: A low activity DNA endonuclease yielding 3'-hydroxyl ends (Probable). Upon expression enhances RecA-independent DNA recombination 2.9-fold, concomitantly reducing viability by 59% and inducing DNA damage as measured by induction of the SOS repair response. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:28096446}. RpnC is one of five proteins in E. coli that belong to the "transposase_31" family , which is distantly related to the PD-(D/E)XK nuclease superfamily . Overexpression of rpnC increases RecA-independent recombination, reduces cell viability, and induces expression of the DNA damage-inducible gene dinD . An insertion mutation in rpnC does not affect the ability to grow on glucose minimal medium . RpnC: "recombination-promoting nuclease C"

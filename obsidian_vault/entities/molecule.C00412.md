## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: Stearoyl-CoA; Stearyl-CoA; Stearyl coenzyme A; Octadecanoyl-CoA Stearate is a saturated fatty acid that occurs in many animal and vegetable fats and oils. It is a large constituent in milk fats, lard, and cocoa and shea butters. The name comes from Greek for "hard fat", reflecting the fact that stearate forms a waxy solid . Stearate was first described by Michel Eugene Chevreul in 1823. In cells STEARIC_ACID is usually found in the form of Stearoyl-ACPs stearoyl-[acp], STEAROYL-COA or Stearoyl-lipid "incorporated into lipids".

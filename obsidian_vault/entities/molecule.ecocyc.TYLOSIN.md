## Enriched Summary

Tylosin is a macrolide type antibiotic produced by several species of bacteria. While the bacterium TAX-1906 is used for the commercial production of TYLOSIN , other species that produce it include strains of TAX-1927 and TAX-1912 . Tylosin has has a broad spectrum of activity against Gram-positive organisms and a limited range of Gram-negative organisms . It is extremely active against Mycoplasma species isolated from both mammalian and avian hosts . Like other macrolides, tylosin has a bacteriostatic effect caused by inhibition of protein synthesis through binding to the 50S subunit of the bacterial ribosome . Tylosin is used in veterinary medicine to treat bacterial infections in a wide range of species and is considered very safe . It has also been used as a growth promotant in some species , and as a treatment for colitis in companion animals . Tylosine is composed of of a 16-membered branched lactone ring (CPD-15957) and three sugars, CPD-13969, CPD-13844, and Mycinopyranose .

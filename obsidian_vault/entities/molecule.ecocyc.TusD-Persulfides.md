## Enriched Summary

TusD TusD functions as a sulfur mediator during synthesis of 2-thiouridine of the modified wobble base mnm5s2U in tRNA. TusD forms a complex with TusB and TusC. Activated sulfur appears to be transferred from TusA TusA to cysteine residue C78 in TusD . The sulfur is subsequently transferred to Sulfur-Carrier-Proteins-TusE TusE, which is known to stimulate the transfer of sulfur from TusA to TusD. The TusBCD complex does not appear to bind tRNA directly in an in vitro assay, but it does interact with TusE . A crystal structure of the TusBCD complex has been determined at 2.15 Å resolution. All three subunits show structural similarity to a protein of unknown function, YchN . A tusD deletion mutant lacks the 2-thio modification of mnm5s2U in tRNA and has a severe growth defect .

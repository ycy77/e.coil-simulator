## Annotation

There are two distinct class II tagatose-1,6-bisphosphate aldolases in E. coli, the kbaY-encoded aldolase 1 and the EG12419 gatY-encoded aldolase 2, both of which participate in galactitol catabolism. They catalyze the reversible aldol condensation of glycerone phosphate (dihydroxyacetone phosphate) with D-glyceraldehyde 3-phosphate to form tagatose 1,6-bisphosphate . The G7631-MONOMER KbaZ protein has no catalytic activity, but appears to stabilize KbaY, is required for full activity of KbaY, and thus may have a chaperone-like function . Recombinant KbaY was overexpressed, purified and kinetically characterized. It is highly specific for tagatose-1,6-bisphosphate as substrate compared with its diastereoisomer fructose-1,6-bisphosphate . Mutant enzymes with increased activity towards fructose-1,6-bisphosphate were generated by directed evolution . The crystal structure of KbaY in complex with phosphoglycolohydroxamate was subsequently solved at 1.45 Å resolution . Mass spectrometry and gel filtration chromatography data as well as crystallographic data revealed a homotetramer, and the catalytic Zn2+ was identified . Simulations suggest that oligomerization stabilizes the structure and enables the dynamic movements of the functional loops . Although an in silico genomic analysis suggested that E. coli K-12 can also catabolize N-acetyl-D-galactosamine and D-galactosamine via the kba genes , this was not experimentally supported. E. coli K-12 was found to carry a deletion in the kba gene cluster that inactivates several genes for PTS transport and a deacetylase for metabolism of these compounds.

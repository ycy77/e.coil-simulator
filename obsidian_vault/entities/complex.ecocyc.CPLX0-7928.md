## Enriched Summary

RelB is the antitoxin of the RelE-RelB toxin-antitoxin system . RelB also represses transcription of relBEF . The RelE toxin inhibits protein translation by catalyzing cleavage of mRNA in the A site of the ribosome . RelB production relieves established RelE-mediated translation inhibition and inhibition of cell growth, indicating that growth inhibition is not irreversible . The activity of tmRNA, SsrA, counteracts RelE-mediated translation inhibition . The RelE-RelB system is involved in regulation of cell growth under conditions with limited nutrients . A Tn10 insertion in ydfV restores colony-forming ability to an rne mutant. The suppression phenotype is reversed by overexpression of relB, but not ydfV . Inhibition of relB expression by CRISPRi reduced cellular fitness under treatment with the antibiotics verapamil or erythromycin . RelE and RelB exhibit a physical interaction, and RelE physically interacts with ribosomes . Regulation has been described . When cells are starved of amino acids, Lon protease degrades RelB; RelB degradation derepresses transcription of relBE; RelE accumulates in excess compared with its RelB antitoxin; and this free RelE causes translation inhibition . Mutations in the relB locus were initially identified by a "delayed relaxed" phenotype . Review: RelB is the antitoxin of the RelE-RelB toxin-antitoxin system...

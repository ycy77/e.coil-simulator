## Enriched Summary

Acetoin, along with diacetyl, is one of the compounds that gives butter its characteristic taste. It is natuarally found in apples, butter, yogurt, asparagus, black currants, blackberry, wheat, broccoli, brussels sprouts, and cantaloupe. Acetoin is a common fermentation product, since it is a neutral compound that allows bacteria to degrade sugars without substantial acidification of the growth medium.

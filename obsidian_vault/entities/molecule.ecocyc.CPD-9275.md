## Enriched Summary

CPD-9275 Allicin is a defense molecule produced by garlic (TAX-4682 with a broad range of biological activities. Allicin is physiologically active in microbial, plant and mammalian cells, due to its ability to participate in redox-reactions with thiol groups in glutathione and proteins. In a dose-dependent manner allicin can inhibit the proliferation of both bacteria and fungi, and even kill cells, including antibiotic-resistant strains like methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA) .

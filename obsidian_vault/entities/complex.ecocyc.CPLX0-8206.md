## Enriched Summary

DhaM is homologous to certain components of phosphotransferase (PTS) transporters: the N terminus is similar to an EIIA component, the central domain is similar to HPr, and the C terminus is similar to the N-terminal domain of EI. DhaM can be phosphorylated in vitro by phosphoenolpyruvate in the presence of PTSI-MONOMER EI (PtsI) and PTSH-MONOMER HPr (PtsH) and serves as the phosphoryl donor of the dihydroxyacetone kinase reaction . DhaM/YcgC was also reported to have protein-acetyllysine deacetylase activity that does not require NAD+ or Zn2+. One of the targets was reported to be lysine-acetylated PD01352 . However, a recent report shows that DhaM/YcgC does not have RutR-acetyllysine deacetylase activity . DhaM forms a homodimer in solution .

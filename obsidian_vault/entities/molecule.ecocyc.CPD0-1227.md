## Enriched Summary

Triclosan is an antibacterial and antifungal agent. At lower concentrations triclosan appears bacteriostatic and targets bacteria mainly by inhibiting fatty acid synthesis via its binding to the bacterial ENOYL-ACP-REDUCT-NADH-MONOMER, which is encoded by EG11528. Triclosan forms a tight, non-covalent ternary complex with the oxidized NADH cofactor in the active site of the enzyme . Some Gram-positive bacteria are naturally resistant to triclosan due to the presence of triclosan-resistant enoyl-ACP reductase isoforms, encoded by G-15302 . In addition, some Gram-negative bacteria have a triclosan-resistant isoform encoded by G-15301 . Some Gram-positive organisms have a different isoform encoded by BSU08650. FabL is inhibited by triclosan in a reversible manner, but does not form a stable NADtriclosan ternary complex . ## Annotation

Triclosan is an antibacterial and antifungal agent. At lower concentrations triclosan appears bacteriostatic and targets bacteria mainly by inhibiting fatty acid synthesis via its binding to the bacterial ENOYL-ACP-REDUCT-NADH-MONOMER, which is encoded by EG11528. Triclosan forms a tight, non-covalent ternary complex with the oxidized NADH cofactor in the active site of the enzyme . Some Gram-positive bacteria are naturally resistant to triclosan due to the presence of triclosan-resistant enoyl-ACP reductase isoforms, encoded by G-15302 . In addition, some Gram-negative bacteria have a triclosan-resistant isoform encoded by G-15301 . Some Gram-positive organisms have a different isoform encoded by BSU08650. FabL is inhibited by triclosan in a reversible manner, but does not form a stable NADtriclosan ternary complex . At higher concentration triclosan acts as a biocide, with multiple cytoplasmic and membrane targets .

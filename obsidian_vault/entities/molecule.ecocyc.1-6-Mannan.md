## Enriched Summary

There are several types of α-1,6-D-mannan. The proteins in yeast cell walls contain particularly complex N-glycans referred to as α-mannans in which the core N-glycan is extended by ~200 α-1,6-mannose units that are also decorated with α-linked mannose side chains . In some yeasts, such as the human pathogen TAX-5476, β-1,2-mannose residues cap the α-mannans side chains, and extended β-1,2-mannan chains are attached to the α-mannan via phosphate bridges . α-1,6-D-mannan also makes up the backbone of the mycobacterial lipoarabinomannan (LAM), which is attached to the plasma membrane .

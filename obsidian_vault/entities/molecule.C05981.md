## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: Phosphatidylinositol-3,4,5-trisphosphate; 1-Phosphatidyl-1D-myo-inositol 3,4,5-trisphosphate; 1,2-Diacyl-sn-glycero-3-phospho-(1'-myo-inositol-3',4',5'-bisphosphate) EcoCyc common name: 1-phosphatidyl-1D-myo-inositol 3,4,5-trisphosphate. The phosphorylated derivatives of L-1-phosphatidyl-inositols "phosphatidylinositol" (PtdIns), collectively called phosphoinositides (PIs), are eukaryotic cell membrane phospholipids whose inositol head group is phosphorylated at position D3, D4, or D5, singly or in all combinations, to yield seven PI metabolites. The seven phosphoinositides found in animals include: Phosphatidylinositol monophosphates: CPD-177, CPD-1108 and 1-PHOSPHATIDYL-1D-MYO-INOSITOL-5-PHOSPHA. Phosphatidylinositol bisphophosphates: 1-PHOSPHATIDYL-1D-MYO-INOSITOL-34-BISPH, 1-PHOSPHATIDYL-1D-MYO-INOSITOL-35-BISPH, and PHOSPHATIDYL-MYO-INOSITOL-45-BISPHOSPHA. Phosphatidylinositol trisphophosphate: PHOSPHATIDYLINOSITOL-345-TRIPHOSPHATE.

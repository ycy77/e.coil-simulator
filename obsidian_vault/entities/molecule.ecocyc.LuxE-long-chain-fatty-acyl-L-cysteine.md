## Enriched Summary

EC-6.2.1.19, encoded by the luxE gene, is part of the fatty acid reductase multienzyme complex found in bioluminescent bacteria. The complex catalyzes the activation, transfer, and reduction of fatty acids to generate fatty aldehydes, which serve as the substrate for the luminescence reaction. The enzyme activates long-chain fatty acids to fatty acyl-AMP, followed by their attachment to a cysteine residue located very close to the carboxyl terminus of the protein. The acyl-protein thioester serves as a substrate for EC-1.2.1.50 which releases the acyl moiety in the form of an aldehyde .

## Annotation

CpxP is involved in resistance to extracytoplasmic stresses; a cpxP mutant exhibits decreased resistance to high pH, compared to wild type . CpxP is a negative regulator of the PWY0-1485 Cpx pathway; this feedback regulation occurs via CPXA-MONOMER CpxA and may serve to restrict the Cpx response to a limited period of time; CpxP overproduction turns off the Cpx response . CpxP binds to CpxA and inhibits the autokinase activity of CpxA . Envelope perturbation inhibits the CpxP-mediated inhibition of CpxA signaling . CpxP may also act as a chaperone ; CpxP prevents aggregation of citrate synthase in vitro . CpxP functions as a periplasmic adaptor to direct CPLX0-2921 DegP mediated proteolysis. CpxP is degraded by DegP in the presence of misfolded pili proteins PapE or PapG (from pathogenic E. coli) . Induction of the Cpx response is compromised in a strain lacking DegP suggesting that DegP mediated proteolysis of CpxP relieves inhibition of the Cpx response . CpxP exists as a dimer in vivo. Purified, crystallised CpxP forms an antiparallel dimer of intertwined α-helices . The removal of CpxP caused by spheroplast formation is an important factor in Cpx pathway activation under these conditions . CpxP interacts directly with CpxA in vivo and this interaction is disrupted by high salt concentration (300mM NaCl) and the presence of misfolded P pilus subunit, PapE (from uropathogenic E. coli) . CpxP has similarity (26.5% protein sequence identity) to the periplasmic chaperone, CPLX0-7923 Spy . Regulation has been described .

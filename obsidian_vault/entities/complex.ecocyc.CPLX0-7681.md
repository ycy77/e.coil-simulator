## Enriched Summary

XapR, "xanthosine phosphorylase regulator," activates transcription of genes involved in xanthosine metabolism when xanthosine is present . Deoxyinosine can also induce XapR activity, although not to the same extent as xanthosine . XapR, which belongs to the LysR family of transcriptional regulators, has a DNA-binding site in the N-terminal domain and a signal-recognition site separated in two regions in the amino acid sequence. In the three-dimensional structure of an XapR homolog (CysB from Klebsiella aerogenes), these two regions appear close to each other, forming one signal-binding site. XapR is homologous to AlsR; they show 68% of identity in an amino-terminal region of 60 amino acids . A xapR mutant can not grow on xanthosine . The xapR gene is located downstream of the genes it regulates and in the same direction, but in an independent transcription unit that appears to be expressed constitutively .

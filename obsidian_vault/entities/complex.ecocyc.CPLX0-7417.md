## Enriched Summary

InsA is a transcriptional repressor for the insertion element IS2. InsA binds in the left inverted repeat of IS2, upstream from the insA promoter in IS2. InsA represses IS2 gene transcription . This DNA binding activity depends both on the first 12 amino-terminal residues of InsA and on its dimerization. Dimerization, in turn, requires the carboxy-terminal 44 residues of InsA . IS2 is an insertion sequence present in 0-12 copies in various strains of E. coli . Though it can cause activation and suppression of adjacent genes, activation appears to be due to generation of novel promoters during the insertion process rather than to the presence of promoters carried within IS2 . In addition to autoregulation by InsA, transcription of IS2 genes is negatively regulated by CPLX0-226 .

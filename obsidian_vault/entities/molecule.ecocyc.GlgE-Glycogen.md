## Enriched Summary

TAX-1773 and other pathogenic mycobacterial species produce a capsular layer outside the bacterial wall and plasma membrane that is thought to be involved in immune evasion and virulence . The capsular layer is composed of α-glucan (80-90%) and arabinomannan (10-20%) polysaccharides, proteins of the ESX-1 secretion system, and small amounts of lipid . The glucan structure resembles Glycogens glycogen, consisting of relatively short chains of α (1->4)-linked glucose molecules, which are connected to each other in a branching pattern by α (1->6) bonds . However, the polymer differs from classical glycogen in seceral aspects. The chains are shorter, consisting of 7 sugars vs 11. In addition, the branching pattern is different - while there are more α-1,6 branches than in glycogen (14% vs 9%), less branches are coming off each branch (1.2-1.6 vs 1.8-1.9 in glycogen) .

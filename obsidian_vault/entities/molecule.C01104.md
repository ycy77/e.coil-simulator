## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: Trimethylamine N-oxide; N,N,N-Trimethylamine N-oxide; (CH3)3NO Trimethylamine N-oxide (TMAO) is an important constituent of fish muscle and body fluids. In mammals it is believed to promote atherogenesis through its interaction with macrophages and lipid metabolism . High serum levels of TMAO have been shown to promote formation of atherosclerotic plaques in a mouse model , and serum levels of TMAO were significantly correlated with the incidence of heart attack, stroke, and death in a clinical population . In deep-living marine animals TMAO stabilizes intracellular proteins and cell structure for their survival under the high hydrostatic pressure of the deep ocean . It is believed that fish cannot survive without TMAO at a depth greater than 5 km . In bacteria the compound serves several roles: it is used as a nitrogen source by the SAR11 clade and the marine TAX-2433 clade of α-proteobacteria , it is used as an electron acceptor by some γ-proteobacteria such as TAX-22 and TAX-662 under anaerobic conditions , and it is accumulated by some deep sea bacteria such as TAX-480520 as a mechanism to cope with the high pressure .

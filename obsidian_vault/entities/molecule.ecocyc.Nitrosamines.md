## Enriched Summary

Nitrosamines have a nitroso group bonded to a deprotonated amine.They are formed at low pH (optimal pH of 3.5) by the electrophilic substitution reaction between a nitrozonium cation (NO+) and an amine nitrogen. Nitrosamines are associated with carcinogenic, mutagenic and teratogenic properties . Nitrosamines are traditionally divided into volatile and non-volatile groups. Volatile nitrosamines found in foods include CPD-28107 (NDEA), CPD-18996 (NDMA), CPD-28108 (NPIP), N-nitrozopyrrolidine (NPYR), N-nitrosodibutylamine (NDBA), N-nitrosomorpholine (NMOR), N-nitrozodipropylamine (NDPA), and N-nitrosodibenzylamine (NDBzA), while common non-volatile nitrosamines invclude N-nitrosodiphenylamine (NDPhA), N-nitrosomethylaniline (NMA), N-nitroso-2-methyl-thiazolidine-4-carboxylic acid (NMTCA), N-nitrosoproline, N-nitrosohydroxyproline, N-nitrososarcosine, N-nitrosothiazolidine, N-nitrosothiazolidine-4-carboxylate, N-nitrozooxazolidine-4-carboxylate, N-nitroso-5-methyloxazoldine-4-carboxylate, and N-nitroso-2-(hydroxymethyl) thiazolidine-4-carboxylate Nitrosamines have a nitroso group bonded to a deprotonated amine.They are formed at low pH (optimal pH of 3.5) by the electrophilic substitution reaction between a nitrozonium cation (NO+) and an amine nitrogen.

## Enriched Summary

A unique feature of mycobacterial cell wall lipids is the presence of very long chain fatty acids with multiple methyl branches toward the carboxyl end of the carbon chain. Many types of such acids exist, including Mycosanoic-Acids "mycosanoic acids", Mycolipanoic-Acids "mycolipanoic acids", Mycolipanolic-Acids "mycolipanolic acids", Mycolipenic-Acids "mycolipenic acids", Mycolipodienoic-Acids "mycolipodienoic acids", Phthioceranic-Acids "phthioceranic acids", and Mycocerosic-Acids "mycocerosic acids". Mycolipenoic acids are 2,4,6-trimethylated 2-alkenoic very-long-chain acids. They typically contain 22-28 carbons and are found only in virulent strains . They were found to be inhibitory to leukocyte migration and leukotoxic.

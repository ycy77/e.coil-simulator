## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Could be a mediator in iron transactions between iron acquisition and iron-requiring processes, such as synthesis and/or repair of Fe-S clusters in biosynthetic enzymes. Necessary to maintain high levels of aconitase under oxidative stress. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:14594836, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15883188}. YggX belongs to a subclass of the treble clef (TC) domain family that appears to have lost zinc binding ability . YggX is proposed to play a role in oxidation resistance of iron-sulfur clusters . YggX is found at high abundance in vivo . A solution structure of YggX has been determined . C7 of YggX is redox-sensitive . A yggX mutant shows paraquat sensitivity, a paraquat-induced aconitase defect and increased sensitivity to redox stress . Multicopy yggX expression results in elevated aconitase activity, compared to wild type . A yggX null mutant produces a higher extracellular thiamine diphosphate titer . Transcription of yggX is induced by SoxS overproduction or by oxidative stress . YggX is upregulated under glucose-limited fed-batch conditions and in response to plumbagin .

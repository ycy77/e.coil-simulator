## Enriched Summary

Neomycins are a family of broad-spectrum highly toxic antibiotics produced by TAX-1906. They were discovered in 1949 by Waksman and Lechevalier (Waksman later received the 1951 Nobel Prize in Physiology and medicine) . Neomycin usually exists as a complex composed of two stereoisomers, CPD-14142 and CPD-14141, which are the two active components. A third component, CPD-14137 "neomycin A", which is a degradation product, is present in small quantities. Neomycin was originally used as a treatment for tuberculosis, but serious side effects soon became apparent, including ototoxicity , which sometimes developed to complete loss of hearing, and nephrotoxicity.

CdiA-CT is the toxic component of a toxin-immunity protein module, which functions as a cellular contact-dependent growth inhibition (CDI) system. CDI modules allow bacteria to communicate with and inhibit the growth of closely related neighboring bacteria in a contact-dependent fashion (PubMed:28223500). The toxin may remodel the EF-Tu-aa-tRNA complex to displace the 3'-end of the aa-tRNA so it can enter the toxin active site and be cleaved. GTP is required for tRNase activity but is not hydrolyzed.

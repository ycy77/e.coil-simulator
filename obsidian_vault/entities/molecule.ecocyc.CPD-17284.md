## Enriched Summary

ARACHIDONIC_ACID "Arachidonate" (arachidonic acid) is a polyunsaturated ω-6 fatty acid with a 20-carbon chain and four cis-double bonds. It is produced at high levels by mosses (see PWY-5353), some plants, and by some marine bacteria (see PWY-7583). Mammals cannot synthesize ARACHIDONIC_ACID de novo, but most mammals are able to synthesize it from simpler unsaturated fatty acids (see PWY-7592). In mammals ARACHIDONIC_ACID is present in membrane phospholipids (especially phosphatidylethanolamine, phosphatidylcholine, and phosphatidylinositides) and is abundant in the brain, muscles, and liver. It accounts for roughly 10-20% of the phospholipid fatty acid content of skeletal muscle cells. In addition to being involved in cellular signaling as a lipid second messenger, arachidonate is also a key inflammatory intermediate and can also act as a vasodilator. Like other fatty acids, ARACHIDONIC_ACID is rarely found in its free form. It is usually found either as ARACHIDONYL-COA or CPD-17284 "incorporated into a lipid".

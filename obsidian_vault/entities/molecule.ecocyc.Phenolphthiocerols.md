## Enriched Summary

Phthiocerols Phthiocerol-based lipids and glycolipids have been found in many species of TAX-1762, including TAX-1773, TAX-1765, TAX-33894, TAX-1781, TAX-1768, TAX-1777, TAX-1769 and TAX-1809 . Phthiocerols comprise a linear carbohydrate molecule to which one methoxyl group, one methyl group, and two secondary hydroxyl groups are attached. Two types of phthiocerols have been described. In Phthiocerol-A "phthiocerol A" the methoxyl and methyl groups are attached at positions 3 and 4, respectively, and the hydroxyl groups are at positions 9 and 11. In Phthiocerol-B "phthiocerol B" a methyl group is missing from the beginning of the molecule, and thus the methoxyl and methyl groups are attached at positions 2 and 3, respectively, and the hydroxyl groups are at positions 8 and 10. ## Annotation

Phthiocerols Phthiocerol-based lipids and glycolipids have been found in many species of TAX-1762, including TAX-1773, TAX-1765, TAX-33894, TAX-1781, TAX-1768, TAX-1777, TAX-1769 and TAX-1809 . In Phthiocerol-A "phthiocerol A" the methoxyl and methyl groups are attached at positions 3 and 4, respectively, and the hydroxyl groups are at positions 9 and 11. In Phthiocerol-B "phthiocerol B" a methyl group is missing from the beginning of the molecule, and thus the methoxyl and methyl groups are attached at positions 2 and 3, respectively, and the hydroxyl groups are at positions 8 and 10. The phthiocerol molecules have a range of 28 to 34 carbon atoms in the main chain, indicating a promiscuity of the enzymes involved in their biosynthesis. Longer molecules have additional carbon atoms added at the ω end of the molecule. Many organisms, including TAX-1769, TAX-1768, TAX-1765, TAX-1781, and TAX-78331, produce the related compound Phenolphthiocerols phenolphthiocerol, which contains a phenol group at the ω end of the molecule . The phenol is usually glycosylated by a type or species-specific mono-, tri-, or a tetrasaccharide. For example, in TAX-78331 the trisaccharide (2,3,4-tri-O-methyl L-fucopyranosyl-(α1→3)-rhamnopyranosyl-(α1→3)-2-O-methyl L-rhamnopyranose) is attached. In addition, the two secondary alcohol functions are often esterified by Mycocerosic-Acids "mycocerosic acids" or Phthioceranic-Acids "phthioceranic acids" (see CPD-18579) .

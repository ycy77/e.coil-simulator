## Annotation

The bgl operon, encoding genes for the uptake and utilization of β-glucosides, is silent in wild type E. coli K-12 due to the interaction of sequences upstream and downstream of the promoter . It has been reported to be activated by the insertion of an IS element into the region upstream of the operon in some E. coli isolates as well as by point mutations within the CAP binding site and gyrA or gyrb mutations . FIS helps to silence bgl by blocking activation by CAP . The bgl operon is also activated in hns mutants , by increased expression of bglJ or leuO , and by the Lac and λ repressors . The bgl operon is activated in E. coli infections of mouse liver . Activation of the bgl operon cannot occur in cells depleted of the histone-like proteins, HU and IHF . BglG is activated by phosphorylation by HPr (or FPr), but is inactivated by additional phosphorylation by BglF at a different site . BglG is both phosphorylated and dephosphorylated by BglF, the enzyme II component of the Bgl sugar-transporting phosphotransferase system. The phosphorylation state of BglG at the BglF site is controlled by a β-glucoside inducer. In the absence of β-glucoside, BglG associates with and is phosphorylated by BglF in the cytoplasmic membrane. Addition of the inducer leads to dephosphorylation of BglG at the BglF site and its localization to the cytoplasm where it forms homodimers and its transcriptional antitermination activity allows transcription of the bgl operon. The doubly phosphorylated form of BglG is unable to dimerize and bind to the target mRNA and does not have antiterminator activity . In the absence of the activated BglG homodimer, transcription from the bgl promoter is terminated at one of two rho-independent terminators. The first is located in the leader region of the bgl mR

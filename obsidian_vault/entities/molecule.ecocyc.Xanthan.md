## Enriched Summary

Xanthan Xanthan is an exopolysaccharide produced by the bacterium TAX-339 and related species. Because of its rheological properties, including high viscosity and pseudo-plasticity, and stability in extreme conditions, xanthan gum has been used in food, agriculture and industry as an emulsifying, thickening or suspending agent. It was approved for use in foods in 1968. Xanthan gum has been implicated as the pathogenic substance causing black rot in crucifers . The polymer is composed of repeating pentasaccharide units with the structure of a β-1,4-linked D-glucose backbone with trisaccharide side chains composed of mannose-(β-1,4)-glucuronic acid-(β-1,2)-mannose attached to alternate glucose residues in the backbone by α-1,3 linkages .

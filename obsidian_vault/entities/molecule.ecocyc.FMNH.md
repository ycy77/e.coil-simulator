## Enriched Summary

FMN is the principal form in which RIBOFLAVIN (vitamin B2) is found in cells and tissues. While it takes more energy to produce than the non-phosphorylated form of RIBOFLAVIN, it is more soluble. FMN is produced from riboflavin by the enzyme riboflavin kinase, and functions as prosthetic group of various oxidoreductases including NADH dehydrogenase. During the catalytic cycle, FMN cycles between the oxidized (FMN), semiquinone (FMNH) and reduced (FMNH2) forms, enabling it to take part in both one and two electron transfers.

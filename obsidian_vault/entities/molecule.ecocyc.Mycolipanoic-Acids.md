## Enriched Summary

A unique feature of mycobacterial cell wall lipids is the presence of very long chain fatty acids with multiple methyl branches toward the carboxyl end of the carbon chain. Many types of such acids exist, including Mycosanoic-Acids "mycosanoic acids", Mycolipanoic-Acids "mycolipanoic acids", Mycolipanolic-Acids "mycolipanolic acids", Mycolipenic-Acids "mycolipenic acids", Mycolipodienoic-Acids "mycolipodienoic acids", Phthioceranic-Acids "phthioceranic acids", and Mycocerosic-Acids "mycocerosic acids". Mycolipanoic acids (also named mycothianoic acids) are 2,4,6-trimethylated very-long-chain alkanoic acids . They are formed by EC-2.3.1.252. 3-Hydroxylated versions of these acids are known as Mycolipanolic-Acids "mycolipanolic acids" . They are produced by a different enzyme .

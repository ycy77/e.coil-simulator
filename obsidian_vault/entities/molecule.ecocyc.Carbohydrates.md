## Enriched Summary

An older definition of carbohydrates stated that they contain only carbon, hydrogen and oxygen. As the term was originally applied only to monosaccharides, the name was formulated in recognition of the fact that their empirical composition can be expressed as Cn(H2O)n. However, the term is now used in a much wider sense and includes Monosaccharides monosaccharides, Disaccharides disaccharides, Oligosaccharides oligosaccharides and Polysaccharides polysaccharides as well as substances derived from monosaccharides by reduction of the carbonyl group (Sugar-alcohols alditols), by oxidation of one or more terminal groups to Carboxylates "carboxylic acids", or by replacement of one or more hydroxy group(s) by a hydrogen atom. Strictly speaking, carbohydrates contain only carbon, hydrogen and oxygen atoms. When one or more of those atoms is substituted by an amino group, a sulfhydryl group, or a similar heteroatomic groups, the resulting compound is considered Carbohydrate-Derivatives. An exception to the rule are Cyclitols cyclitols, which are generally not regarded as carbohydrates even though they consist of only carbon, hydrogen and oxygen atoms.

## Enriched Summary

Lignans are dimers formed from β-β' linkages between monolignols (COUMARYL-ALCOHOL, CONIFERYL-ALCOHOL and SINAPYL-ALCOHOL), which in turn are formed from the hydroxycinnamic acids COUMARATE, FERULIC-ACID and SINAPATE, respectively . They are found in plants and many algae, where they function as defense molecules against herbivores and microorganisms .

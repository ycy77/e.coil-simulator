## Enriched Summary

A unique feature of mycobacterial cell wall lipids is the presence of very long chain fatty acids with multiple methyl branches toward the carboxyl end of the carbon chain. Many types of such acids exist, including Mycosanoic-Acids "mycosanoic acids", Mycolipanoic-Acids "mycolipanoic acids", Mycolipanolic-Acids "mycolipanolic acids", Mycolipenic-Acids "mycolipenic acids", Mycolipodienoic-Acids "mycolipodienoic acids", Phthioceranic-Acids "phthioceranic acids", and Mycocerosic-Acids "mycocerosic acids". Hydroxyphthioceranic acids are hepta and octa-methylated alkanoic very-long chain fatty acids with a hydroxyl group on the carbon next to the last methylated carbon. They are the major acyl group found in CPD-18600, the major sulfolipid of TAX-1773 . In heptamethylated hydroxyphthioceranic acids R1=OH, R2,R3 = H. In octamethylated hydroxyphthioceranic acids, R1= H, R2=CH3, and R3=OH.

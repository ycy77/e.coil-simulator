## Enriched Summary

When incubated together in the presence of NADH, YeaWX liberated TRIMETHYLAMINE (TMA) from several substrates, including CARNITINE, GAMMA-BUTYROBETAINE and CHOLINE . The production of TMA from carnitine and γ-butyrobetaine by the gut microbiota is considered an important process that occurs in the host gut during carnitine metabolism. Liver enzymes convert TMA to TRIMETHYLAMINE-N-O, which is thought to accelerate atherosclerosis .

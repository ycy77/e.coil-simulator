## Enriched Summary

Prophage lysis protein S homolog EssD (Lysis protein S homolog from lambdoid prophage DLP12) EssD is a predicted class II holin of the cryptic lambdoid prophage DLP12. The protein has two potential transmembrane domains . EssD has also been predicted to be a bitopic inner membrane protein . The effect of an essD deletion on biofilm formation is dependent on the strain background. In the BW25113 background, an essD mutation increases biofilm formation , while an essD mutant is impaired in curli-based biofilm formation, which may be an indirect effect due to altered peptidoglycan (PG) metabolism . Deletion of the lysis module of DLP12 defective prophage (essD, rrrD, rzpD/rzoD) induces an hypervesiculation phenotype (possibly due to an excess of PG fragments in the periplasmic space); expression of the module is sensitive to environmental stress factors (low temperature, high osmolarity and acidic pH) .

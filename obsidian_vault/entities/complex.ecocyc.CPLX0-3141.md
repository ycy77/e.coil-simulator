## Enriched Summary

YgiN is able to reoxidize menadiol that has been reduced by MdaB quinone reductase in vitro (and is referred to as a quinol monooxygenase (QuMo)), and the two enzymes may form a quinone redox cycle. Catalysis by YgiN does not appear to require metal ions or other cofactors. The reaction is not sensitive to inhibition by heptyl-4-hydroxyquinoline-N-oxide (HOQNO), a typical inhibitor of single electron reduction reactions, indicating that the reaction proceeds via a two-electron transfer mechanism. The biological role of a quinone redox cycle may be to maintain an intracellular pool of menadione and ubiquinone using a catalytic mechanism that avoids the formation of a semiquinone intermediate, and to act as a quinone buffer . YgiN protein has been detected in E. coli and has orthologs in a variety of other organisms . The ygiN gene may be transcribed in an operon together with mdaB; the genomic organization of orthologs of both genes is conserved . Expression of ygiN is upregulated by rapid external acidification and in an evolved strain that lacks the three cytochrome oxidases encoded by cydAB, cyoABCD, and cbdAB .

## Enriched Summary

CPD-25639 "Mannosylerythritol lipids" (MELs) are amphiphilic glycolipids that display high surface activity and act as biodetergents (biosurfactants). The compounds are produced by yeast strains that belong to the TAX-5269 and TAX-63261 genera. They show excellent surface-active properties and versatile biochemical actions. The compounds were first described in 1956 from an TAX-5269 species . However, the most common MEL producing species are members of the TAX-63261 genus, such as TAX-84753, TAX-84754, TAX-84752, and TAX-203536 . The lipids are acylated at positions O-2' and O-3' by a range of fatty acyl residues. In TAX-5270 the fatty acid at O-2' ranges from C2 to C6, while that at O-3' ranges from C12 to C16. In addition, MELs differ in their acetylation status. Four general types are produced differing in the acetylation of positions O-4' and O-6'. Mel-D has no acetylation, MEL-B is acetylated only at the O-6' position, MEL-C is acetylated only at the O-4' position, and MEL-A is acetylated at both positions . The biological function of MELs is still unknown, but they are of growing biotechnological interest as a sustainable alternative for chemical surfactants .

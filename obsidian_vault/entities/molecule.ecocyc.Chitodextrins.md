## Enriched Summary

CHITIN Chitin is a polymer of repeating units of β-(1,4)-linked N-ACETYL-BETA-D-GLUCOSAMINE (β-GlcNAcn). It may be the second most abundant biopolymer in the world, after cellulose. It is the main component of the cell walls of fungi, the exoskeletons of arthropods such as crustaceans and the insects, the radula of mollusks, and the beaks of cephalopods. In nature, chitin polymers are likely to have a high degree of polymerization and are insoluble in water . Chitodextrins Chitodextrins are polymers that are still purely composed of β-(1,4)-linked N-ACETYL-BETA-D-GLUCOSAMINE residues, but have smaller degrees of polymerization than native CHITIN chitin. Though the specific upper limit for defining a chitodextrin is hard to define, a class of related compounds referred to as chitooligosaccharides, composed of both N-ACETYL-BETA-D-GLUCOSAMINE and GLUCOSAMINE typically have a degree of polymerization of 20 or fewer residues .

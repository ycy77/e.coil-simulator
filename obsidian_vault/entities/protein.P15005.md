## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Recognizes N4- and C5-methylcytosine (and 5-hydroxy-methylcytosines) produced by a broad range of DNA methylases and appears to act against 5-methylcytosine preceded by a purine residue. Binds to DNA containing methylated cytosines; also binds to GTP. Isoform 33 kDa is less active than isoform 51 kDa and may play a role in regulating the activity of isoform 51 kDa by competing with it in DNA and protein binding abilities. {ECO:0000305|PubMed:2649480}. The McrBS gene product of mcrB is generated by alternative translation initiation, resulting in a shorter peptide that lacks the 161 N-terminal amino acids of McrB that are required for DNA binding . McrBS may be required for stabilization of the McrBC restriction complex or as an inhibitor of DNA cleavage due to sequestration of McrC . The McrB protein together with McrC forms the McrBC restriction system . The McrB subunit mediates binding of the enzyme to hemi- and fully-methylated 5'PuC sites . Sequence determinants for DNA binding have been studied . McrB also binds and hydrolyzes the essential cofactor GTP. McrB contains an N-terminal DNA binding domain , a AAA+ domain and GTP-binding motifs . Cryo-EM shows hexameric ring structures formed by McrB . DNA binding is specific for methylcytosine-containing DNA and requires both Mg2+ and GTP...

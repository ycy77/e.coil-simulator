## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Involved in iron-sulfur (Fe-S) cluster assembly (PubMed:16603772, PubMed:19305405, PubMed:21799759, PubMed:25430730). May act as a regulator of Fe-S biogenesis (PubMed:19305405, PubMed:21799759, PubMed:25430730). Can bind both Fe(2+) and Fe(3+) ions (PubMed:15530368, PubMed:16603772, PubMed:17244611, PubMed:17651435). In vivo, has a positive effect on Fe-S cluster biogenesis under iron-rich growth conditions (PubMed:25430730). In vitro, can inhibit IscS cysteine desulfurase activity and the formation of Fe-S clusters on IscU (PubMed:19305405, PubMed:21799759). In vitro, in the presence of IscS and cysteine, Fe(3+)-CyaY can be used as an iron donor during Fe-S cluster assembly on the scaffold protein IscU (PubMed:16603772). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:15530368, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16603772, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17244611, ECO:0000269|PubMed:17651435, ECO:0000269|PubMed:19305405, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21799759, ECO:0000269|PubMed:25430730}. CyaY is a protein with similarity to human frataxin, mutations in which are responsible for the neurodegenerative disease Friedreich's ataxia. CyaY is involved in iron-sulfur cluster assembly, where its function is still not fully understood. Recent data support a regulatory role of CyaY, slowing the rate of Fe-S cluster formation by regulating CPLX0-248 IscS function...

## Enriched Summary

N-Linked glycans are polysaccharides or oligosaccharides that are linked to a protein via the nitrogen atom in the side chain of an asparagine residue. These asparagine residues are found in special sequences known as the sequon, which are composed of three consecutive amino acids that are recognized as the signal for the attachment of the N-glycan. The sequence of a sequon is either Asn-X-Ser or Asn-X-Thr, where X is any amino acid except for proline. N-glycans may be composed of N-acetyl galactosamine, galactose, neuraminic acid, N-acetylglucosamine, fructose, mannose, fucose and other monosaccharides.

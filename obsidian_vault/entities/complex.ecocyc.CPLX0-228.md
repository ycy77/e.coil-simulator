## Annotation

ArgR complexed with L-arginine represses the transcription of several genes involved in biosynthesis and transport of arginine, transport of histidine, and its own synthesis and activates genes for arginine catabolism . ArgR is also essential for a site-specific recombination reaction that resolves plasmid ColE1 multimers to monomers and is necessary for plasmid stability |CITS[3149585]|. The role of ArgR in this latter reaction may be structural |CITS[3149585]|. Inhibition of argR expression by CRISPRi reduced cellular fitness under treatment with the antibiotics carbonyl cyanide 3-chlorophenylhydrazone (CCCP), pyocyanin, rifampicin or sulfamethizole . The hexamer is the active form of ArgR, and L-arginine binds to ArgR and stabilizes the hexamer and increases its DNA affinity by allosteric activation . ArgR belongs to the ArgR/AhrC family, whose members are widely distributed in bacteria . The DNA sequence which the proteins of the family recognize and to which they bind are also well-conserved among genomes . This DNA sequence consists of a palindromic sequence of 18 bp, called the ARG box . An ArgR hexamer binds to a pair of these boxes that are generally separated by 3 bp . The repressor can bind to a single box but with a greatly reduced affinity . The ARG box generally overlaps the promoter sequence and the transcriptional start site ; therefore, it was suggested that it represses the transcription by steric exclusion of RNA polymerase binding . By using synthetic gene circuits, it was suggested that transcription activation or repression by ArgR depends on the position of its binding site relative to the promoter .

## Enriched Summary

Polyketides are secondary metabolites produced by some bacteria, fungi, plants, and animals. Polyketides are usually biosynthesized by Polyketide-Synthases "polyketide synthase" (PKS) enzymes through the decarboxylative condensation of extender units that are derived from malonyl-CoA, in a process similar to fatty acid synthesis. PKSs can be divided to two types: multifunctional polyproteins (type I) and discrete protein (type II). Type I PKSs are organized into modular complexes, each catalyzing individual chain elongation steps using an associated carrier protein. The minimal type I PKS module consists of 3 domains: a ketosynthase, a acyltransferase, and an acyl-carrier protein (ACP). The acyltransferase domain catalyzes the priming of the flanking ACP domain with the appropriate substrate. Type II PKS ACPs, on the other hand, possess catalytic self-acylation activity .

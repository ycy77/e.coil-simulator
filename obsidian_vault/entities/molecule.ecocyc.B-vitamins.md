## Enriched Summary

B vitamins are a class of eight water-soluble vitamins that play important roles in cell metabolism. The B vitamins were classified together because all of them serve as either enzyme cofactors, cosubstrates in important metabolic reactions, or as precursors for cofactors or cosubstrates. The different B vitamins are chemically distinct compounds that do not resemble one another. The dietary supplements known as "B complexes" contain all eight of these compounds.

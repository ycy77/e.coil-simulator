## Enriched Summary

The lanthanide (or lanthanoid) series is a group of 15 elements that have atomic numbers 57 to 71. This group starts from lanthanum and it ends at lutetium. All lanthanoids except for lutetium are f-block elements, meaning that electrons fill the 4f electron shell, except for lutetium which is a d-block lanthanoid. They also belong to the rare earth elements, along with the non-lanthanoids Scandium and Yttrium. Lanthanoids are metals. Most of them slowly turn into their hydroxides when they are placed into water, like the alkali metals. When placed in the air they usually form a coating of oxide like most metals.

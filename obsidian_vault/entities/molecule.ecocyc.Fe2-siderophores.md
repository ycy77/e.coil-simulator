## Enriched Summary

This entry stands for siderophores-bound FE+2 cation(s). Siderophores are small, high-affinity iron chelating compounds secreted by microorganisms. Once outside the cell, siderophores bind FE+3 with high affinity, and the iron-siderophore complexes are imported back into the cell by active-transport mechanisms. Siderophores are very common in pathogenic bacteria, since iron in the host organism is usually tightly bound to proteins, resulting in an extremely low concentration of free iron in the blood. Most siderophores belong to one of three major groups: the Catecholate-siderophores "catechols", Hydroxamate-siderophores "hydroxamates" and Thiazoline-siderophores "thiazolines".

## Enriched Summary

Amidine compounds are derivatives of carboxylic acids (R-COOH) in which the hydroxy group is replaced by an amino or a modified amino group and the oxo group is replaced by R=N. The most common amidines in organic chemistry are Carboxamidine carboxamidines (RC(=NR)NR2).

## Enriched Summary

The histo-blood group antigen precursor disaccharides are found at the terminal end of oligosaccharide chains attached to proteins or sphingolipids, and serve as the actual substrates for various glycosyltransferases that terminate the chain elongation process, forming the ABH or Lewis antigens. Five types of precursor disaccharides are known to carry ABH activity - type 1 through type 4 and type 6. Type 1 precursors are widely expressed on endodermally-derived tissues such as lining epithelia and glandular epithelia. Type 2 precursors are found mainly on ecto- or mesodermally-derived tissues, including erythrocytes . Type 3 and 4 precursors are found on ecto- or endodermally-derived tissues, including the salivary glands and kidneys . ## Annotation

The histo-blood group antigen precursor disaccharides are found at the terminal end of oligosaccharide chains attached to proteins or sphingolipids, and serve as the actual substrates for various glycosyltransferases that terminate the chain elongation process, forming the ABH or Lewis antigens. Five types of precursor disaccharides are known to carry ABH activity - type 1 through type 4 and type 6. Type 1 precursors are widely expressed on endodermally-derived tissues such as lining epithelia and glandular epithelia. Type 2 precursors are found mainly on ecto- or mesodermally-derived tissues, including erythrocytes . Type 3 and 4 precursors are found on ecto- or endodermally-derived tissues, including the salivary glands and kidneys . Since ABH antigens are present not only on erythrocytes, but also on various organs such as the salivary glands and kidneys , they are called histo-blood group ABH antigens.

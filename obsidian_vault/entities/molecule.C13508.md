## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: Sulfoquinovosyldiacylglycerol; SQDG; 1,2-Diacyl-3-(6-sulfo-alpha-D-quinovosyl)-sn-glycerol EcoCyc common name: a 1,2-diacyl-3-(6-sulfo-α-D-quinovosyl)-sn-glycerol. SULFOQUINOVOSYLDIACYLGLYCEROL "Sulfoquinovosyl diacylglycerol" (SQDG) is an abundant class of sulfolipids composed of a glycoside of CPD-10247 and 1-2-Diglycerides diacylglycerol, which are found in many photosynthetic organisms, including all photosynthetic plants, algae, cyanobacteria, and purple sulfur and non-sulfur bacteria. SQDG is synthesized from UDP-SULFOQUINOVOSE by the action of EC-2.4.1.M2. The synthesis of SQDG is not crucial under normal growth conditions. It only becomes important when phosphate is limited . Under such conditions, the increase of SQDG levels compensates for the decrease in phospholipids and ensures a constant proportion of anionic lipids in photosynthetic membranes . SQDG is localized in photosynthetic membranes (in plants in the thylakoid membranes), where it is closely associated with certain membrane proteins.

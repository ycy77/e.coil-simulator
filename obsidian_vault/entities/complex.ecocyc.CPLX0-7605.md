## Annotation

The transcription factor IclR, for "Isocitrate lyase Regulator," is negatively autoregulated and it regulates the expression of the glyoxylate bypass operon . Transcription of this operon is induced when E. coli is grown during acetate accumulation in the exponential phase. While glyoxylate favors the inactive dimeric state of IclR, pyruvate increases the binding of IclR to the aceBp promoter by stabilizing the active tetrameric form of the protein . Perhaps the IclR binding at both sites forms an intermolecular bridge that leads to a DNA loop structure and thus enhances the aceBAK repression . This regulator belongs to the IclR family of repressors. IclR is composed of two domains: the amino-terminal domain, which contains the DNA-binding region, and the carboxy-terminal domain, which is responsible for inducer binding .

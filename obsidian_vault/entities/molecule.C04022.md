## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: S,S-Dimethyl-beta-propiothetin; S-Dimethylsulfonium propionic acid; Dimethylsulfoniopropionate; Dimethylpropiothetin; DMPT; DMSP EcoCyc common name: dimethylsulfoniopropanoate. Dimethylsulfoniopropanoate (DMSP), an osmolyte of many marine algae and certain plants , is also the main source for CPD-7670 (DMS) in the marine environment . It was first identified in 1948 in the red alga TAX-1261582 , and is found in the highest concentrations in TAX-2864 (dinoflagellates) and TAX-2608131 (haptophytes). However, DMSP is produced by many more organisms including diatoms, green algae, some higher plants like TAX-4547 (sugarcane) and the coastal angiosperms TAX-15853 and TAX-101473, bacteria, and some coral species . Marine phytoplankton synthesize DMSP not only as an osmolyte, but also as a predator deterrent and an antioxidant . The degradation of DMSP to DMS and subsequent exchange of DMS across the ocean-atmosphere boundary is the main natural source of sulfur to the atmosphere.

## Annotation

The YajL protein functions as a covalent chaperone for sulfenylated proteins . YajL forms mixed disulfides with cellular proteins in response to oxidative stress; its cellular targets include a number or ribosomal proteins as well as members of the cellular thiol proteome, including aconitase and Fe-S cluster-containing subunits of dehydrogenases . Free YajL can be regenerated by reduced glutathione or dihydrolipoamide, but not thioredoxin . YajL has now been identified as a protein deglycase that repairs glyoxal- and methylglyoxal-glycated proteins . Glyoxalase III activity of YajL with glyoxal, but not methylglyoxal, has been measured . The glyoxalase activity may in fact reflect the protein deglycase activity of the enzyme . Crystal structures of YajL have been solved at 1.1 Å and 0.98 Å resolution. YajL shows similarity in the overall structure and in predicted functional sites to the human oncogene and neuroprotective protein DJ-1, a protein deglycase . YajL is a dimer in solution; the C47 residue is required for dimerization . The conserved C106 residue is important for the formation of mixed disulfides . The length of the Glu14-Asp23 intersubunit hydrogen bond has been studied . YajL was mistakenly identified as ThiJ and was implicated in thiamine biosynthesis. However, later experiments showed that the original complementation phenotype was due to the neighboring gene, thiI . A yajL mutant is more sensitive to oxidative stress than wild type; it accumulates aggregated proteins under aerobic conditions , shows altered ribosome profiles and increased frameshifting after oxidative stress , and contains higher levels of sulfenylated and glycated proteins than wild type. Viability of a yajL mutant is severely decreased when grown in LB medium containing 0.6% glucose

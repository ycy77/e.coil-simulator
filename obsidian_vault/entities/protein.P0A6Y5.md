## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Redox regulated molecular chaperone. Protects both thermally unfolding and oxidatively damaged proteins from irreversible aggregation. Plays an important role in the bacterial defense system toward oxidative stress. hslO encodes the zinc-dependent, redox regulated chaperone known as Hsp33. Hsp33 is thought to function as a 'holdase' - a chaperone that binds tightly to unfolding proteins under stress conditions with subsequent release to chaperone 'foldases' (such as the DnaK system) when non-stress conditions resume. hslO expression is induced by heat shock (7 mins at 50°C); expression is stimulated by Eσ32 in an in vitro transcription-translation system . Purified Hsp33 suppresses thermal aggregation of the model substrates citrate synthase and firefly luciferase in vitro; purified Hsp33 exists in an equilibrium between reduced and oxidized species; chaperone activity is activated under oxidizing conditions . Hsp33 contains 6 cysteine residues, 4 of which are conserved and are implicated in zinc coordination under reducing conditions; two intramolecular disulfide bonds are formed between the conserved cysteines Cys230 and Cys232, and Cys263 and Cys266 upon H2O2 oxidation .

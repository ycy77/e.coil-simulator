## Enriched Summary

Luciferin (from the Latin lucifer, "light-bringer") is a generic term for the substrates of the luciferase enzymes, which are found in organisms that generate bioluminescence. Luciferins typically undergo an enzyme-catalyzed oxidation and the resulting excited state intermediate emits light upon decaying to its ground state.

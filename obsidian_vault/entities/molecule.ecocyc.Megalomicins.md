## Enriched Summary

The megalomicins, which were discovered in 1968 , are a family of related macrolide antibiotics produced by the soil bacterium TAX-47868. They are therapeutically diverse compounds that possess antiparasitic, antiviral and antibacterial properties. Like other macrolide antibiotics, they are more active against Gram-positive bacteria than against Gram-negative bacteria . The first family member to have its structure reported was CPD-12966 . Acyl derivatives of CPD-12966, including CPD-14689, CPD-12967, CPD-14691, and CPD-14690, were soon discovered . The megalomicins have the same aglycone as CPD-13804 and CPD-13951, but include a unique deoxyamino sugar, CPD-14692, at the C-6 hydroxyl.

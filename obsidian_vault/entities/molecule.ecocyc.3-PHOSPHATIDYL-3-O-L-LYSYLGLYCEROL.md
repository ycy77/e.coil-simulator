## Enriched Summary

Lysyl-phosphatidylglycerol (LPG) is a well-known membrane lipid in several Gram-positive bacteria like TAX-1280, TAX-1423, TAX-1773 and TAX-1365 , and has also been reported in some Gram-negative bacteria, such as TAX-398 . By increasing the positive charge on membrane lipids, LPG affects resistance of the organism to many cationic antimicrobial peptides such as moenomycin, vancomycin, methicillin, oxacillin, bacitracin, gentamicin, beta-lactams, and human defensins, as well as synthetic cationic peptides .

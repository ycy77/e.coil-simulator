## Enriched Summary

Proanthocyanidins are a class of polyphenols found in a variety of plants. They are flavonoid oligomers obtained by the condensation of two or more units of Flavanols flavanols. Many proanthocyanidins are oligomers of CPD-1961 and CPD-7630 and their gallic acid esters. More complex polyphenols, having the same polymeric building block, form the group of tannins. The structure shown here is for illustration only, and shows a generic dimeric proanthocyanidin. Natural procyanidins are highly diverse in size and composition, and the monomers are usually lined up by C-4/C-8 or C-4/C-6 linkages .

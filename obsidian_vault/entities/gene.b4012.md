## Enriched Summary

yjaB (gene.b4012) is a gene entity. It encodes yjaB (protein.P09163). Encoded protein function: FUNCTION: N-epsilon-lysine acetyltransferase that catalyzes acetylation of a large number of proteins (PubMed:30352934). Binds acetyl-CoA (PubMed:19343803). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:19343803, ECO:0000269|PubMed:30352934}. EcoCyc product frame: EG11207-MONOMER. Genomic coordinates: 4213680-4214123. EcoCyc protein note: YjaB is an Nε-lysine acetyltransferase that targets 171 unique lysine residues in 128 proteins . Overexpression of YjaB in an acetylation-gutted strain (Δ pta patZ acs cobB) leads to the appearance of acetylated proteins in an anti-acetyllysine Western blot. Mutagenesis of a predicted catalytic residue in YjaB, Y117A, eliminates the acetylation signal observed with the wild-type protein . An NMR solution structure of apo-YjaB has been generated, showing a structure typical for Gcn5-related N-acetyltransferases (GNATs). Addition of acetyl-CoA caused chemical shifts in the NMR structure, indicating that YjaB is able to bind acetyl-CoA . A yjaB insertion mutant is viable . Review:

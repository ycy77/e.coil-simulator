## Enriched Summary

Glucuronoxylans Glucuronoxylans are linear polymers of β-D-xylopyranosyl units linked by (1→4) glycosidic bonds, with many of the xylose units substituted with 2, 3 or 2,3-linked galacturonate residues. Many of the D-glucuronosyl units are methylated at position 4. Glucuronoarabinoxylans, typically found in softwood, have the same xylan backbone as in Glucuronoxylans glucuronoxylans , but about one of ten β-D-xylopyranosyl resudue is substituted by α-L-arabinofuranosyl at position 3. Unlike glucuronoxylans, glucuronoarabinoxylans are not acetylated .

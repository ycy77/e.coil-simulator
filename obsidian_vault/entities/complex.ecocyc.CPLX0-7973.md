## Enriched Summary

ZapD interacts with the C-terminal tail of FtsZ and promotes FtsZ polymer bundling. Localization of ZapD to the mid-cell is dependent on FtsZ . ZapD interacts with the C-terminal variable region (CTV) of FtsZ. The FtsZ K380 residue facilitates a conformation that enables optimal contact with ZapD . A structural model of how ZapD promotes FtsZ bundling has been proposed . ZapD has been crystallized . The protein was shown to be a dimer in solution ; the crystal structure shows a symmetrical dimer with β-sheet domains flanking centrally located α-helices . Site-directed mutagenesis showed that the residues R56, R116, R221, and R225 are important for FtsZ filament bundling and/or morphology . Sites within FtsZ that are important for ZapD binding were identified by site-directed mutagenesis . A crystal structure of the C-terminal domain peptide of FtsZ bound to ZapD has been solved at 2.67 Å resolution. The Phe377 residue of FtsZ anchors it within a binding pocket in ZapD . A zapD null mutant has no discernable cell division defect, while a zapD ftsZ84 (Ts) and zapD zapA double mutants have more severe cell division phenotypes than the single mutants alone.

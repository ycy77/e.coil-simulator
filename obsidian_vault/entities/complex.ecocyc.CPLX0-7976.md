## Enriched Summary

TamA and TamB form a heterooligomeric complex that enables efficient secretion of the adhesion protein G7080-MONOMER " Ag43" . The complex has been named the 'translocation and assembly module' (TAM) . TAM may facilitate the movement of phospholipids between the inner and outer membrane . TamA is an integral outer membrane protein and TamB is an integral inner membrane protein and it is thought that the TAM complex may span the periplasmic space (see also ). Protein complexes, immunoprecipitated with TamB antiserum contain both TamA and TamB proteins . Mutant strains lacking tamA and tamB fail to secrete the 60kDa Ag43 subunit and accumulate unprocessed Ag43 (110 kDa) in the periplasm . Overexpression of Ag43 from a plasmid results in the aggregation of wild type cells. This phenotype is not seen in mutant strains lacking tamA and tamB but can be restored by the presence of a plasmid expressing the tamA and tamB genes .

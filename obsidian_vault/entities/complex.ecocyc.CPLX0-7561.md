## Enriched Summary

ChpB and ChpS are two matching elements of a toxin-antitoxin system. ChpB in the absence of ChpS leads to growth inhibition. Conversely, the presence of matching ChpS allows normal growth. Thus, the normal arrangement of paired ChpB and ChpS is nontoxic . A certain amount of mixing and matching with other toxin-antitoxin systems works with the ChpB-ChpS system. ChpS can negate the Kid toxin of the plasmid R1 parD toxin-antitoxin system. Similarly, the plasmid R100 PemI protein can block ChpB toxicity, although ChpS cannot reciprocally block PemK-induced toxicity . The chpSB locus is weakly autoregulated, possibly via a motif in the region preceding chpS . A complete deletion of the chpSB locus is viable .

## Annotation

Rho is required for one of the two major types of termination of RNA transcription. It is a hexameric helicase that binds to nascent RNA transcripts and halts transcription by displacing the template DNA. Results from single-particle cryo-EM analysis of "peri-termination" complexes supported a model of transcription termination where Rho is recruited via interactions with the RNA polymerase, NusA and NusG before interacting with the nascent RNA . Structural analysis reported by Molodtsov et al. revealed that a pretermination complex consists of a closed-ring Rho hexamer . This complex contains RNA that is threaded through the central channel of Rho . Specifically, 60 nucleotides of RNA interact with the outer surface of Rho, while 6 nucleotides of RNA interact with the central channel of Rho . The positioning of Rho oriented to RNAP allows for ATP-dependent translocation by Rho to exert a mechanical force on RNAP . Moreover, NusG acts as a bridge between Rho and RNAP within this complex . Rho was identified as a putative phosphohistidine acceptor using chemoproteomics analysis with a stable analogue of 3-pHis . Rho is required for termination of transcription of many RNAs . Rho is also a global regulator of gene expression, being especially important in downregulation of a number of phage-derived and laterally acquired genes, most likely via termination of transcription ahead of those genes. Rho is important in preventing the toxic effects of these "passenger" genes . Rho also terminates transcription of antisense RNA in regions where RNA polymerase-binding RNA aptamers (RAPs) are enriched. In this way, Rho preserves sense transcription .

## Annotation

The regulatory protein CpxR appears to control at least 100 operons . Some of these operons are involved in cellular functions, such as "conjugative plasmid gene expression" (cpx) , the envelope stress response system, pilus assembly, secretion, motility and chemotaxis, adherence, biofilm development, multidrug resistance and efflux , acid stress and the copper-responsive regulatory system , among others. In pathogenic bacteria it controls genes involved in invasion of host cells . Sometimes, CpxR acts to modulate the action of the main activators or repressors of some promoters . CpxR is involved in the cellular response to gentamicin by directly repressing genes encoding respiratory complexes, leading to membrane depolarization and reduced gentamicin uptake . A transposon insertion mutation in the cpxR gene produces cellular susceptibility to the antibiotic sulfamethoxazole . On the other hand, inhibition of cpxR expression by CRISPRi enhanced cellular fitness under treatment with the antibiotic carbonyl cyanide 3-chlorophenylhydrazone (CCCP), but reduced it under treatment with polymyxin B, puromycin or phleomycin, . A knockout mutant in cpxR gene showed increased sensitivity to gentamicin . The strong transcription activation by CpxR appears to be carried out by stabilization and acceleration of the RNApol, but the weak activation could be due to stabilization of RNApol and the slow rate of promoter escape . Consistent with a broader principle observed for many transcription factors, CpxR-mediated regulation is compatible with primary RNAP stabilization at promoters (even when net repression is observed), with fold-changes scaling inversely with promoter basal activity and buffering promoter-to-promoter variability .

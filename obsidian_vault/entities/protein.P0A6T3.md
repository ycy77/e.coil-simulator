## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Catalyzes the transfer of the gamma-phosphate of ATP to D-galactose to form alpha-D-galactose-1-phosphate (Gal-1-P). To a lesser extent, is also able to phosphorylate 2-deoxy-D-galactose and D-galactosamine. Is not able to use D-galacturonic acid, D-talose, L-altrose, and L-glucose as substrates. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00246, ECO:0000269|PubMed:12816414, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14612558, ECO:0000269|PubMed:200486}. Galactokinase (GalK) catalyzes the first step in the Leloir pathway of galactose metabolism . The substrate specificity of GalK is high; however, a single amino acid change, Y371H, leads to substantial kinase activity with a variety of substrates . The 3-D structure of GalK has been modeled . Transcriptional and post-transcriptional regulation of the gal locus is complex. A galK-encoding mRNA species, named mK2, appears to be generated by RNase P cleavage of a longer transcript . Translation of GalK is independently downregulated by SPF-RNA , likely due to degradation of the galK-specific mK2 mRNA that is generated by RNase P cleavage . Spot 42 also affects polarity of gene expression; its binding site overlaps with a putative CPLX0-2441 Rho-binding site and may enhance Rho-mediated transcription termination .

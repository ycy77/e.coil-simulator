## Enriched Summary

Cardanols are a group of related phenols obtained from Anacardic-Acid "anacardic acids", a common component of cashew nutshells with anitibacterial activity, that consists of a CPD-110 core substituted by saturated or unsaturated alkyl chains of 15-17 carbons. Heating anacardic acids results in decarboxylation and their conversion to cardanols without destroying their activity. The most common form of cardanol is CPD-14641.

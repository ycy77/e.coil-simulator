## Enriched Summary

## Annotation

HdeB is a periplasmic acid stress chaperone. Both HdeB and CPLX0-801 "HdeA" are required for optimal protection against acid stress in vivo . HdeB and HdeA form mixed aggregates with proteins that have aggregated under acid stress conditions and assist in solubilization and renaturation upon return to neutral pH; HdeB and HdeA decrease the exposed hydrophobicity of aggregated periplasmic proteins . HdeB has optimal in vitro chaperone activity at pH 4; HdeB dimers bind denatured client proteins, protect against acid induced aggregation and facilitate refolding upon neutralization . Overproduction of HdeB stabilises a highly unstable MBP mutant; purified HdeB inhibits the aggregation of lactate dehydrogenase at pH 7.5 HdeB is dimeric at neutral pH but forms monomers at low pH (pH 2-3) . HdeB remains dimeric at pH 4 . HdeB adopts a well-folded dimer structure at neutral pH, starts to undergo partial unfolding near pH 3 and reaches a generally unfolded state at pH 2-1.5; activation of HdeB chaperone function may be linked to pH dependent protein dynamics rather than to the large scale structural change (dimer to monomer transition) associated with activation of HdeA (see also ). The HdeB monomer contains two 12 residue alpha helices plus three shorter (4-5 residue) alpha helices separated by interconnecting loops . HdeB has structural similarity to HdeA . HdeB copurifies with HdeA . HdeB and HdeA do not appear to heterodimerize . Transcription from the hdeAB promoter can be initiated by both σ38- and σ70-containing RNA polymerase .

## Enriched Summary

The IS3 insertion element transposase carries out transposition of IS3 in collaboration with the IS3 proteins InsE and InsF. The IS3 transposase carries out transposition of the insertion sequence, generating linear IS3 molecules with staggered ends featuring 5' overhangs . The mechanism of this transposition has been studied in detail . Though the transposase alone can carry out deletions, the other IS3 proteins appear to be required for full transposition . As in many insertion sequences, IS3 codes for three products, one of which is generated by a -1 frameshift that allows readthrough on the first stop codon, resulting in a superproduct that includes most of the first gene in the element. This frameshift occurs in an AAAAG motif in the latter end of the insE gene and yields the IS3 transposase . The IS3 frameshift motif has been used in experiments studying the basis of regulatory translational frameshifts . The insertion of an IS3 element upstream from a gene can lead to enhanced transcription of that gene due to a "Pribnow box" (CAATTT) and a -35 region (TTGGTC) toward the end of insF, which effectively make IS3 a mobile promoter . IS3 elements have been found in 4-6 copies across many E. coli genomes .

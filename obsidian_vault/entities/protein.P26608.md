## Enriched Summary

Flagellar secretion chaperone FliS FliS, along with FliT and FlgN, are cytoplasmic, substrate-specific chaperones of the flagellar export system. They share several similarities with other type III cytoplasmic chaperone family members . Mutation-deletion studies have shown that FliS is the chaperone for FliC or flagellin, the subunit which polymerizes to form the filament of the flagellar structure . FliS has also been shown to be responsible for negatively regulating the export of FlgM, the anti-sigma factor . Co-purification and affinity blotting studies showed that FliS binds to the C-terminal 40 amino acid region of the D0 domain of FliC which is the polymerization domain and without FliS binding the C-terminus is degraded. This supports the role of FliS as a domain-specific chaperone that prevents degradation of FliC as well as premature self-aggregation .

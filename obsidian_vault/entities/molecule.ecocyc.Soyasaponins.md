## Enriched Summary

Saponins are a class of secondary metabolites that have been grouped together phenomenologically by the foaming they produce when shaken in aqueous solutions, and structurally by their composition of one or more hydrophilic glycoside moieties combined with a lipophilic triterpene derivative. They are particularly abundant in various plant species, but are also found in marine organisms. Soyasaponins are saponins found in soy beans. The aglycones of these compounds are Soyasapogenols soyasapogenols. Triterpene saponins have diverse biological actions including antiinflammatory, antifungal, antimicrobial, antiparasitic, and antitumor activities.

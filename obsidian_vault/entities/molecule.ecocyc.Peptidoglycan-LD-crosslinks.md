## Enriched Summary

Transpeptidases cross link glycan strands within newly synthesized peptidoglycan polymers and between newly made material and preexisting peptidoglycan; they also link peptidoglycan to Braun's lipoprotein (Lpp) in the outer membrane and catalyze the exchange of carboxy terminal D-alanine residues with glycine in the peptide stem. DD-transpeptidases catalyze the cross-linking of the glycan strands between the D-alanine at position 4 of a pentapeptide stem and the meso-diaminopimelyl or lysyl residue at position 3 of a different side-chain. LD transpeptidases catalyze the cross linking between two meso-diaminopimelyl or lysyl residues of separate side-chains.

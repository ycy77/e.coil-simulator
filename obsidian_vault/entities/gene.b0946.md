## Enriched Summary

zapC (gene.b0946) is a gene entity. It encodes zapC (protein.P75862). Encoded protein function: FUNCTION: Contributes to the efficiency of the cell division process by stabilizing the polymeric form of the cell division protein FtsZ. Acts by promoting interactions between FtsZ protofilaments and suppressing the GTPase activity of FtsZ. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00906, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21216995, ECO:0000269|PubMed:21216997}. EcoCyc product frame: G6486-MONOMER. EcoCyc synonyms: ycbW. Genomic coordinates: 1005952-1006494. EcoCyc protein note: ZapC is a cell division protein that binds FtsZ, promotes lateral interactions/bundling between FtsZ protofilaments and suppresses the GTPase activity of FtsZ . ZapC interacts with the globular core of FtsZ ; reports disagree on whether or not ZapC also interacts with the C-terminal tail of FtsZ. A structural model suggests that ZapC bridges two FtsZ protofilaments, thereby enhancing bundling . Localization of ZapC to the Z ring is dependent on FtsZ and independent of FtsA, ZipA, ZapA or ZapB . Crystal structures of ZapC have been solved , showing an N-terminal α/β region and a C-terminal twisted β barrel-like domain. Residues within a basic pocket in the C-terminal domain are involved in the interaction with FtsZ . Reports disagree on whether ZapC is a monomer or a weak dimer in solution...

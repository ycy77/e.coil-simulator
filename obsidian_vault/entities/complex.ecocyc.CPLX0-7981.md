## Enriched Summary

YbdG is a low abundance mechanosensitive channel of miniconductance . Expression of ybdG at wild-type levels confers protection against mild hypoosmotic shock while over-expression of ybdG is able to protect against severe rapid shock . Miniconductance of the YbdG channel cannot be detected in standard patch clamp experiments; however a YbdG V229A mutant exhibits a high frequency of miniconductance channel activity . ybdG expression is stimulated by growth at high osmolarity . YbdG has a large membrane domain organised into 5 transmembrane (TM) regions. The 5th TM helix is homologous to the MscS pore-lining helix . Blue native PAGE and cross-linking experiments suggest a stoichiometry of 7 YbdG subunits per channel . Seven mechanosensitive ion channels have been characterised in E. coli K-12: CPLX0-7627 "MscL", EG11160-MONOMER "MscS", G6255-MONOMER "MscK", CPLX0-7985 "MscM", CPLX0-7982 "YnaI", CPLX0-7983 "YbiO" and YbdG.

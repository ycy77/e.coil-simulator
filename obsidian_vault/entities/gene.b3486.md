## Enriched Summary

rbbA (gene.b3486) is a gene entity. It encodes rbbA (protein.P37624). Encoded protein function: FUNCTION: Exhibits an intrinsic ATPase activity that is stimulated by both 70S ribosomes and 30S ribosomal subunits. Could be involved in protein-chain elongation and in release of deacyl-tRNA from ribosomes after peptide bond synthesis. Stimulates the synthesis of polyphenylalanine in vitro. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10607404, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11168361, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16495476}. EcoCyc product frame: YHIH-MONOMER. EcoCyc synonyms: yhiG, yhiH. Genomic coordinates: 3626803-3629538. EcoCyc protein note: RbbA is associated with the 30S subunit of the ribosome and has ribosome-dependent ATPase activity . Stimulation of protein synthesis by RbbA is dependent on ATP hydrolysis, and the ATPase activity is inhibited by hygromycin . The effect of hygromycin may be due to its ability to release RbbA from the ribosome . RbbA specifically interacts with EF-Tu and crosslinks to the 30S ribosomal subunit protein S1 . The E-site base A937 of 16S rRNA in the intact 70S ribosome is protected by a truncated form of RbbA . The truncated form of RbbA may be identical to the translation factor "Protein W" . RbbA also interacts with the membrane protein YhjD and certain lipopolysaccharide transport components...

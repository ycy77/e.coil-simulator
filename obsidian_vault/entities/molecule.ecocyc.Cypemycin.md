## Enriched Summary

Cypemycin is a modified peptide antibiotic produced by TAX-932694. It has potent activity against mouse leukemia cells . The peptide is classified as a member of the lantibiotic family of peptides mostly due to the presence of dehydrobutyrine (Dhb) and S-[(Z)-2-aminovinyl]-D-cysteine (Avi-Cys) residues. Six distinct posttranslational modifications participate in cypemycin biosynthesis, altering 9 of the 22 amino acids found in the mature peptide, including four threonine residues that are dehydrated to form Dhb, one cysteine that is dethiolated to form dehydroalanine, a C-terminal Cys that is oxidatively decarboxylated to form an enethiolate, two isoleucine residues that are isomerized to form L-allo-Isoleucine, and two N-methylations of the N-terminal alanine .

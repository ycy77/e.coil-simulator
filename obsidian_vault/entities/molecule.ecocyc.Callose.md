## Enriched Summary

Cell walls of higher plants contain callose, which is a linear homopolysaccharide made up 1-3-beta-D-Glucans with β-1,6 branches. Callose plays an important role in many processes associated with plant development and in response to environmental stimuli in the form of abiotic and biotic stresses. Callose is also a transitory component of cell plate involved in the formation of new walls during cell division. It is a structural component of plasmodesmatal canals, and is also found in developing cell walls of fibre cells and cotton seed hairs . The main enzyme in callose biosynthesis is callose synthase. The molecular mechanism underlying callose biosynthesis is still poorly understood. Molecular studies have revealed a set of genes in Arabidopsis genome that catalyze and degrade callose . 12 glucan synthase like (GSL) genes appear to be involved in male gametogenesis in Arabidopsis . Two genes, GSL1 and GSL5, are also known to play a role in pollen development and fertility . Callose is degraded by β-1,3-glucanases . See for a review.

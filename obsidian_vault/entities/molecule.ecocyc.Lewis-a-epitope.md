## Enriched Summary

The Lewis-a-epitope "Lewis a epitope" is formed by α(1,4) fucosylation of the N-acetylglucosaminyl residue of Core1, the disaccharide precursor that can also lead to formation of the Type-1-H-Antigen by α(1,2) fucosylation at its terminal galactosyl residue . The related CPD-8594 "Lewis x epitope" is formed by α(1,3) fucosylation of the N-acetylglucosaminyl residue of B-Gal-14-NacGlc-R. Both the Lewis-a-epitope and the CPD-8594 cannot be further transformed to ABH-type antigens .

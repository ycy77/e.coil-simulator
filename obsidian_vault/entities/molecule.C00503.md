## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: Erythritol; Erythrol; Phycitol; Phycite; Erythrite Erythritol is produced by microorganisms as an osmoprotectant, and occurs naturally in small quantities in mushrooms, fruits such as pears, grapes, and watermelons, and in fermented foods. Erythritol is a four-carbon polyol that is used a natural sweetener in the food and pharmaceutical industries. It exhibits about 70% of the relative sweetness of sucrose, and was approved as low calorie sweetener by the FDA in 2001. The majority of erythritol (ca. 90%) is not metabolized in the human body and is excreted into urine within 48 h after oral intake, resulting in an extremely low energy value (0.2 - 0.4 kcal/g) . Erythritol is produced on an industrial scale through the fermentation of sugars by some yeasts, such as TAX-64607 sp., . As of 2018, the world production of erythritol was about 60000 ton/year . Despite having two stereo centers, erythritol is a completely symmetric compound, and even though one could find literature that refers to D and L forms, they are in fact identical.

## Enriched Summary

Pyrones (also called pyranones) are a class of heterocyclic chemical compounds. They contain a six-membered heterocyclic, non-aromatic ring consisting of five carbon atoms and one oxygen atom and containing two double bonds and a ketone functional group. Most pyones belong to either of two isomers based on the CPD-11706 (2-pyrone) or CPD-26550 (4-pyrone) structure. The 2-pyrone structure common in nature as part of the Coumarins "coumarin" ring system, while the 4-pyrone structure is found in some natural chemical compounds such as CPD-26551, CPD-15508 and KOJIC-ACID. 3-Pyrone structures exist, but are far less common.

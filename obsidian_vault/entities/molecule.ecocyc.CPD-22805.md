## Enriched Summary

Microcin B17 is a linear azol(in)e-containing peptide (LAP) that functions as a narrow-spectrum bactericidal peptide. The peptide targets DNA gyrase . It is active mostly against Enterobacteriaceae and some Pseudomonas species, due to its limited ability to enter bacterial cells. The precursor peptide is encoded by the mcbA gene. All four CYS and four of the six SER residues are modified by EC-6.2.2.2 to azoline residues, which are further oxidized to azole rings. These modifications are catalyzed by a trimeric synthetase comprising McbB, McbC, and McbD (substrate recognition module, dehydrogenase, and YcaO proteins, respectively). Following generation of the azole rings the leader region is removed by a protease (tldD/tldE) encoded on the chromosome of E. coli. After complete processing, a two-component ABC transporter (McbE/McbF) exports the mature microcin B17 while also providing a level of self-protection. Microcin B17 is imported into target cells by two membrane proteins - the outer membrane protein OmpF and the plasma membrane protein SmbA. Once inside the cells, the peptide inhibits DNA gyrase and triggers double strand breaks in DNA.

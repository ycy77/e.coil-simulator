## Enriched Summary

Chlorophylls Chlorophyll is a green photosynthetic pigment found in photosynthetic plants, algae, and bacteria. Its name is derived from ancient Greek: chloros = green and phyllon = leaf. Chlorophyll functions as the main light harvesting pigment in photosynthesis, capturing photons and transfering them to the reaction centers. Chlorophyll absorbs mostly in the blue and, to a lesser extent, red portions of the electromagnetic spectrum, hence its intense green color. There are several variants of chlorophyll (CHLOROPHYLL-A, CPD-10334, CHLOROPHYLL-B, CPD-18895, CPD-10336, CPD-10337, CPD-10335, CPD-18896, CPD-18897 and CPD-18898). While the chlorophyl a found in most organisms is esterified with a PHYTOL side-chain, some organisms, such as the green sulfur bacterium TAX-1097, produce a chlorophyll a that is esterified with a CPD-18893 side-chain.

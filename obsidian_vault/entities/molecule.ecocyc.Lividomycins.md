## Enriched Summary

The lividomycins are a family of Aminoglycoside-Antibiotics "aminoglycosidic antibiotics" produced by the bacterium TAX-282216. The lividomycins were discovered In Japan in the early 70s . Like other aminoglucoside antibiotics, it inhibits protein synthesis by binding to 16S ribosomal RNA . Despite promising initial results against urinary tract infections , tuberculosis and other respiratory tract infections , lividomycin never became widely used.

## Enriched Summary

The carbon-phosphorus lyase core complex consists of the PhnG, PhnH, PhnI and PhnJ subunits . A crystal structure of the complex has been solved at 1.7 Å resolution. The crystal structure revealed a symmetrical hetero-octamer consisting of a compact central tetramer comprised of PhnI and PhnG subunits, each interacting individually with the distal PhnJ and PhnH subunits . Curiously, the site of the Fe4S4 cluster, which is likely required for radical formation, is located at a 30 Å distance from a glycine residue in PhnJ which is the location of a radical between enzymatic turnover cycles . A zinc binding site shared between PhnI (His328 and His333) and PhnJ (His108) observed in the crystal structure is functionally important . The core complex was initially shown to stably associate with PHNK-MONOMER PhnK .

## Enriched Summary

Dermatan-Sulfate "Dermatan sulfate" is a glycosaminoglycan found mostly in skin but also in blood vessels, the heart valves, tendons, and the lungs. Dermatan sulfate is composed of repeating disaccharide units composed of a hexose sugar and a hexuronic acid, many of which sulfated. The most common disaccharide unit in dermatan sulfate is composed of CPD-12515 and CPD-12557 (many are sulfated) linked by β(1 -> 3) linkages. This compound describes CPD-12557 residues sulfated at both the 4' and 6' positions within the context of Dermatan-Sulfate.

## Enriched Summary

MoaB is part of the moaABCDE operon, whose products participate in the synthesis of the molybdenum cofactor . However, the role of MoaB has yet been discovered. Inactivation of the moaB gene coincides with reduced biofilm biomass accumulation . Although MoaB shows sequence similarity to MogA, expression of MoaB does not complement the lack of nitrate reductase activity of a mogA mutant and does not have molybdopterin adenylyltransferase activity in vitro . Neither in-frame deletion nor overexpression of moaB has an effect on chlorate resistance or 6-N-hydroxylaminopurine (HAP) sensitivity of E. coli, indicating that MoaB does not play a role in molybdenum cofactor biosynthesis . The moaB mutant from the Keio collection, which carries a kanamycin resistance cassette and thus likely has a polar effect on the downstream genes, shows a defect in tellurate reductase activity . MoaB binds GTP and has GTPase activity in vitro that is stimulated by the presence of Mn2+ ions . Crystal structures of MoaB have been determined to 2.1 Å and 1.6 Å resolution. MoaB is a hexamer in solution; the crystal structure shows that the complex is composed of a dimer of trimers .

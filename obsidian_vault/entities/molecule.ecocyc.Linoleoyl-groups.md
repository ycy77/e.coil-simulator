## Enriched Summary

This compound class describes a linoleoyl group that is incorporated within a lipid molecule. LINOLEIC_ACID Linoleate (linoleic acid) is an ω-6 polyunsaturated fatty acid with an 18-carbon chain and two cis double bonds at positions 9 and 12. Linoleate is produced by many organisms, including bacteria, plants, fungi and some insects. It is abundant in many vegetable oils, especially safflower and sunflower oils. While mammals require linoleate, which is found in their membranes and is used as the precursor for production of CPD-8117, ARACHIDONIC_ACID, Prostaglandins prostaglandins, Leukotrienes leukotrienes, and Thromboxanes thromboxanes, they are not able to produce it since they lack EC-1.14.19.6. Thus linoleate is an essential fatty acid, and must be obtained through the diet. Like other fatty acids, LINOLEIC_ACID is rarely found in its free form.

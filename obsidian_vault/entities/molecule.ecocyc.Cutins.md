## Enriched Summary

The cuticle is one of four hydrophobic macromolecular assemblies (along with Suberins suberin, Lignins lignin and Sporopollenins sporopollenin) that enabled the terrestrialization of vascular plants. The cuticle covers all of the aerial surfaces of plants and fulfills multiple functions, e.g., control of water and gas exchanges, defense signaling against biotic and abiotic stresses, protection against UV radiation, retention of environmental pollutants, and induction of responses to mechanical stimuli . The main component of the cuticle is cutin, a waxy polymer that consists of OMEGA-HYDROXY-FATTY-ACID "ω-hydroxy fatty acids" and their derivatives, interlinked via ester bonds, and also contains cuticular waxes. It is insoluble and provides awaterproof layer. There are two major families of cutin monomers, comprising C16 and C18 compounds. The C16 family consists mainly of 16-HYDROXYPALMITATE and CPD-17653 and 1016-DIHYDROXYPALMITATE.

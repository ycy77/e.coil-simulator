## Enriched Summary

Fatty-Aldehydes "Fatty aldehydes" are aldehydes that are formed by the reduction of the carboxylic acid group of Fatty-Acids fatty acids. Many enzymes that act on fatty acids, as well as the corresponding Fatty-Acyl-CoAs "fatty acyl-CoAs", Fatty-Alcohols alcohols, and Fatty-Aldehydes aldehydes, have evolved to recognize substrates with a limited range of chain lengths. To classify these enzymes, it is helpful to divide the substrates into smaller groups based on their chain length. The following subgroups have been defined: Short-Chain-Aldehydes "Short-chain fatty aldehydes" are derived from Short-chain-fatty-acids "fatty acids that have 3-5 carbons in the longest chain". Medium-Chain-Aldehydes "Medium-chain fatty aldehydes" are derived from Medium-chain-fatty-acids "fatty acids that have 6-12 carbons in the longest chain". Long-Chain-Aldehydes "Long-chain fatty aldehydes" are derived from Long-Chain-Fatty-Acids "fatty acids that have 13-22 carbons in the longest chain". Very-Long-Chain-Aldehydes "Very-long-chain fatty aldehydes" are derived from Very-long-chain-fatty-acids "fatty acids that have 23-27 carbons in the longest chain". Ultra-Long-Chain-Aldehydes "Ultra-long-chain fatty aldehydes" are derived from Ultra-long-chain-fatty-acids "fatty acids that have more than 27 carbons in the longest chain"...

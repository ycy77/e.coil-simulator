## Enriched Summary

Sphingoids (sphingoid bases, or long chain bases) are building blocks of sphingolipids. They are amino alcohols, composed predominantly of 18 carbon atoms. They are characterized by the presence of a hydroxyl group at C1 and C3, and an amine group at C2. The commonly known sphingoids include sphingosine, sphinganine (dihydrosphingosine), and phytosphingosine (4-hydroxysphinganine). Sphingoid bases can be phosphorylated at C1.

## Enriched Summary

SlmA is a cell division inhibitor that is required for nucleoid exclusion . By simultaneously binding chromosomal DNA and FtsZ and thereby disrupting normal FtsZ polymerization near the nucleoid, SlmA derails Z-ring formation to effect nucleoid exclusion . The site responsible for disrupting FtsZ polymerization maps to a region that is occluded by the DNA-binding domain in apo-SlmA . DNA-activated oligomerization of SlmA prevents FtsZ protofilament propagation and bundling .

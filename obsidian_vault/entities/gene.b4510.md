## Enriched Summary

rzoD (gene.b4510) is a gene entity. It encodes rzoD (protein.P58041). Encoded protein function: FUNCTION: Component of the spanin complex that disrupts the outer membrane and causes cell lysis during virus exit. The spanin complex conducts the final step in cell lysis by disrupting the outer membrane after holin and endolysin action have permeabilized the inner membrane and degraded the host peptidoglycans (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}. EcoCyc product frame: MONOMER0-2658. Genomic coordinates: 578327-578509. EcoCyc protein note: RzoD is a predicted lysis protein of the cryptic lambdoid prophage DLP12 and is part of the lysis cassette. An rzpD/rzoD mutant is impaired in curli-based biofilm formation, which may be an indirect effect due to altered peptidoglycan metabolism . Deletion of the lysis module of DLP12 defective prophage (essD, rrrD, rzpD/rzoD) induces a hypervesiculation phenotype (possibly due to an excess of PG fragments in the periplasmic space); expression of the module is sensitive to environmental stress factors (low temperature, high osmolarity and acidic pH) .

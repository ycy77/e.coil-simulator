## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Single strand-specific metallo-endoribonuclease involved in late-stage 70S ribosome quality control and in maturation of the 3' terminus of the 16S rRNA. Acts together with the RNase R to eliminate defective 70S ribosomes, but not properly matured 70S ribosomes or individual subunits, by a process mediated specifically by the 30S ribosomal subunit. Involved in the processing of 16S, 23S and 5S rRNAs, with a particularly strong effect on maturation at both the 5'- and 3'-ends of 16S rRNA as well as maturation of the 5'-end of 23S and 5S rRNAs. {ECO:0000255|HAMAP-Rule:MF_00009, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16511207, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20639334, ECO:0000269|PubMed:20807199, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23273979}. YbeY is a metal-dependent single strand-specific endoribonuclease that is involved in maturation of 16S rRNA and in transcription antitermination at an rRNA promoter . RNase R and YbeY together (but not alone) are able to completely degrade defective 70S ribosomes generated in a ybeY mutant . In separate reports, YbeY did not appear to be involved in 3' end processing of 16S rRNA; any one of the four 3'->5'-exoribonucleases RNase II, RNase R RNase PH, and PNPase are able to perform this function at varying efficiencies .

## Enriched Summary

Uncharacterized lipoprotein YjbH The yjbEFGH operon is involved in the production of an extracellular polysaccharide . Sequence similarity suggests that YjbH may be a lipoprotein and/or an outer membrane porin . Overexpression of yjbEFGH alters colony morphology and leads to increased binding of toluidine blue-O and Congo red, indicating increased production of an EPS that is distinct from colanic acid . Disruption of yjbE has a polar effect on expression of yjbFGH, indicating that these genes form an operon; however, a full-length mRNA could not be detected, possibly due to strong secondary structure in the yjbE-yjbF intergenic region . Expression of yjbEFGH is highest at slow growth rates, low cell density and high oxygen availability; expression is higher in an rpoS mutant and induced by 0.7 M NaCl .

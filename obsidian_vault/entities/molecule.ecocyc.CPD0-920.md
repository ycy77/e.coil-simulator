## Enriched Summary

CPD0-920 Indolmycin is an antibacterial drug produced by both terrestrial and marine bacteria that exhibits potent antibacterial activity against human pathogens such as TAX-210 and mupirocin-, methicillin-resistant TAX-1280 . Indolmycin, discovered in 1960 , is a structural analog of TRP and competitively inhibits the bacterial EC-6.1.1.2 . Organisms that produce indolmycin are resistant to it due to the presence of a resistant tryptophan--tRNA ligase . Indolmycin has not been tested in humans, although it has been proposed to be tested as a topical treatment for staphylococcal infections. The indolmycin structure contains an oxazolinone ring in its core, making it of interest to synthetic chemists, and over the years several total synthetic routes have been reported . The complete biosynthetic pathway has been described in 2015 .

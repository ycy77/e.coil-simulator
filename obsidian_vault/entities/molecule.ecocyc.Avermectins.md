## Enriched Summary

The avermectins are a family of 16-membered macrocyclic lactone derivatives with potent anthelmintic and insecticidal properties produced by TAX-33903. The compounds are glycosylated with two molecules of CPD-13948 . Eight different avermectins are produced, consisting of four pairs of homologue compounds, with a major (a-component) and minor (b-component) components that are produced in ratios of 80:20 to 90:10. The a compounds and b compounds differ by having a butyl or a propyl side chain, respectively. Other differences regard the presence or absence of a double bond in one of the ketide rings and a methyl group in one of the CPD-13948 sugars . The avermectins block the transmission of electrical activity in invertebrate nerve and muscle cells mostly by enhancing the effects of glutamate at the glutamate-gated chloride channel that is specific to protostome invertebrates. This results in an influx of chloride ions into the cells, leading to hyperpolarisation and subsequent paralysis of invertebrate neuromuscular systems . Half of the 2015 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine was awarded to William C. Campbell and Satoshi Omura for discovering avermectin. Several derivatives of avermectins have been developed, including CPD-25537, CPD-25538, CPD-22128, CPD-25539, and CPD-25437...

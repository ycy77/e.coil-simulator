## Enriched Summary

YgbT/Cas1 is a nuclease with activity against single-stranded DNA as well as branched DNAs such as Holliday junctions, replication forks and 5'-flaps. Cas1 is specifically recruited to DNA double strand breaks. It physically and functionally interacts with the DNA repair system and may function in the RuvABC DNA repair pathway . Cas1 and Cas2 are the only proteins universally conserved between CRISPR-Cas systems and form a stable complex that is required for spacer acquisition . Cas1 and Cas2 together are required for efficient adaptation of the CRISPR array . Activity of Cas1 is dependent on a divalent metal cation . Spacer acquisition occurs by a concerted cleavage-ligation reaction that depends on the metal-binding center of Cas1 . In vitro, Cas1 is able to catalyze transesterification of branched DNA substrates . Crystal structures of the Cas1 C-terminal domain and the full-length protein have been solved at 1.4 and 1.95 Å resolution, respectively. Possible active site residues were identified, and site-directed mutagenesis of these residues confirmed their importance . Cas1 and Cas2 form a stable complex; a crystal structure of the complex has been solved at 2.3 Å resolution .

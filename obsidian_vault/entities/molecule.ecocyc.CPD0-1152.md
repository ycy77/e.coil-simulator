## Enriched Summary

N-pentylpantothenamide (N5-Pan) is an analogue of pantothenate in which the terminal carboxyl group has been condensed with a five-carbon alkyl amine to give an amide with a five-carbon alkyl chain. It is a potent inhibitor of E. coli growth. The compound and its derivatives are accepted with high efficiency (10-fold faster than pantothenate) by the last five enzymes of COA-PWY CoA biosynthesis pathway, forming CPD-26636, an analogue of CO-A . The main mechanism by which N5-Pan inhibits growth of E. coli is by binding of CPD-26636 to the EG12211 "PanZ protein", which results in loss of its ability to activate self-cleavage by the ASPDECARBOX-MONOMER "PanD protein", resulting in inhibition of B-ALANINE synthesis and as a result, a defficiency of CoA . ## Annotation

N-pentylpantothenamide (N5-Pan) is an analogue of pantothenate in which the terminal carboxyl group has been condensed with a five-carbon alkyl amine to give an amide with a five-carbon alkyl chain. It is a potent inhibitor of E. coli growth. The compound and its derivatives are accepted with high efficiency (10-fold faster than pantothenate) by the last five enzymes of COA-PWY CoA biosynthesis pathway, forming CPD-26636, an analogue of CO-A . The main mechanism by which N5-Pan inhibits growth of E. coli is by binding of CPD-26636 to the EG12211 "PanZ protein", which results in loss of its ability to activate self-cleavage by the ASPDECARBOX-MONOMER "PanD protein", resulting in inhibition of B-ALANINE synthesis and as a result, a defficiency of CoA .

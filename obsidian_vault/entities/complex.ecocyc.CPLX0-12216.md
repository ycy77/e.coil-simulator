## Enriched Summary

YejK was first identified as a protein associated with DNA in nucleoids . It is widespread in proteobacterial genomes . The purified protein can bind double-stranded, but not single-stranded DNA and displays no preference for curved or supercoiled DNA. The size of the binding site is likely to be 12-14 bp. YejK binding may untwist DNA by appoximately 4 degrees per binding unit. It is also able to bind RNA . Although YejK appears to be monomeric in solution, it binds DNA primarily as homodimers or tetramers . A crystal structure of the full YejK protein has been obtained to a resolution of 3.56 Å. Despite no sequence homology to other proteins structures and no identifiable DNA-binding motif, YejK has a unique fold structure of two domains, and it homodimerizes in solution forming a clamp-like structure with basic nucleotides in the pore that mediate DNA binding. Mutational characterization of the YejK dimer revealed open conformation that allowed the DNA to load and be clamped . YejK interacts with and affects the activity of CPLX0-2425 and CPLX0-2424 .

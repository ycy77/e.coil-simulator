## Enriched Summary

CPD-17005 Diphthamide is a unique posttranslationally modified histidine residue found only in translation elongation factor 2 (eEF-2). This factor is found in archaebacteria and all eukaryotes, but not in eubacteria, and is conserved from archaebacteria to humans. Diphthamide serves as the target for the CPLX-8048 and for Pseudomonas MONOMER-15589 "endotoxin A". Both toxins catalyze the transfer of ADP-ribose from NAD+ to diphthamide on eEF-2, inactivating it and halting cellular protein synthesis, causing cell death .

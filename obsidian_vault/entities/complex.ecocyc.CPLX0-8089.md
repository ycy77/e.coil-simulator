## Enriched Summary

The SecA protein is a multifunctional and dynamic component of the Sec protein translocation pathway. SecA is an ATPase that uses the energy of nucleotide binding and hydrolysis to drive the export of proteins through the inner membrane at the SECE-G-Y-CPLX "SecYEG translocon". Although best characterized for its role in post-translational translocation of periplasmic and outer membrane proteins, SecA also participates in a cotranslational pathway and is required for the biogenesis of some inner membrane proteins. ## Annotation

The SecA protein is a multifunctional and dynamic component of the Sec protein translocation pathway. SecA is an ATPase that uses the energy of nucleotide binding and hydrolysis to drive the export of proteins through the inner membrane at the SECE-G-Y-CPLX "SecYEG translocon". Although best characterized for its role in post-translational translocation of periplasmic and outer membrane proteins, SecA also participates in a cotranslational pathway and is required for the biogenesis of some inner membrane proteins. Temperature sensitive secA mutations are associated with defects in protein export; mutant strains accumulate precursors of normally secreted proteins (MalE, PhoA, LamB, OmpF) in the cytosol when grown at the non-permissive temperature (see also ). SecA is required for translocation of OmpA and alkaline phosphatase into everted membrane vesicles . In vitro reconstitution of preOmpA translocation in proteoliposomes requires SecA, CPLX0-8046 "SecB", the translocation complex consisting of SecY, SecE and an additional unidentified polypeptide (later shown to be SecG ), and ATP; translocation is stimulated by the proton motive force (see also ). In vitro systems of Sec-dependent transport have been investigated by . SecA is a peripheral inner membrane protein ; SecA partitions equally between the cytoplasm and inner membrane . SecA binds both SecB and a SecB-preOmpA complex in vitro; SecA interacts with SecYEG in the membrane (see also ). The SecB binding site of SecA contains a zinc atom that is coordinated by cysteine residues and is needed for SecA-SecB functional interaction (see also ). SecA binding to membranes requires acidic phospholipids (see also ). SecA has multiple ATP binding sites; SecA has preOmpA and membrane dependent ATPase activity (see also ).

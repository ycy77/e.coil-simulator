## Enriched Summary

UspA belongs to a superfamily of widely conserved proteins whose physiological function is unknown. Synthesis of UspA is induced in response to a variety of stresses , including nitrosative stress . UspA protein is autophosphorylated at serine/threonine residues in response to the same conditions that induce its expression . The overlapping and distinct roles of the various Usp proteins in E. coli have been studied . A uspA null mutant has a metabolic defect, leading to a diauxic growth phenotype when diluted into fresh medium . Mutants also show a defect in survival of prolonged periods of growth inhibition and are sensitive to UV irradiation and mitomycin C exposure . Overexpression of UspA leads to decreased growth rate in minimal medium and causes global changes in protein synthesis . uspA mRNA stability during glucose starvation is diminished in the absence of CPLX0-3281 function . Transcription of uspA is repressed by FadR , but repression can be overcome by the alarmone ppGpp during severe cellular stress .

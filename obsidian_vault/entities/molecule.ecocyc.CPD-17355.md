## Enriched Summary

CPD-10244 Docosahexaenoate (DHA) is a 22-carbon long-chain polyunsaturated fatty acid (LCPUFA) with 6 methylene interrupted cis-double bonds . It is prevalent in the membrane lipids of some human tissues such as brain, testis and retina, and plays a critical role in the development and function of these tissues. It is also a precursor of different classes of icosanoid effectors involved in the regulation of many important functions in mammals . Many of the compounds drived from DHA are listed under DHA-Derived-Metabolites. DHA is an n-3 (ω-3) fatty acid which is defined according to the location of the last double bond at the methyl end. It is synthesized de novo in several microalgae as well as in some fungi. It is also produced by some marine bacteria, mostly those adapted to low temperatures and high pressures . Mammals are not able to synthesis DHA de novo, and instead synthesize it from the essential fatty acid LINOLENIC_ACID (which they obtain in their diet) via alternating steps of microsomal aerobic desaturation and elongation followed by a peroxisomal β-oxidation step . Like other fatty acids, CPD-10244 is rarely found in its free form. It is usually found either as CPD-17312 or CPD-17355 "incorporated into a lipid".

## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: Hemine; Hemin EcoCyc common name: hemin. Hemin is PROTOPORPHYRIN_IX containing an Fe3+ ion with a coordinating chloride ligand. It is similar to CPD-11677, in which the iron ion is coordinated to a hydroxyl group instead of a chloride ion. Both of these molecules are derived from PROTOHEME, which contains a Fe2+ that is not liganded to an additional ion. Hemin is endogenously produced in the human body, and was first crystallized out of blood in 1853, by Ludwik Karol Teichmann. Hans Fischer received the 1930 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his work on the synthesis of hemin . Several proteins in human blood, such as hemopexin and serum albumin, bind to hemin.

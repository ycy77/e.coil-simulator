## Enriched Summary

Microcin C, originally named microcin 7 and often referred to as microcin C7, is a heptapeptide produced by TAX-562 with an N-formylated methionine and a C-terminal phosphoramidate linkage to adenosine, modified with an aminopropyl moiety . Microcin C is exported from producing cells through a major facilitator superfamily efflux pump encoded by the G-124166 gene. It is imported by target cells via the ABC-41-CPLX "inner membrane transporter YejABEF", which recognizes the peptide part of the microcin . Following import, the peptide is cleaved by non-specific peptidases, releasing CPD-25930, a toxic CPD0-915 mimic that targets EC-6.1.1.12, inhibiting protein synthesis .

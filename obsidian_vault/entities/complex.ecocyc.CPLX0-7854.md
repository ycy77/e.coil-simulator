## Enriched Summary

"Trehalose repressor," TreR, acts as a repressor of operons involved in trehalose transport and degradation under osmotic stress . In the presence of trehalose-6-phosphate and at low osmolarity levels, TreR derepresses an operon related to trehalose transport and catabolism , while trehalose is inactive . TreR occurs as a homodimer and is composed of two domains: an amino-terminal domain that contains a potential helix-turn-helix DNA-binding motif, and a carboxy-terminal domain involved in effector recognition . This transcription factor is a regulator that belongs to the LacI/GalR family (specifically, to the HutC subfamily) . This regulator binds to a 14-bp-long palindromic sequence . The crystal structure of the trehalose repressor (TreR) in a complex with its inducer trehalose-6-phosphate has been published . Based on saturation transfer difference NMR, it was shown that both trehalose-6-phosphate and trehalose bind to TreR with similar affinities. ## Annotation

"Trehalose repressor," TreR, acts as a repressor of operons involved in trehalose transport and degradation under osmotic stress . In the presence of trehalose-6-phosphate and at low osmolarity levels, TreR derepresses an operon related to trehalose transport and catabolism , while trehalose is inactive . TreR occurs as a homodimer and is composed of two domains: an amino-terminal domain that contains a potential helix-turn-helix DNA-binding motif, and a carboxy-terminal domain involved in effector recognition . This transcription factor is a regulator that belongs to the LacI/GalR family (specifically, to the HutC subfamily) . This regulator binds to a 14-bp-long palindromic sequence . The crystal structure of the trehalose repressor (TreR) in a complex with its inducer trehalose-6-phosphate has been published . Based on saturation transfer difference NMR, it was shown that both trehalose-6-phosphate and trehalose bind to TreR with similar affinities. Titration showed that trehalose-6-phosphate binds more strongly than trehalose . The ratio of dissociation of TreR [koff(trehalose)]/[koff(trehalose/6-phosphate)] is >/-2,800, and its rate of association (kon) is >/-102 . The TreR binding landscape was globally mapped in vivo by standardized ChIP-seq and quantitatively modeled using BoltzNet, a biophysically informed neural network that infers binding energy from sequence data . The resulting ChIP-seq binding regions are available in RegulonDB (Galagan collection), and the corresponding energy matrix and genomic binding profiles can be accessed at boltznet.bu.edu .

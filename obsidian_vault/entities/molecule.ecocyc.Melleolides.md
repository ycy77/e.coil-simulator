## Annotation

The Melleolides melleolides are a large family of aryl ester secondary metabolites produced by species of the basidiomycete genus TAX-47424, which are composed of an CPD-47 moiety esterified to a tricyclic sesquiterpene (CPD-14796) alcohol . Both CPD-47 and protoilludane derivatives have been described from numerous other organisms , but these two elements are combined into a single entity only in the melleolides, which are not found outside the TAX-47424. Biosynthesis of the sesquiterpene protoilludene moiety is achieved by cyclization of FARNESYL-PP, catalyzed by EC-4.2.3.135, and results in the tricyclic CPD-14796 backbone . In TAX-47429, CPD-47 is biosynthesized by a seven-domain polyketide synthase whose terminal thioesterase domain also catalyzes the coupling of CPD-47 to protoilludene-based alcohols . Modifications of the basic structure include hydroxylation at various positions of the sesquiterpene, oxidation of the primary alcohol at C-1 to an aldehyde or carboxy group, shift or reduction of the cyclohexene double bond, methyl ether formation at O-5', and chlorination at C-6' , resulting in excess of 60 melleolide analogs and making the melleolides one of the most diverse groups of fungal natural products . Various melleolides have been shown to inhibit microbial growth and display phytotoxicity . The antifungal activity has been linked to the position of the double bond within the sesquiterpene moiety . Melleolides have also been reported to have cytotoxic effects on human cancer cell lines by decreasing DNA synthesis .

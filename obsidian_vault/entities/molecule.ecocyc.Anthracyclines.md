## Enriched Summary

Anthracyclines are a class of antibiotics produced by TAX-1883 bacteria that are used in cancer as chemotherapy drugs. This group of antibiotics is defined by the presence of a four fused-ring aromatic system, based on a 7,8,9,10-tetrahydro-5,12-naphthacenequinone framework, which usually occurs as a glycoside with a deoxy(amino)sugar. The framework is synthesized by a polyketide-synthase from an acetyl-CoA or propanoyl-CoA starter unit and malonyl-CoA extender units, and subsequently modified by other enzymes . The anthracyclines intercalate between base pairs of the DNA/RNA strand, inhibit topoisomerase II enzymes, and prevent DNA replication. They are among the most effective anticancer treatments ever developed and are effective against more types of cancer than any other class of chemotherapeutic agents.

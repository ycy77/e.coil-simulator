## Enriched Summary

CPD0-971 "A limit dextrin" is the name given to the highly-branched core of the polysaccharide fragments remaining at the end (limit) of exhaustive hydrolysis of CPD-7043 amylopectin or Glycogens glycogen by α-1,4-glucan hydrolase enzymes such as GLYCOPHOSPHORYL-CPLX and MALDEXPHOSPHORYL-CPLX. These enzymes shorten the chains, but are not able to hydrolyze the α-1,6 bonds at branch points, forming Truncated-Limit-Dextrins. Following the action of the YPR184W-MONOMER, which completely removes the branching chains, CPD0-1027 is formed.

## Enriched Summary

A sulfenic acid is an organosulfur compound and oxoacid with the general formula RSOH. In contrast to Alkyl-Sulfinates sulfinic and SULFONATES sulfonic acids, simple sulfenic acids, such as CPD-17426, are highly reactive and cannot be isolated in solution. Sulfenic acids are fromed in plants of the TAX-4678 genus from S-Alkyl-L-Cysteine-S-Oxides "S-alkyl-L-cysteine S-oxide" by the action of EC-4.4.1.4 (alliinase). They are acted upon quickly by a second enzyme, the lachrymatory-factor synthase, giving a stable S-oxide product. In the absence of lachrymatory-factor synthase they tend to condense with other sulfenic acid molecules quickly to form CPD-17425 thiosulfinates.

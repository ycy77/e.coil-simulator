## Enriched Summary

The Lewis y epitope (Ley) is a difucosylated oligosaccharide derived from B-Gal-14-NacGlc-R "type 2 precursor disaccharides" carried by glycoconjugates on the cell surface. Ley is expressed predominately during embryogenesis, and expression on adult tissue is restricted to granulocytes and epithelial surfaces , where it acts as a ligand for lectin-like proteins . However, Ley is overexpressed in the majority of carcinomas, including breast, ovary, pancreas, prostate, colon, and non-small cell lung cancers, and has been correlated to the pathological staging and prognosis . In these cases, Ley occurs either at the plasma membrane as a glycolipid or linked to surface receptors . Ley was shown to play an important role in promoting cell proliferation, tumorigenecity and adhesion , and several clinical trials were conducted using Ley monoclonal antibodies for targeted immunotherapy of tumors . The human pathogenic bacterium TAX-210 is known to express the Ley antigen in its lipopolysaccharide, mimicking human cell surface glycoconjugates .

## Enriched Summary

Levan is a non-structural polysaccharide present in several microorganisms and a few plant species. It consists almost solely of fructosyl residues linked via β-2,6 linkages, and is packed into nano-sized spherical forms, giving it a very low intrinsic viscosity. Levan is a main ingredient in natto, a traditional Japanese food. Plants synthesize levan in the vacuole by the concerted action of various fructosyltransferases such as EC-2.4.1.10. When acting alone, this enzyme synthesizes linear levan chains from sucrose via the trisaccharide intermediate 6-KESTOSE (it can also use 1-KESTOTRIOSE and NEOKESTOSE as the initial fructose acceptors). Plant fructans generally have a low degree of polymerization (DP <10 to 100). The occurrence of levansucrase is widespread in Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacteria. Bacterial levans reach much higher molecular masses and contain up to 10,000 fructosyl units. Species that belong to the TAX-1301, TAX-1243, and TAX-1578 genera also contain EC-2.4.1.9, which forms β-(2,1) fructosyl-fructose linkages. When expressed together with levansucrase, that enzyme creates branching points that connect multiple β-(2,6) linked levan chains, forming a branched polymer known as Graminans graminan .

## Enriched Summary

Probable zinc protease PqqL (EC 3.4.24.-) PqqL is a periplasmic protein; a 26 residue signal peptide is cleaved in vivo. Purified recombinant PqqL, crystallized in the absence of substrate, contains two M16 protease domains each of which forms one half of an extended 'clam-shell like' structure; the N-terminal domain contains a putative zinc ion in the protease active site. PqqL is flexible in solution. Purified PqqL cleaves a small subset of peptides within a combinatorial peptide library; it does not show proteolytic activity towards human ferredoxins and globins, or plant ferredoxin . pqqL is located in a 3 gene cluster (yddA-yddB-ppqL) which may represent a 3-component iron-uptake system . Distant homologs (fusD, fusA and fusC respectively) encode a ferredoxin uptake system (Fus) in plant pathogenic Pectobacterium spp. . pqqL expression increases under iron-limiting conditions; PqqL does not support growth during iron limitation in LB media but may have a niche specific role in iron acquisition from a distinct protein substrate . PqqL is a putative zinc peptidase; pqqL is part of a 5 ORF fragment that is able to complement pyroquinoline quinone (ppqE and ppqF) mutants of Methylobacterium organophilum .

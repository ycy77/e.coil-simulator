## Enriched Summary

The strength of the peptidoglycan polymer is obtained by extensive cross-linking between the peptide side-chains that extend from the glycan strand. The cross-linking is formed by transpeptidases and occur within newly synthesized peptidoglycan polymers and between newly made material and preexisting peptidoglycan. Cross-links are also present between peptidoglycan and lipoproteins in the outer membrane. Common forms of cross-linking enzymes are the DD-transpeptidases that catalyze cross-linking between the D-alanine at position 4 of a pentapeptide side-chain and the meso-diaminopimeloyl or lysyl residue at position 3 of a different side-chain; and the LD transpeptidases, which catalyze the cross linking between two meso-diaminopimeloyl or lysyl residues of separate side-chains.

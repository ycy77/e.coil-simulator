## Enriched Summary

L-AZASERINE is the most widely studied naturally occurring diazo compound (with over 1100 publications as of 2011). It was isolated from TAX-67301 in 1954 as part of a screening program for new antitumour and antibiotic agents . L-AZASERINE acts as an analog of GLN, and is a competitive inhibitor of enzymes using glutamine. A large number of studies on the antitumour effects of azaserine have been conducted and included a number of clinical trials. Although azaserine showed some useful activity in leukaemia, it was less effective than other agents, and as a result, clinical studies on the compound have been abandoned .

## Enriched Summary

The CPD-8594 "Lewis x" epitope, also known as stage-specific embryonic antigen-1 (SSEA-1) or CD15 , is a carbohydrate structure expressed on several glycolipids, glycoproteins, and proteoglycans of the nervous system and has been implicated in cell-cell recognition, neurite outgrowth, and neuronal migration during development. It appears to be involved in regulating neural stem cells via the Notch signaling pathway . The CPD-8594 "Lewis X epitope" is formed by α(1,3) fucosylation of the N-acetylglucosaminyl residue of B-Gal-14-NacGlc-R, the disaccharide precursor that can also lead to formation of the Type-2-H-Antigen by α(1,2) fucosylation at its terminal galactosyl residue. In mice the α-(1,3)-fucosyltransferase encoded by the HS10520 gene is the main contributor to the production of the CPD-8594 "Lewis x" epitope . The related Lewis-a-epitope is formed by α(1,4) fucosylation of the N-acetylglucosaminyl residue of beta-Gal-13-beta-GlcNac-R, which can also lead to formation of Type-1-H-Antigen by α(1,2) fucosylation at its terminal galactosyl residue. Both the CPD-8594 and the Lewis-a-epitope cannot be further transformed to ABH-type antigens .

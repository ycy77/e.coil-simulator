## Enriched Summary

UDP-N-acetylmuramate-alanine ligase (MurC) is responsible for the addition of the first amino acid of the peptide moiety in the assembly of the monomer unit of peptidoglycan . The formation of the peptide bond involves a covalent acyl-phosphate intermediate between the carboxyl group of UDP-N-acetylmuramate and the γ phosphate of ATP . Kinetic data are consistent with an Ordered Ter-Ter reaction mechanism . MurC exists in both monomeric and dimeric form, and both are active . Crystal structures of the enzyme have been solved . Temperature-sensitive murC mutants have been isolated . The murC3 allele was determined to carry a G344D mutation in a conserved region of the enzyme . Additional point mutations were generated by site-directed mutagenesis; analysis of their effect on activity suggested the location of the active site and a residue involved in enzyme stability . MurC is a potential antimicrobial drug target; inhibitors of MurC activity have been identified . MurA, MurB and MurC are capable of chemo-enzymatic labeling and modifying of UDP-GlcNAc-RNA in vitro .

## Enriched Summary

Phenolphthiodiolone Phenolphthiodiolones are intermediates in Phenolphthiocerols phenolphthiocerol biosynthesis. Phenolphthiocerol glycolipids have been found in many Mycobacterium species, including TAX-1769, TAX-1768, TAX-1765, TAX-1781, and TAX-78331 . Two types of phenolphthiodiolone exist. In Phenolphthiodiolone-A "phenolphthiodiolone A" the oxo and the methyl groups are attached at positions 3 and 4, respectively, and the hydroxyl groups are at positions 9 and 11. In Phenolphthiodiolone-B "phenolphthiodiolone B" a methyl group is missing from the beginning of the molecule, and thus the oxo and methyl groups are attached at positions 2 and 3, respectively, and the hydroxyl groups are at positions 8 and 10.

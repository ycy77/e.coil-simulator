## Enriched Summary

Plant arabinogalactan-proteins belong to a large family of wall glycoproteins/proteoglycans, known as the Pro/Hyp-rich glycoproteins. They are found on the plasma membrane, in the wall, in the apoplastic space, and in secretions. The glycan moiety of these glycoproteins constitutes 90% to 98% of the weight. It usually consists of branched type II arabino-3,6-galactans, primarily O-linked to Hyp residues . In general, the glycan moieties of plant arabinogalactan-proteins (AGPs) demonstrate a great structural and compositional variability. The structure shown here is based on a representative Hyp-AG polysaccharide (AHP-1) released by base hydrolysis from a synthetic AGP (Ala-Hyp)51 from tobacco BY2 cells .

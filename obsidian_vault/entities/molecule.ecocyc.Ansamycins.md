## Enriched Summary

Ansamycins Ansamycins are a class of bacterial secondary metabolites that includes several important antibiotics such as the Streptovaricins streptovaricins and Rifamycins rifamycins. They are named ansamycins (from the Latin ansa, handle) because of their unique structure, which consists of an aromatic moiety bridged at nonadjacent positions by a polyketide-derived aliphatic chain through an amide linkage . The different members of the ansamycin family differ mainly in their aromatic moiety, which can include a naphthalene ring, a naphthoquinone ring, a benzene ring or or a benzoquinone ring. Beyond their interesting structures, the ansamycins display a number of significant bioactivities including RNA polymerase inhibition , heat shock protein HSP90 inhibition , and antiproliferation activities .

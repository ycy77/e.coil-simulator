The S5 protein is a component of the 30S subunit of the ribosome. It was suggested that S5 is positioned to have access to the interface between the 30S and 50S subunits of the ribosome . The N-terminal half of S5 contains the sites of mutations that confer resistance to spectinomycin and binds non-specifically to helix 34 of the 16S rRNA . S5 may be involved in modulating the conformation of the 16S rRNA . S5 can be cross-linked to mRNA and tRNA . The S5 protein is acetylated at the N-terminus ; mutants in the alanine acetyltransferase enzyme, EG10851-MONOMER RimJ, are temperature sensitive . N-terminal acetylation of S5 and S18 correlates with correct folding of the platform and central pseudoknot domains of 16S rRNA...

## Enriched Summary

15-ANHYDRO-D-GLUCITOL is a six-carbon monosaccharide that is the 1-deoxy form of D-glucopyranose. It is generally found in animals and plants, and is inert in these organisms (some bacteria and fungi are able to metabolize it. ). Its physiological role is unknown. Most of the 15-ANHYDRO-D-GLUCITOL found in animal blood is derived from food. The level of 15-ANHYDRO-D-GLUCITOL in human body fluid is used as a sensitive marker for glycemic control in the diagnosis of diabetes, since the plasma level of this compound decreases dramatically in diabetes patients. ## Annotation

15-ANHYDRO-D-GLUCITOL is a six-carbon monosaccharide that is the 1-deoxy form of D-glucopyranose. It is generally found in animals and plants, and is inert in these organisms (some bacteria and fungi are able to metabolize it. ). Its physiological role is unknown. Most of the 15-ANHYDRO-D-GLUCITOL found in animal blood is derived from food. The level of 15-ANHYDRO-D-GLUCITOL in human body fluid is used as a sensitive marker for glycemic control in the diagnosis of diabetes, since the plasma level of this compound decreases dramatically in diabetes patients. The lower levels in diabetic patients are thought to be due to competitive inhibition of the reabsorption of the compound at the renal proximal tubuli by a glucose transporter in the presence of excess glucose in the glomerular filtrate . 15-ANHYDRO-D-GLUCITOL can also act as a marker for coronary artery disease .

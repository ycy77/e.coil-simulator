## Enriched Summary

Osmoregulated periplasmic glucans (OPGs), formerly known as membrane derived oligosaccharides (MDOs), represent a class of periplasmic oligosaccharides occuring in Gram-negative bacteria and implicated in osmoregulation. Cyclic, branched, and open-chain variants have been found, usually containing in the order of a dozen D-glucose units. The MDO of TAX-562 constitute a closely related family of compounds containing about 8-10 glucose units in a highly branched structure linked by β-1→2 and β1→6 bonds. The glucose residues are substituted with various amounts of sn-1-phosphoglycerol and smaller amounts of phosphoethanolamine residues in phosphodiester linkage on the 6 position of certain of the glucose residues. Some species of membrane-derived oligosaccharide also contain succinate in O-ester linkage .

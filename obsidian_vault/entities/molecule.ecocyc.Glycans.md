## Enriched Summary

Glycans are polysaccharides or oligosaccharides, usually consisting solely of O-glycosidic linkages of monosaccharides. Glycans can be homo- or heteropolymers of monosaccharide residues, and can be linear or branched. The term is also often used to describe the carbohydrate portion of a glycoconjugate, such as a glycoprotein, glycolipid, or a proteoglycan.

## Enriched Summary

## Annotation

The transcriptional repressor HipB, for "High persistence," is negatively autoregulated and controls the transcription of a critical persistence factor . hipB foms an operon with hipA, and the products of this operon are classified as a toxin (HipA)-antitoxin (HipB) system. This HipAB system is involved in the high persistence, which is the capacity of the bacteria to survive prolonged exposure to antibiotics , and Kawano et al. also showed that this system is important for survival during the long-term stationary phase . The crystal structure of the HipB-HipA-DNA complex has been solved at 2.68 Å resolution . The complex is tetrameric and is comprised of a HipB homodimer that interacts with DNA, sandwiched by a monomer of HipA on each side . In the HipB-HipA complex, each monomer of HipA interacts with two monomers of HipB . The crystal structure of an extended promoter region that contained two operator sites revealed that the interaction with HipB bound to both sites leads to dimerizarion of HipA, which blocks the active sites of both HipA molecules . HipB is an antitoxin that counteracts and neutralizes the HipA toxin, and HipA is toxic in the absence of its binding partner HipB . Although HipA does not bind the Hip regulatory region, it plays an indirect role via its binding to HipB. HipB contains a helix-turn-helix motif near the N terminus and is a Cro-like DNA-binding protein. It binds to four operator sites with the conserved inverted repeat sequence motif TATCCN8GGATA, and its binding occurs cooperatively and almost simultaneously on the same face of the DNA helix.

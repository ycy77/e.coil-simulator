## Enriched Summary

Uncharacterized electron transport protein YkgF While their physiological role in E. coli remains unknown, the ykgEFG genes are homologous to newly identified lactate utilization genes in Shewanella oneidensis. Low-copy expression of E. coli ykgEFG in S. oneidensis complements the ability of the Δdld-II ΔlldF double mutant for growth on L-lactate, but not D-lactate . Overexpression of ykgEFG in E. coli restores the ability of a EG10231 dld mutant to grow on D-lactate; crude extracts of an overexpressing strain contain elevated levels of both L- and D-lactate dehydrogenase activity . A ykgF insertion mutant is still able to utilize L-lactate as the sole source of carbon . However, a quadruple null mutant strain (ΔlldD dld glcD ykgF) is unable to grow on L- or D-lactate .

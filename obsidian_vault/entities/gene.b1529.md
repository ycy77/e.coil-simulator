## Enriched Summary

marC (gene.b1529) is a gene entity. It encodes marC (protein.P0AEY1). Encoded protein function: UPF0056 inner membrane protein MarC EcoCyc product frame: EG11637-MONOMER. EcoCyc synonyms: ydeC, ydeB. Genomic coordinates: 1618243-1618908. EcoCyc protein note: MarC is an inner membrane protein with six predicted transmembrane domains. The C terminus is located in the periplasm . MarC is not involved in multiple antibiotic resistance . MarC, YchE and YhgN are paralogs. Neither a marC single deletion mutant nor a triple marC ychE yhgN deletion mutant shows a difference in susceptibility to various antibiotics compared to wild type . Overexpression of MarC also has no effect on antibiotic susceptibility . Although marC mRNA levels are increased by tetracycline and chloramphenicol , this is likely due to mRNA stabilization . marC expression is not regulated by MarR . Non-synonymous SNPs and an IS1 insertion in marC were identified in adaptive laboratory evolution (ALE) experiments selecting for increased isobutanol tolerance. marC mutants were also identified in ALE experiments selecting for increased isoprenol tolerance .

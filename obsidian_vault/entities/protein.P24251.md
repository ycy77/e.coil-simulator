## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Binds to the sigma-S subunit of RNA polymerase, activating expression of sigma-S-regulated genes, such as the csgBAC operon encoding the subunits of curli proteins. Stimulates RNA polymerase holoenzyme formation and may bind to several other sigma factors, such as sigma-70 (rpoD) and sigma-32 (rpoH). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:1357528, ECO:0000269|PubMed:16980472}. Resequencing of multiple isolates of the MG1655 strain has identified several genetic variations compared to the reference sequence, including an IS1 insertion in crl in strain ATCC700926 , which is the source of the genome sequence within EcoCyc (U00096.3). This EcoliWiki page summarizes these sequence differences. The crl gene is part of a commonly deleted region in long-term stab cultures of E. coli . A crl deletion strain has a fitness advantage in stationary phase . An intact version of crl can be seen in E. coli K-12 W3110 here. Crl is an RNA polymerase holoenzyme assembly factor . By tilting the competition between RPOS-MONOMER σS and RPOD-MONOMER σ70 for binding to APORNAP-CPLX toward σS, more RNAPS-CPLX (EσS) is formed, allowing more transcription from promoters within the σS regulon . Crl is able to enhance both promoter binding and opening by EσS holoenzyme at certain promoters . Crl acts by promoting the active conformation of σS . 40% of the σS regulon is modulated by Crl...

## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: cis-1,4-Polyisoprene; Rubber particle; Rubber; Rubber (all-cis) EcoCyc common name: rubber. Natural rubber is an important raw material in an enormous number of products. The backbone of natural rubber comprises a (cis)-1,4-polyisoprene with two or three trans-isoprene units at the ω-terminus, formed by the sequential condensation of DELTA3-ISOPENTENYL-PP (IPP) units in cis configuration onto a short all-trans prenyl diphosphate. It is very similar in structure to DOLICHOL dolichols, but the chain length is much longer (C>1500). Natural rubber is produced from latex - a material that makes up the cytoplasmic content of the latecifers or latex vessels of TAX-3981 (the Brazilian rubber tree) . Although over 2000 plant species capable of producing rubber have been identified, only a few synthesize the high molecular weight rubber that is required for the production of high quality rubber products, and natural rubber is currently produced commercially only from TAX-3981. A potential alternative source is the Russian rubber-producing dandelion (TAX-333970), which was cultivated in the former Soviet Union during World War II to satisfy the demand for natural rubber .

## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Putative O-acetyltransferase that transfers an O-acetyl group to the O antigen. wbbJ is predicted to encode an O-acetyltransferase involved in the biosynthesis of O-antigen repeat units (see ). E. coli K-12 is phenotypically rough and does not express O-antigen due to mutations in the rfb region; E. coli K-12 MG1655 contains the rfb-50 mutation - an IS5 element which disrupts EG11986 wbbL encoding rhamnose transferase; an engineered strain of E. coli K-12 with all rfb genes intact synthesizes CPD0-2249 O16 antigen .

## Enriched Summary

CPD0-971 "A limit dextrin" is the name given to the highly-branched core of the polysaccharide fragments remaining at the end (limit) of exhaustive hydrolysis of CPD-7043 amylopectin or Glycogens glycogen by α-1,4-glucan hydrolase enzymes such as GLYCOPHOSPHORYL-CPLX and MALDEXPHOSPHORYL-CPLX. These enzymes shorten the chains, but are not able to hydrolyze the α-1,6 bonds at branch points. Since the branching residues block the enzymes from getting close, the enzymes leave a tail of 4 α-1,4-linked residues next to the branch points.

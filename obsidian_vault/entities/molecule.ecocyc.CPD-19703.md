## Enriched Summary

The imidazole ring is aromatic due to the presence of a sextet of π-electrons, consisting of a pair of electrons from the protonated nitrogen atom and one from each of the remaining four atoms of the ring. Thus the compound exists in several resonance structures. Imidazole also exists in two equivalent tautomeric forms, IMIDAZOLE-RING and CPD-19703. Different numbering systems have been used leading to confusion in the naming of imidazole compounds. When a side chain exists (as in L-histidine), the nitrogen atoms of the ring are denoted by pros ('near', abbreviated π) and tele ('far', abbreviated T) to show their position relative to the side chain. When a substituent is introduced at positions 4 or 5, the tautomeric character of the molecule precludes a definite numbering of these positions, resulting in names in the form "4(or 5)-substituted imidazole", as in CPD-54 "4(or 5)-aminoimidazole". However, when the imino hydrogen is substituted, tautomerism is no longer possible, and the numbering of positions 4 and 5 becomes absolute, as in CPD-19704.

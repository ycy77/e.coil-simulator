## Enriched Summary

YghU is one of eight glutathione transferase homologs in E. coli . YghU has modest activity with some organic hydroperoxides; the physiological substrates remain to be discovered . YghU expressed in a cell-free system did not exhibit GSH-conjugating activities toward 1-chloro-2,4-dinitrobenzene (CDNB) . Although yghU is located close to gss, there is no indication that YghU regulates gluthationylspermidine synthetase activity as initially proposed . A crystal structure of YghU has been solved at 1.5 Å resolution. The structure reveals two molecules of glutathione bound in the active site .

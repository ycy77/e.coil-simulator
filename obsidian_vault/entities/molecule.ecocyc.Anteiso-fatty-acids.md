## Enriched Summary

Anteiso-fatty acids are a class of branched-chain fatty acids (BCFA) that are characterized by a methyl-substituent located on the antepenultimate carbon of the carboxyalkyl chain. Anteiso-fatty acids are the most prominent fatty acids of cell membranes of some bacteria, especially of the genus Bacillus. The most relevant anteiso-fatty acids in food and bacteria are CPD-9917 and CPD-23509 . Unlike classic fatty acids and Iso-fatty-acids "iso-fatty acids", anteiso-fatty acids are chiral molecules due to the stereogenic center at the carbon of the branching point. A distinct pre-dominance of (S)-anteiso-fatty acids, or even (S)-enantiopurity has been described in bacteria , and also in fish , dairy products , mutton fat , and Brussel sprouts .

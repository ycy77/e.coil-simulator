Glycosphingolipids Glycosphingolipids are a family of complex lipids composed of Ceramides and mono- or oligosaccharide moieties that play an important role in various cellular functions including recognition, cell adhesion, proliferation and differentiation . Glycosphingolipids are synthesized by the sequential addition of sugar residues to ceramides by glycosyltransferases that are specific to each glycosidic linkage. Lactosyl-Ceramides Lactosylceramide, a product of the second glycosylation step in glycosphingolipid biosynthesis, is an important branching point, being a common precursor for the synthesis of the three major classes of glycosphingolipids: the ganglio series, the lacto (and neolacto) series, and the globo series . The fatty acid composition of Lactosyl-Ceramides lactosylceramide is variable, but it comprises primarily PALMITATE, DOCOSANOATE, and TETRACOSANOATE.

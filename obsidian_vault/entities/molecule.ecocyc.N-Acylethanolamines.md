## Enriched Summary

N-Acylethanolamines N-acylethanolamines are a class of endogenous bioactive lipid molecules formed by the linkage of an acyl group to the nitrogen atom of ETHANOL-AMINE. These compounds exert a variety of biological actions through their interactions with various receptors. One of the N-acylethanolamines, CPD-7598, has received much attention because it is an endocanabinoid (an endogenous ligand of the cannabinoid receptors CB1 and CB2). The other N-acylethanolamines do not bind to cannabinoid receptors.

## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Part of a heterodimeric complex that catalyzes the two-step biosynthesis of 4-amino-4-deoxychorismate (ADC), a precursor of p-aminobenzoate (PABA) and tetrahydrofolate. In the first step, a glutamine amidotransferase (PabA) generates ammonia as a substrate that, along with chorismate, is used in the second step, catalyzed by aminodeoxychorismate synthase (PabB) to produce ADC. PabB, in the absence of PabA, can catalyze the formation of ADC in the presence of exogenous ammonia. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16605270, ECO:0000269|PubMed:2251281, ECO:0000269|PubMed:4914080}. PabB (component 1) catalyzes the formation of 4-amino-4-deoxychorismate (ADC) from chorismate and ammonia generated by PabA within the amiodeoxychorismate synthase complex . Kinetic studies support an ordered bi-bi catalytic mechanism with chorismate binding first . In the absence of PabA, PabB can utilize ammonia (at a reduced rate) to aminate chorismate and produce 4-amino-4-deoxychorismate . The reaction catalyzed by PabB in vitro in the presence of ammonia is reversible in the absence of PabC . Crystal structures of PabB have been determined at 2.0 Å resolution; a potential active site has been identified. A deeply embedded tryptophan molecule may play a structural role . Random and site-directed mutagenesis has identified residues important for the interaction with PabA and for catalytic activity...

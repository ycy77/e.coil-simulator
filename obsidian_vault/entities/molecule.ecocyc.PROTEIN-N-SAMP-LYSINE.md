## Enriched Summary

Archaea have a mechanism of protein post-translational modification, termed SAMPylation, that shares many biochemical features with the ubiquitination system of eukaryotes. The archaeal equivalents of ubiquitin have been named Small Archaeal Modifier Proteins (SAMPs). Like ubiquitin, these proteins possess a β-grasp fold and carboxy-terminal diglycine motif, and as in ubiquitination, the C-terminal carboxylate forms an isopeptide bond with the ε-amino group of lysine residues on target proteins . The C-terminal glycine of SAMP2 can form a bond with a lysine residue of another SAMP2 molecule, forming poly-SAMP chains, as in the case of polyubiquitination.

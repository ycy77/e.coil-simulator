## Annotation

The 4.5S RNA and Ffh protein together form the signal recognition particle (SRP) , which recognizes exposed signal anchor sequence and targets ribosome nascent chain complexes (RNC) to the SRP receptor, EG10346-MONOMER "FtsY" |CITS [8107852]|. SRP is involved in the targeting and integration of primarily inner membrane proteins as opposed to secretory proteins; the SRP-FtsY co-translational pathway targets RNC to the SEC-SECRETION-CPLX Sec translocon for subsequent integration of inner membrane proteins . SRP discriminates against periplasmic and outer membrane proteins; SRP preferentially binds transmembrane domains enriched in hydrophobic residues . The interaction between 4.5S RNA and Ffh , between SRP and the ribosome and the GTP-dependent interaction between SRP (or SRP-RNC) and FtsY have been examined in detail. Incorrect cargos are discriminated against at each step of the SRP pathway ensuring fidelity of the system despite the degenerate nature of signal sequences (see also and references within). Structures have been determined for SRP (specifically, domain IV of Ffs in complex with the M domain of Ffh) , SRP-FtsY , SRP-RNC, SRP-RNC-FtsY and for the quarternary complex between SRP, FtsY, RNC and the translocon The SRP-FtsY mediated pathway of co-translational protein targeting is believed to represent a diminished version of the eukaryote SRP system which targets proteins to the endoplasmic reticulum (ER). Ffh is homologous to the mammalian SRP54 subunit involved in preprotein signal-sequence binding while the 4.5S RNA (ffs gene product) is homologous to 7SL RNA of eukaryote SRP .

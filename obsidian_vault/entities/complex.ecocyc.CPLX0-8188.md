## Enriched Summary

MinC and MinD form nucleotide dependent copolymeric filaments which bind to lipid membranes; MinCD filaments form the active Z-ring inhibitor complex; MinCD copolymers position MinC for binding to FtsZ filaments. MinE disrupts MinCD filaments; MinE competes with MinC for binding to MinD. MinC and MinD form an alternating copolymer (MinC2:MinD2:MinC2..etc) with a stoichiometric ratio of 1:1 . MinCD copolymers are not the active form of MinCD; mutants that form MinCD heterodimers but are unable to form copolymers are able to regulate Z-ring assembly in vivo .

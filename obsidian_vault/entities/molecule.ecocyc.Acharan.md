## Enriched Summary

Acharan sulfate is a member of the Glycosaminoglycans "glycosaminoglycan" family discovered in the giant African snail TAX-2315439 . It consists of a repeating disaccharide unit composed of -[→4)-N-acetyl-α-D-glucosamine-(1→4)-2-sulfo-α-L-idopyranosyluronate-(1→]-, making it structurally related to HEPARIN and Heparan-Sulfate . In the snail acharan sulfate is produced by internal granules and is mainly secreted into the outer surface of the body as a mucous material . Acharan sulfate has been shown to present several biological activities in vitro, including inhibition of angiogenesis due to inhibition of vascular endothelial growth factor and the decrease of mitogenic activity of fibroblast growth factor .

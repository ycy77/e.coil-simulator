## Enriched Summary

The EutL protein resembles carboxysomal shell proteins and is thought to form a proteinaceous membrane that confines a microcompartment for ethanolamine ammonia-lyase . The flat, cyclic pseudohexameric structure with a central pore may be a basic building block for a microcompartment shell . The pore structure can be triggered to open upon exposure to zinc . Crystal structures of EutL have been solved at 2.2 Å , 2.3 Å and 2.6 Å resolution. The structures show that the protein is arranged in a hexagonal trimeric structure, with the monomers arranged around a large central pore that is either closed or open . In solution, EutL initially elutes as a trimer during size-exclusion chromatography, but forms larger complexes at lower pH or by longer incubation time after cell lysis . The functions of the orthologous eut operon genes for ethanolamine utilization in Salmonella typhimurium have been studied experimentally . A eutL up allele increases fitness during growth in medium containing lignocellulosic hydrolysate .

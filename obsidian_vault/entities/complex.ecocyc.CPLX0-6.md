## Enriched Summary

The transcription factor ModE, for "Molybdenum," is the principal regulator that controls the transcription of operons involved in the transport of molybdenum and synthesis of molybdoenzymes and molybdate-related functions, among others . The regulatory effect of ModE is induced when E. coli is grown under anaerobic growth conditions and when the physiological inducer, molybdenum, binds to ModE and promotes the dimerization of the ModE-Mo complex . The ModE dimer binds two molecules of molybdenum with a Kd of 0.8 mM. Tungsten can act as a substitute for Mo in ModE binding to the modABCD promoter in vitro, but the complex may be biologically inactive . The binding targets for ModE consist of inverted repeat sequences that possess conserved motifs; each monomer binds to one of these conserved sequences .

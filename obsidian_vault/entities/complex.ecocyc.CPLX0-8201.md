## Enriched Summary

GlaH is a glutarate dioxygenase that stereospecifically produces L-2-hydroxyglutarate . GlaH belongs to the non-haem iron(II)-dependent oxygenase superfamily of proteins. Apo- and ligand-bound crystal structures of GlaH ("Gab protein") have been determined . Expression of glaH is induced by carbon starvation and is dependent on σS and cAMP-CRP and repressed by GlaR . Lrp and H-NS are involved in regulation of the operon . Overexpression of GlaH leads to increased tolerance to furfural . A ΔglaH strain accumulates higher levels of glutarate than wild type during carbon starvation and entry into the stationary phase .

## Enriched Summary

YnaI is a mechanosensitive channel of low conductance. In a strain lacking all seven mechanosensitive channels (CPLX0-7627 "MscL", EG11160-MONOMER "MscS", G6255-MONOMER "MscK", CPLX0-7985 MscM, YnaI, CPLX0-7983 "YbiO" and CPLX0-7981 "YbdG"), expression of ynaI from a multicopy plasmid results in stable channel openings in patch clamp experiments . YnaI protects cells against hypoosmotic shock when highly overexpressed . YnaI is predicted to have 5 transmembrane segments; YnaI forms homoheptameric channels . YnaI is a Na+/K+ selective channel and the determinants of ion selectivity have been studied . Cryo-EM structural studies of YnaI are available . YnaI is a member of the small mechanosensitive ion channel MscS family; E. coli K-12 contains contains 6 mechanosensitive channels of small conductance including the archetypical MscS. Sequence analysis identifies ynaI as a segmentally variable gene, that is, one that contains highly variable regions interspersed among well-conserved regions .

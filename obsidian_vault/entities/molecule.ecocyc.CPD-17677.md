## Enriched Summary

Tranylcypromine is a drug of the substituted phenethylamine and amphetamine classes that acts as a monoamine oxidase inhibitor (MAOI). It is indicated primarily for the treatment of major depressive disorder, and can also be used in the management of various mood and anxiety disorders, typically as a last resort after conventional antidepressants have been tried without success. Indications for monoamine oxidase inhibitors are currently very limited, due to their significant potential for adverse effects, many interactions, and the availability of newer and safer (although not necessarily more efficacious) antidepressants. Tranylcypromine is a nonselective and irreversible inhibitor of the enzyme monoamine oxidase (MAO).

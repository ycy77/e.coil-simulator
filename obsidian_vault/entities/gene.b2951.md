## Enriched Summary

yggS (gene.b2951) is a gene entity. It encodes yggS (protein.P67080). Encoded protein function: FUNCTION: Pyridoxal 5'-phosphate (PLP)-binding protein, which is involved in PLP homeostasis. May have a carrier function to deliver PLP to the target enzymes or a protective function so that PLP does not inactivate essential lysines in proteins (PubMed:26872910). Does not have amino acid racemase activity (PubMed:24097949). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:24097949, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26872910}. EcoCyc product frame: G7527-MONOMER. Genomic coordinates: 3095098-3095802. EcoCyc protein note: YggS is a pyridoxal phosphate (PLP)-binding protein that appears to function in PLP homeostasis and/or regulation of CPD0-1942/2-OXOBUTANOATE levels . YggS binds PLP at Lys36 with a KD of 0.37±0.49 µM . YggS belongs to a subfamily of the fold-type III family of PLP-dependent enzymes . No enzymatic function has been identified for YggS or its homologs as of 2023 . yggS mutants were found to have pleiotropic phenotypes. A yggS null mutant is sensitive to sulfa drugs, but not trimethoprim . A yggS deletion strain was found to accumulate 2-ketobutyrate during log phase and the amino acids L-valine, L-leucine, L-histidine, L-phenylalanine, L-tyrosine, as well as 2-aminobutyrate in stationary phase.

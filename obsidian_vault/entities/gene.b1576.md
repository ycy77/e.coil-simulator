## Enriched Summary

ydfD (gene.b1576) is a gene entity. It encodes ydfD (protein.P29010). Encoded protein function: Uncharacterized protein YdfD EcoCyc product frame: EG11303-MONOMER. Genomic coordinates: 1649794-1649985. EcoCyc protein note: YdfD is a lysis protein encoded by the Qin prophage. The protein contains a predicted N-terminal hydrophobic domain, but lacks a recognizable signal peptide . YdfD has been shown to interact directly both in vitro and in vivo with EG10370 and may be a specific inhibitor of IspG; through this interaction YdfD compromises cell wall biosynthesis leading to cell lysis . Inducing expression of YdfD leads to lysis of 99.9% of cells within 2 hours. The predicted cytoplasmic domain, residues 23-63, retains this activity. Both the co-expression of dicB or of SulA, another protein that inhibits cell division, abolishes this phenotype, suggesting that the lytic effect of YdfD is dependent on cell division . A ydfD deletion exhibits aggravating genetic interactions with genes involved in mismatch repair .

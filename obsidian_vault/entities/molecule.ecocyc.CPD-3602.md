## Enriched Summary

Laminaran (also known as laminarin) is a storage glucan produced by brown algae. It is a highly water soluble, branched polysaccharide based on a linear β-(1→3)-linked glucose-based chain with an average degree of polymerization (DP) of 20-30 glucose units and β-(1→6)-linked side chains consisting of one or more glucose residues. The ratio of β(1→3) to β(1→6) linkages is 3:1. Measurements conducted in 2019 along transects in the Arctic, Atlantic, and Pacific oceans revealed a median concentration of 26 ± 17% laminarin within the particulate organic carbon pool .

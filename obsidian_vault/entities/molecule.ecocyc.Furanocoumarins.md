## Enriched Summary

The Furanocoumarins furanocoumarins are one of the main groups of coumarins. Their synthesis starts with the coupling of CPD-4211 to the coumarin CPD-8186, leading to formation of a skeleton that contains a furan ring fused with coumarin. Furanocoumarins are minor constituents in plants belonging to TAX-4037, TAX-3803, TAX-3487, and TAX-23513 families. They are natural toxins that protect the plants from insects, herbivory, and microbes. Production is triggered by several types of stresses, including fungal infections and stresses such as wounding or acid fog . Furanocoumarins are also toxic to humans. Direct contact with the sap from some plants containing furanocoumarins (such as TAX-380077) causes inflammation, burns and irritating sensitivity to sunlight . Some naturally occurring methoxy derivatives of furanocoumarins have been applied clinically, coupled with exposure to sunlight, to cure dermatological diseases for more than 2000 years.

## Enriched Summary

Sialic acids are an important family of related 9-carbon sugars acids, present on the surface of many different cells and functioning in a wide range of different biological processes. Among the sialic acids, N-ACETYLNEURAMINATE is the most frequently found member in humans, and CPD-273 is abundant in many other species. A total of more than 50 different sialic acids have been described, including the deaminated compound CPD-21259 "3-deoxy-D-glycero-D-galacto-nononate" or KDN . They can be O-acetylated in positions 4, 7, or 9 , but also N-deacetylated, O-methylated, sulfated, or modified by lactonization .

## Annotation

This G6680 gene in TAX-562 K-12 was identified as a putative homolog of the λ phage cI repressor gene and is located upstream of G6681 and G6682, coding for transcription regulators . RacR is characterized as an essential transcriptional regulator of ydaS and ydaT genes. Deletion of the racR gene in E. coli K-12 is lethal except when the adjacent, divergent ydaS gene or both ydaST genes are deleted. RacR binds to the intergenic region (IGR) between racR and ydaS and represses transcription of genes immediately downstream of the IGR in multiple mutants. Silencing of racR expression results in increased expression of both ydaS and ydaT as well as YdaS levels . RacR appears to bind DNA as a dimer . This protein has two domains: the N-terminal domain responsible for binding DNA and the C-terminal domain involved in protein oligomerization . Studies with silencing racR in deletion mutant strains of ΔydaS, ΔydaT, and ΔydaS ΔydaT resulted in differential effects on cell viability and morphology; YdaS was predominantly contributing to cell toxicity whereas YdaT contributed to both toxicity and increased filamentation . Derepression of ydaST and subsequent, extensive cell filamentation was observed when racR expression was reduced by a C protein regulator of the R-M system resulting from transcription factor cross-talk when the TAX-544 sp. Csp231I restriction-modification (R-M) system was introduced into E. coli K-12 . When RacR is overexpressed, growth is inhibited in E. coli strains JM83 or DH5 but not in strains BL21(DE3) or MG1655 . When the expression of the racR gene is reduced, the prophage Rac, which contains the gene, is excised from the genome, possibly as a counteracting measure to protect the cell from the toxicity of the yda genes .

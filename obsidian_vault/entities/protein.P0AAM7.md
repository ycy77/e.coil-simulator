## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Involved in the maturation of [NiFe] hydrogenases. Involved in the biosynthesis of the Fe(CN)(2)CO cofactor (PubMed:16412426). HybG delivers iron-bound CO(2) to HypD where reduction to CO probably occurs (PubMed:23851071). In complex with HypD, accepts the cyanide ligand generated by HypF and HypE, and also coordinates the carbon monoxide ligand (By similarity). Involved in the maturation of the hydrogenase 2. Also participates in the maturation of hydrogenase 1 (PubMed:11292801). {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:P0AAM3, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11292801, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23851071, ECO:0000305|PubMed:16412426}. HybG is a chaperone-like protein which is specifically required for the generation of active FORMHYDROG2-CPLX (Hyd-2) and can also function in the maturation of FORMHYDROGI-CPLX (Hyd-1) . HybG appears to be the carrier that delivers the Fe(CN)2CO group to HYBC-MONOMER large subunit of hydrogenase 2 (pre-HybC) . HybG and pre-HybC interact. The amino-terminal methionine residue of HybG appears to be removed, and the second amino acid residue, cysteine, is required for interaction of HybG with HybC and for Hyd-1 and Hyd-2 maturation . Co-purification assays confirmed interactions between HybG and the large subunits of Hyd-1 and Hyd-2 (HyaB and HybC), as well as HypD and HypE . The C-terminal 15 amino acids of HybC are required for interaction with HybG...

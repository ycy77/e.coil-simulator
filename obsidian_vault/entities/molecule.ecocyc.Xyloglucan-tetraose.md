## Enriched Summary

Xyloglucan is the major hemicellulose in dicotyledon type I cell walls and has a backbone of β-1,4-linked cellotetraose units, which are substituted in C-6 by a-D-xylosyl residues at two or three of the first three glucosyl residues (from the reducing end) . Some of the xylosyl residues are further substituted by galactosyl, arabinosyl or fucosyls residues . The cross-linking between xyloglucan chains is a major factor in the stability of the three-dimensional matrix between adjacent cellulose microfibrils . A common terminology refers to a glucosyl unit within a xylosylglycan as G and to a glucose residues substituted by D-xylose in a (1,6)-α-linkage as X. Using this terminology, most xylosylglucans consist of repeating heptasaccharide XXXG units. Subsequently (1,2)-β-D-galactosyl residues are added at the second and/or third xylose residue and an(1,2)-α-L-fucose is added at the galactosyl unit of the third xylose residue .

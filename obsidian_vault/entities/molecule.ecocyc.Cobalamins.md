## Enriched Summary

Cobamides "A cobamide" is a corrinoid molecule based on the structure of CPD-20843, a cobalt-corrinoid hexaamide that is formed from COBINAMIDE by the attachment of an α-D-ribofuranose 3-phosphate residue at the 2 position of the aminopropanol chain. Active forms of cobamides, such as the B12 vitamins, contain upper (β) and lower (α) ligands. The most common type of cobamides, Cobalamins cobalamin, contains DIMETHYLBENZIMIDAZOLE as the lower ligand. Since the cobalamin term does not specify the upper axial ligand, it includes several compounds that differ in their upper axial ligand. The functional forms of cobalamin, CPD-9037 and ADENOSYLCOBALAMIN, include either a methyl group or an 5'-deoxyadenosyl group as their upper axial ligand, respectively. The form of cobalamin known as vitamin B12 is the artificially-made CPD-315, which is converted to an active form in the human body upon ingestion.

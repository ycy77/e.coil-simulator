## Enriched Summary

Furan-containing fatty acids are generated in large amounts in algae, but they are also produced by plants and microorganisms. Fish and other marine organisms as well as mammals consume these acids in their food and incorporate them into phospholipids and cholesterol esters. They are catabolized to dibasic urofuran acids, which are excreted in the urine. Furan-containing fatty acids react readily with peroxyl radicals to generate dioxoenes. Their radical-scavenging ability may contribute to the protective properties of fish and fish oil diets against mortality from heart disease .

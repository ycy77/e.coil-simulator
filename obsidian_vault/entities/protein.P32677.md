## Enriched Summary

Uncharacterized HTH-type transcriptional regulator YijO YijO, which contains a helix-turn-helix motif to bind DNA in its C-terminal domain, appears to belong to the AraC family of transcription factors and it was predicted to regulate genes related to metabolism . Upstream of the yijO gene, a sequence was found that could work as a transcriptional promoter, and the transcription of the yijO gene is affected by conditions that generate general transcriptional termination or lead to biofilm formation . A mutation affecting yijO was identified as one of several mutations in an evolved strain adapted to osmotic stress by growth in 0.3 M NaCl . The YijO binding landscape was globally mapped in vivo by standardized ChIP-seq and quantitatively modeled using BoltzNet, a biophysically informed neural network that infers binding energy from sequence data . The resulting ChIP-seq binding regions are available in RegulonDB (Galagan collection), and the corresponding energy matrix and genomic binding profiles can be accessed at boltznet.bu.edu .

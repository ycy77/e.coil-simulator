## Enriched Summary

Inner membrane transport protein YnfM Expression of cloned ynfLM in a K-12 strain which lacks the major efflux pump permease AcrB (E. coli KAM3; see ) confers hypersensitivity to acriflavine (minimum inhibitory concentration, 12.5 µg/mL for KAM3 versus 3.13 µg/mL for KAM3/pUCynfLM) but does not impact the resistance to a range of other antibiotics, dyes and toxic compounds; expression of cloned ynfM (pTrcHynfM) cannot be detected and KAM3 cells harbouring this expression vector do not exhibit the acriflavine hypersensitivity phenotype . In the Transporter Classification Database, YnfM is a member of the Acriflavin-sensitivity family within the Major Facilitator Superfamily (MFS) . YnfM has been implicated in arabinose efflux . ynfM is one of 35 efflux-encoding genes that have been deleted or inactivated in 'Efflux KnockOut-35' (EKO-35) - a K-12 BW25133-derived strain that lacks 35 efflux pumps and can be used as a platform to study their function .

## Enriched Summary

Cobamides "A cobamide" is a corrinoid molecule based on the structure of CPD-20843, a cobalt-corrinoid hexaamide that is formed from COBINAMIDE by the attachment of an α-D-ribofuranose 3-phosphate residue at the 2 position of the aminopropanol chain. Biologically active forms of cobamides contain an upper (β) and a lower (α) axial ligands. The most common upper axial ligand are either a methyl group or a 5'-deoxyadenosyl group, although some other forms exist. More variability is found with the lower axial ligand, where more that 16 different forms have been described. Cobamides that have an adenine molecule as the lower ligand are known as pseudocobalamins. They are known to be produced by different bacterial genera . Many cyanobacterial species produce pseudocobalamin, which is used as a cofactor in their specialized methionine synthase (MetH) .

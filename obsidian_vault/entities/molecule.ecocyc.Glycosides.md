## Enriched Summary

A glycoside is any molecule in which a sugar group is bonded through its anomeric carbon to a non-acyl group via a glycosidic bond. Glycosides can be linked by a C-O bond (an O-glycoside), C-N bond (a glycosylamine), C-S bond (a thioglycoside) or a C-C bond (a C-glycoside). When the sugar is bound to a non-carbohydrate moiety, the sugar group is known as the glycone and the non-sugar group as the aglycone or genin part of the glycoside.

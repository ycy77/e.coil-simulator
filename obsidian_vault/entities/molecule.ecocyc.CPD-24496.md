## Enriched Summary

CPD-24496 "A cobinamide" is a cobalt-corrinoid hexaamide. The key structural feature of cobinamides is a corrin ring with a cobalt ion in its center. Four of the six coordination sites of the cobalt ion are provided by the corrin ring. In many cobinamides a fifth coordination site is provided by Cobamide-Upper-Ligands "an upper ligand" located above the ring plane. Unlike in Cobamides cobamides, cobinamides lack Cobamide-Lower-Ligands "a lower ligand". The three common upper ligands are an 5'-deoxyadenosyl group, as in ADENOSYLCOBINAMIDE, a methyl group, as in CPD-24497, and a cyano group, as in CPD-22314.

## Enriched Summary

Bile (or gall) is a dark green to yellowish brown fluid, produced by the liver of most vertebrates, that aids the digestion of lipids in the small intestine . In humans bile is produced continuously by the liver and stored and concentrated in the gall bladder. After eating, the bile is discharged into the the first section of the small intestine (the duodenum). The main active ingredient (about 0.7%) of the bile is bile acids. Bile acids are classified into primary and secondary. The primary bile acids are produced in the liver from CHOLESTEROL, while the secondary bile acids are produced by gut bacteria that modify the primary acids. About half of the cholesterol produced in the body is used for bile acid synthesis. The primary bile acids are conjugated in the liver with GLY or TAURINE before secretion into the bile. The structures and proportions of the different bile acids vary among different species . Bile acids also act as steroid hormones through their interaction with receptors encoded by the NR1H4 and GPBAR1 genes .

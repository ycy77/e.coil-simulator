## Enriched Summary

The CpG sites or CG sites are regions of DNA where a cytosine nucleotide occurs 5' to a guanine nucleotide. "CpG" is shorthand for "-C-phosphate-G-", that is, cytosine and guanine separated by a single phosphate group. The "CpG" notation is used to distinguish this linear sequence from the CG base-pairing of cytosine and guanine. Cytosines in CpG dinucleotides are often methylated to form 5-methylcytosine.

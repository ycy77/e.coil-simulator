## Enriched Summary

A nucleotide is composed of Bases (nitrogenous base), a five-carbon sugar (either CPD0-1110 ribose or 2-DEOXYRIBOSE), and one to three phosphate groups. When joined together, nucleotides make up the structural units of RNA and DNA. The nucleotides are called Ribonucleotides when the sugar is CPD0-1110 ribose, and Deoxynucleotides when it is 2-DEOXYRIBOSE.

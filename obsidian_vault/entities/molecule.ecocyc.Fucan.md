## Enriched Summary

Fucan is a generic term used to define a family of L-fucoses-containing sulfated polysaccharides with extremely variable molecular weights that are produced by brown algae (TAX-2870) and marine vertebrates . It has been recommended to use this term to refer to polysaccharide based mainly on sulfated L-fucose, with less than 10% other monosaccharides . In marine invertebrates, sulfated fucans have been found in the egg jelly coat of sea urchins and in the body wall of sea cucumber . In brown algae the fucan is present in the cell walls and can account for more than 40% of the dry weight of isolated cell walls . The fucans of brown algae are often called Fucoidans fucoidans. ## Annotation

Fucan is a generic term used to define a family of L-fucoses-containing sulfated polysaccharides with extremely variable molecular weights that are produced by brown algae (TAX-2870) and marine vertebrates . It has been recommended to use this term to refer to polysaccharide based mainly on sulfated L-fucose, with less than 10% other monosaccharides . In marine invertebrates, sulfated fucans have been found in the egg jelly coat of sea urchins and in the body wall of sea cucumber . In brown algae the fucan is present in the cell walls and can account for more than 40% of the dry weight of isolated cell walls . The fucans of brown algae are often called Fucoidans fucoidans. The structures of fucans vary among species and sometimes among different parts of the organism . Fucan structures can be divided into three main groups: one group, found in TAX-33637 species of brown algae and several species of sea urchines, has a central chain composed of (1→3)-linked α-L-fucopyranose residues ; a second group, found so far only in the sea urchins TAX-7640 and TAX-7671, has a central chain composed of (1→4)-linked α-L-fucopyranose residues ; the third group, which includes fucoidan isolated from TAX-52968 and TAX-3011 species of brown algae, has a central chain composed of repeating (1→3) and (1→4) linked α-L-fucopyranose residues .

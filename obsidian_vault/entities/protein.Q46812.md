## Enriched Summary

Putative aminohydrolase SsnA (EC 3.-.-.-) SsnA has similarity to allantoinase enzymes . Purified SsnA does not have isoguanine deaminase activity . An ssnA mutant retains a higher number of viable cells upon entry into stationary phase in rich media than wild type . Strains harboring high-copy episomal ssnA show aberrant cell morphology and growth defects, and induction of ssnA expression correlates with decreased viability . Increased expression of σE suppresses ssnA-dependent growth inhibition . Expression of ssnA increases upon entry into stationary phase; expression is negatively regulated by RpoS . SsnA: subject to RpoS-dependent negative regulation in stationary phase A

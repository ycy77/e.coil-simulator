## Enriched Summary

PqsD from TAX-287 is a member of the FabH family of condensing enzymes that participates in the synthesis of the signal molecule CPD-12838 (see PWY-6660) and of 4-hydroxy-2-1H-quinolone (DHQ) , a molecule whose function is not yet known (see PWY-6661). PqsD catalyzes the condensation of 2-AMINOBENZOYL-COA (anthraniloyl-CoA) with MALONYL-COA. The enzyme forms an acyl-enzyme intermediate with 2-AMINOBENZOYL-COA, which then reacts with MALONYL-COA to produce the short-lived thioester CPD-19113. This compound forms a branch - in the absence of PqsE it is susceptible to spontaneous cyclization to form 4-hydroxy-2-1H-quinolone. However, when PqsE is present, it removes the CoA moiety, forming CPD-19114 . The enzyme activity was analyzed by TLC analysis of reaction mixtures containing MALONYL-COA and [14C]2-AMINOBENZOYL-COA. Products were quantitated by LC-MS . The crystal structure of recombinant wild-type and mutant enzymes has been determined. The protein was found to be a homodimer and has structural similarities to a variety of condensing enzymes including EG10277 from TAX-562 (see CPLX0-252) .

## Enriched Summary

hypC (gene.b2728) is a gene entity. It encodes hypC (protein.P0AAM3). Encoded protein function: FUNCTION: Involved in the maturation of [NiFe] hydrogenases. Involved in the biosynthesis of the Fe(CN)(2)CO cofactor (PubMed:15504408, PubMed:23022438). HypC delivers iron-bound CO(2) to HypD where reduction to CO probably occurs (PubMed:23851071). In complex with HypD, accepts the cyanide ligand generated by HypF and HypE, and also coordinates the carbon monoxide ligand (PubMed:15504408, PubMed:23022438). Involved in the maturation of the hydrogenase 3 (PubMed:10783387, PubMed:11292801). Also participates in the maturation of hydrogenase 1 (PubMed:11292801). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10783387, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11292801, ECO:0000269|PubMed:15504408, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23022438, ECO:0000269|PubMed:23851071}. EcoCyc product frame: EG10485-MONOMER. Genomic coordinates: 2851864-2852136. EcoCyc protein note: HypC is an accessory protein primarily involved in the maturation of the HYDROG3-CPLX isoenzyme and also participates in the maturation of FORMHYDROGI-CPLX hydrogenase 1 . Current data suggests that HypC delivers Fe-CO2 to HypD, where CO2 is reduced to CO . The precursor of mature HycE (pre-HycE), the large subunit of hydrogenase 3, interacts directly with HypC, while the processed form of HycE is not associated with HypC...

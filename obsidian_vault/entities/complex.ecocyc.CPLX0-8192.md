## Annotation

GrpE is a nucleotide exchange factor in the EG10241-MONOMER "DnaK"-DnaJ-GrpE chaperone system. GrpE regulates the release of ADP from DnaK, providing further stimulation for the turnover of ATP; GrpE may act as a timing factor that speeds up DnaK-substrate association/dissociation cycles . GrpE reduces the affinity of ADP for DnaK 200 fold; GrpE facilitates the switch to the ATP state by allowing for fast ADP dissociation and thus ATP rebinding . GrpE accelerates the rate of peptide release from ADP-DnaK . GrpE is implicated in the modulation of DnaK's interdomain allostery (and also ). GrpE acts as a thermosensor; at high temperatures (above 40 °C) reversible structural change of GrpE shifts DnaK towards the ADP bound state (known as the R state); in this state the affinity of DnaK for substrate is high which results in increased sequestering of substrate and improved chaperone function during heat shock . GrpE and DnaK interact in vitro and in vivo . GrpE exists as a dimer or higher order oligomer in solution . The GrpE homodimer consists of two unusually long, paired α helices that lead to a smaller 4 helix bundle; GrpE binds DnaK with a 2:1 stoichiometry; in complex with the ATPase domain of DnaK the structure of the GrpE dimer is asymmetric; the long helices are minimally involved in the interaction with DnaK's ATPase domain and may interact with the peptide binding domain of DnaK . The flexible N-terminal domain (residues 1-33) interacts with DnaK, possibly binding in the substrate binding domain . grpE mutants cannot support replication of bacteriophage λ . grpE is essential for growth at all temperatures . Expression of grpE is induced by heat shock in a σ32 dependent manner Review: grp: gro-P like

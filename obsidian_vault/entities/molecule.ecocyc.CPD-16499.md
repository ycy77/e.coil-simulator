## Enriched Summary

Iron(III) oxide-hydroxides may occur in anhydrous [FeO(OH)] or hydrated [FeO(OH)·nH2O] forms. The most common forms of iron(III) oxide-hydroxides are the minerals goethite and lepidocrocite, but other forms exist, including akaganeite, feroxyhyte, siderogel and limonite.

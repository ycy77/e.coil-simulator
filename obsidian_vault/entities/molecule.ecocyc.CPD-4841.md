## Enriched Summary

CPD-4841 Dipicolinate is a small polar molecule that accumulates to high concentrations in bacterial endospores (up to 25% of spore core dry weight) , and is thought to play a role in spore heat esistance, or the maintenance of heat resistance . The biosynthetic pathway for CPD-4841 has been studued (see PWY-8088). Not much is known about the degradation of CPD-4841. A Gram-negative, non-sporeforming strictly anaerobic marine bacterium could utilize it as sole source of carbon, energy, and nitrogen, and fermented it to acetate, propionate, ammonia, and 2 molecules of CO2 . Another study has shown that the compound can be transformed by aerobic marine bacteria to CPD-22553, which was then photolyzed with a half-life of 100 minutes. The details of this degradation process are not known .

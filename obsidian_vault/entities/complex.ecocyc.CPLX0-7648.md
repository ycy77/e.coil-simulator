## Enriched Summary

RseB interacts with EG12341-MONOMER "RseA", the anti-sigma factor that inhibits RPOE-MONOMER "sigma E" . RseB is a negative regulator of sigma E activity . RseB stimulates binding of RseA to sigma E . RseB binds to the periplasmic region of RseA and prevents EG11652-MONOMER "DegS" from cleaving RseA . Lipopolysaccharide (LPS) and lipid A fragments displace RseA from RseB in vitro. LPS binds RseB and coactivates cleavage of RseA. Off pathway LPS intermediates are thus thought to be one of two signals (the other being misfolded/unfolded OMPs) that are necessary for DegS cleavage of RseA and activation of sigma E . ## Annotation

RseB interacts with EG12341-MONOMER "RseA", the anti-sigma factor that inhibits RPOE-MONOMER "sigma E" . RseB is a negative regulator of sigma E activity . RseB stimulates binding of RseA to sigma E . RseB binds to the periplasmic region of RseA and prevents EG11652-MONOMER "DegS" from cleaving RseA . Lipopolysaccharide (LPS) and lipid A fragments displace RseA from RseB in vitro. LPS binds RseB and coactivates cleavage of RseA. Off pathway LPS intermediates are thus thought to be one of two signals (the other being misfolded/unfolded OMPs) that are necessary for DegS cleavage of RseA and activation of sigma E . Upon extracytoplasmic stress, misfolded/unfolded porins interact with the DegS PDZ domain, releasing an inhibitory interaction and activating DegS protease . DegS and EG12436-MONOMER "RseP" then sequentially catalyze proteolytic cleavage of RseA, thereby relieving repression of sigma E and activating the sigma E-mediated stress response . The sigma E system is also involved in the response to hyperosmotic shock and in release of nutrients from dead cells during long periods of stationary phase . RseB is localized to the periplasm . Crystals of RseB that diffract to 2.8 Å and 2.4 Å show that it exists as a homodimer, with each constituent monomer composed of two domains, one large, one small . An rseB mutant shows wild-type sigma E activity under inducing conditions, and exhibits a small increase in sigma E activity under non-inducing conditions . RseB: "regulator of sigma E" .

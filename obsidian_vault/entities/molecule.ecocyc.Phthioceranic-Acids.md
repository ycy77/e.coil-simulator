## Enriched Summary

A unique feature of mycobacterial cell wall lipids is the presence of very long chain fatty acids with multiple methyl branches toward the carboxyl end of the carbon chain. Many types of such acids exist, including Mycosanoic-Acids "mycosanoic acids", Mycolipanoic-Acids "mycolipanoic acids", Mycolipanolic-Acids "mycolipanolic acids", Mycolipenic-Acids "mycolipenic acids", Mycolipodienoic-Acids "mycolipodienoic acids", Phthioceranic-Acids "phthioceranic acids", and Mycocerosic-Acids "mycocerosic acids". Phthioceranic acids are hepta and octa-methylated alkanoic very-long chain fatty acids found in Mycobacteria . The chiral centers bearing the methyl groups have a D (dextrorotatory) stereo configuration . In heptamethylated phthioceranic acids R1 = H. In octamethylated phthioceranic acids R1=CH3.

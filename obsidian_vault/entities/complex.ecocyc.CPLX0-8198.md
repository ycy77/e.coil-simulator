## Enriched Summary

NlpI is a lipoprotein that appears to be involved in cell division . NlpI crystallises as a dimer; NlpI is a tetratricopeptide repeat (TPR)-containing globular protein (see further ). NlpI localizes along the entire cell envelope and its concentration remains constant during the cell cycle . An nlpI mutation (nlpI::Tn5) is associated with increased production of outer membrane vesicles - a phenotype that is associated with improved bacterial survival under stress . A ΔnlpI mutation results in a hypervesiculating phenotype possibly due to high levels of peptidoglycan synthesis and decreased levels of Lpp-outer membrane cross-links; deletion of mepS suppresses the hypervesiculating phenotype associated with deletion of nlpI . An nlpI::cm mutation results in a filamentous phenotype at high temperature (42°C) and increased sensitivity to osmotic stress.

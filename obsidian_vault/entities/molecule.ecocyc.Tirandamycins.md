## Enriched Summary

The tirandamycins belong to a small group of natural products based on tetramic acid, containing a 2,4-pyrrolidinedione ring system. Compounds on this gamily demonstrate extensive biological activities such as antibacterial, antiviral, antiulcerative, cytotoxic, mycotoxic, and antitumor . In addition to dienoyltetramic acid moiety, tirandamycins possess a bicyclic ketal skeleton that is also found in some other antimicrobial tetramic acid antibiotics, such as CPD-18222, CPD-18221, CPD-18224, and CPD-18223 . Tirandamycins inhibit the bacterial RNA polymerase (RNAP), and inhibit both chain initiation and chain elongation . CPD-18214 "Tirandamycin A" was first isolated from the terrestrial bacterium ORG-6282 , and its absolute stereochemistry was determined by X-ray diffraction shortly after . Tirandamycin A and its other congeners CPD-18216, CPD-18217, and CPD-18218 were later reisolated or discovered from TAX-67297 , the marine-derived TAX-529089 , and TAX-861528, which was isolated from South China Sea marine sediment.

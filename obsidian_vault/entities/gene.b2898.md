ygfZ (gene.b2898) is a gene entity. It encodes ygfZ (protein.P0ADE8). Encoded protein function: FUNCTION: Folate-binding protein involved in regulating the level of ATP-DnaA and in the modification of some tRNAs. It is probably a key factor in regulatory networks that act via tRNA modification, such as initiation of chromosomal replication. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:16359333}. EcoCyc product frame: G7511-MONOMER. EcoCyc synonyms: yzzW. Genomic coordinates: 3041313-3042293. EcoCyc protein note: YgfZ is a folate (most likely tetrahydrofolate)-dependent protein . Although various physiological functions for YgfZ have been proposed, its true function is still unknown. YgfZ is currently thought to play a role in Fe-S cluster synthesis or repair during oxidative stress . The mitochondrial homolog of YgfZ, IBA57, promotes assembly of [4Fe-4S] clusters and their insertion into acceptor proteins. However, while IBA57 is essential, YgfZ is not. YgfZ is needed for activity of the radical S-adenosyl enzymes G6364 MiaB and G6435 RimO, which thiomethylate some tRNAs and the S12 ribosomal protein, respectively.

## Enriched Summary

Maackiain Maackiain is a common pterocarpan that has been detected in many legumes. Within the two subtribes Diocleinae and Erythrininae it was found in 12 species representing 4 genera . Maackiain was originally discovered in the TAX-37501 tree, which is named after its discoverer, the 19th-century Siberian explorer Karlovich Maack. Maackiain can be found in both a (-) and a (+) conformations. In chickpea, where it is a major pterocarpan phytoalexin together with medicarpin, it occurs exclusively in the (-) conformation .

## Enriched Summary

While D-Ribofuranose (D-Ribf) is present in the O antigens of several bacterial strains (such as the TAX-562 serogroups O5, O20, O54, O105, O114, O153, O178, O183 and O185), ribofuranose transfer reactions are not well characterized. The donor in these reactions is assumed to be a nucleotide diphosphate form, as is standard in this type of reactions, though this has not been verified. As of 2022, the existence of a nucleotide-activated ribofuranose has not been demonstrated in any bacterial strain . Studies of the biosynthesis of the D-ribofuranose donor in Salmonella suggested that it is not produced by a hexose to pentose conversion .

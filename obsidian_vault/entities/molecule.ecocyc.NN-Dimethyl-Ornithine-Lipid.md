## Enriched Summary

Ornithine lipids are phosphorus-free membrane lipids that are widespread in bacteria but absent from archaea and eukaryotes. They comprise of LYSO-ORNITHINE-LIPIDS with a second fatty acyl group ester-linked to the 3-hydroxy position of the first fatty acyl group (reviewed in ). The composition of the amide-linked (R1) and ester-linked (R2) fatty acids in ornithine lipids varies among bacterial species . In addition, ornithine lipids are often modified by hydroxylation and methylation at different positions .

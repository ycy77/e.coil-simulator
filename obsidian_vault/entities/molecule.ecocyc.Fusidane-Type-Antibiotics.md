## Enriched Summary

Fusidane-type antibiotics are a group of fungi-derived 29-nor-protostane triterpenoid antibiotics that are characterized by a Z-configured double bond between C17 and C20, a C21 carboxylic acid, and a 16-OAc in the protostadienol framework. Fusidane-type antibiotics are the only known clinically used antibiotics that target elongation factor G (EF-G) to inhibit bacterial protein synthesis. Owing to their unique mechanism of action, they have little cross-resistance with other commonly used antibiotics .

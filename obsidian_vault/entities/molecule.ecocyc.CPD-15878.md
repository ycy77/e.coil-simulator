## Enriched Summary

Membrane protein integrase (MPIase) is an inner membrane glycolipid in E. coli K-12 composed of diacylglycerol linked by pyrophosphate to a repeating trisaccharide unit composed of three acetylated aminosugars (N-acetyl-D-glucosamine, 2-acetamido-2-deoxymannuronic acid, and 4-acetamido-4-deoxyfucose) . MPIase differs from the related glycolipid, CPD-21605 enterobacterial common antigenPG (ECAPG), owing to the length of its glycan chain (n = 9-11) and its pyrophosphate linkage . The complete pathway for biosynthesis remains unknown and does not appear to involve proteins that are responsible for ECA production; CDPDIGLYSYN-MONOMER and G6728 are involved in synthesis of 'compound 1' (CPD0-2717 GlcNAc-PP-DAG) which is the first step (and see ). Membrane protein integrase is implicated in the protein translocation and integration pathways of the inner membrane .

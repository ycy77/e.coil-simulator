## Enriched Summary

Haptens are small molecules that elicit an immune response only when attached to a large carrier such as a protein. The FORSSMAN-HAPTEN "Forssman hapten" (Fs), which was described in 1911 , is one of the most potent haptenic sphingoglycolipids . Primates, including humans, lack Fs synthase activity, and have naturally occurring Fs antibodies in plasma. However, some individuals with the enigmatic ABO subgroup Apae were shown to expressed Fs glycolipids on their erythrocites . The compound was also reported from human tumors . Primates and humans have a gene that encodes the Fs synthase, but it encodes an inactive protein . In individuals that do express the Fs antigen, the HS07505 gene contains a mutation that results in an arginine to glutamine change at residue 296. Transfection experiments and molecular modeling showed that the Agr296Gln mutation reactivates the human Fs synthase. Based on these findings, the authors suggested to name a new histo-blood group system in man, FORS .

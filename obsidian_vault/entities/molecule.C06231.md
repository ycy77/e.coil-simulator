## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: Ectoine; L-Ectoine; (4S)-2-Methyl-1,4,5,6-tetrahydropyrimidine-4-carboxylate ECTOINE Ectoine is a natural compound found in high concentrations in some halophilic microorganisms. It is a compatible solute, helping the organisms survive extreme osmotic stress by acting as an osmolyte. Along with BETAINE, it is one of the two predominant compatible solutes in halophilic bacteria . Ectoine was discovered in the halophilic γ-proteobacterium TAX-1052 (at the time identified only as a species of TAX-1051, hence the name ectoine) , but is now known to be synthesized by a wide range of Gram-negative and Gram-positive bacteria. ECTOINE Ectoine has commercial uses as an enzyme stabilizer and as a cell protectant in skin and health care applications, and is produced in an industrial process using the halophilic bacterium TAX-2746 as a producer strain.

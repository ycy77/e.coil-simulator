## Enriched Summary

MenI (YdiI) is the 1,4-dihydroxy-2-naphthoyl-CoA thioesterase enzyme in the biosynthesis of menaquinone . Esterase activity of MenI was initially discovered in a high-throughput screen of purified proteins . Crystal structures of MenI alone and in complexes with inert substrate analogs have been solved . Enzymes with mutations in predicted active site residues have been purified and kinetically characterized . MenI belongs to the 4-hydroxybenzoyl-CoA thioesterase subfamily of the hot dog fold superfamily of proteins . Structural and functional comparisons between MenI and its paralog CPLX0-7766 EntH provided insight into the substrate specificity and evolution of the hotdog fold enzymes . The quarternary structure is best described as a dimer of dimers . A menI mutant produces significantly less menaquinone than wild type and shows reduced growth under anaerobic conditions with glucose and fumarate as the electron acceptor .

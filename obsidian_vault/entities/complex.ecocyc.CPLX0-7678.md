## Enriched Summary

ZntR, "zinc transport regulator," regulates a gene involved in the Zn(II), Cd(II), and Pb(II) transport systems . In the presence of any of these ions, ZntR activates transcription of zntA by a DNA distortion mechanism that makes the promoter a better substrate for RNA polymerase . The interaction of ZntR with Zn(II) has been characterized , but its interaction with other ions has not been described. ## Annotation

ZntR, "zinc transport regulator," regulates a gene involved in the Zn(II), Cd(II), and Pb(II) transport systems . In the presence of any of these ions, ZntR activates transcription of zntA by a DNA distortion mechanism that makes the promoter a better substrate for RNA polymerase . The interaction of ZntR with Zn(II) has been characterized , but its interaction with other ions has not been described. When ZntR is bound to zinc, it activates transcription, but when it is not bound to zinc and is present in a high concentration, it is able to repress transcription . An inverted repeat sequence of 22 bp, located in the spacer between the -10 and the -35 boxes, has been identified as the ZntR dimer-binding site . For the ZntR, CueR, and Zur proteins, a temporal correlation of approximately one second between binding and unbinding to DNA is observed, along with a spatial restriction of approximately 100 nm . This 100 nm microdomain includes the DNA region containing the sequence recognized by the transcription factor (TF), the surrounding space, and the TFs themselves . It is suggested that these microdomains, which could function as cellular compartments without involving membranes, possess particular physicochemical properties and could increase the frequency of transcription factors (TFs) binding to DNA . The crystal structure of an N-terminally truncated fragment of ZntR bound to zinc was solved at a resolution of 19 Å . Each monomer of ZntR has three domains: a dimerization helix, a signal recognition site and a DNA-binding motif (helix-turn-helix) . Two zinc ions are bound to each monomer .

## Enriched Summary

Fatty-Acids "A fatty acid" is a carboxylic acid with a long unbranched aliphatic tail. Even though fatty acids derived from natural fats and oils, such as CPD-195, are usually at least 8 carbon atoms long, carboxylic acids as short as BUTYRIC_ACID (4 carbon atoms) are considered to be fatty acids. When a fatty acid contains only single bonds, it is referred to as Saturated-Fatty-Acids. When it contains one or more double bonds, it is referred to as Unsaturated-Fatty-Acids. If it contains a double bond in the trans configuration, it is often referred to as a trans fatty acid.

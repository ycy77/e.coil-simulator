## Enriched Summary

Cobamides "A cobamide" is a corrinoid molecule based on the structure of CPD-20843, a cobalt-corrinoid hexaamide that is formed from COBINAMIDE by the attachment of an α-D-ribofuranose 3-phosphate residue at the 2 position of the aminopropanol chain. Biologically active forms of cobamides contain an upper (β) and a lower (α) axial ligands. The most common upper axial ligand are either a methyl group or a 5'-deoxyadenosyl group, although some other forms exist. More variability is found with the lower axial ligand, where more that 16 different forms have been described. Some procedure for cobamide purification involve the replacement of the upper ligand with a cyano group. A common example is CPD-315, also known as vitamin B12. Cyanocobamides do not occur in living organisms, and are only produced artificially. This class includes all cobamides that have a cyano group as their upper axial ligand.

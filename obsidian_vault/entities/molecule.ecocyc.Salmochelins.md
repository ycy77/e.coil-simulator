## Enriched Summary

Salmochelins are C-glucosylated siderophores related to enterobactin that are produced by Salmonella species, uropathogenic and avian pathogenic Escherichia coli strains, and certain Klebsiella strains. Mono-, di-, and triglucosylated forms have been reported . The glucosylation has been interpreted as a bacterial evasion mechanism against the mammalian catecholate siderophore-binding protein siderocalin.

In the major PGL of TAX-1769, known as PGL-1, the phenolphthiocerol moiety is attached to the trisaccharide 3,6-di-O-methyl-β-D-glucopyranosyl-(1→4)-2,3-di-O-methyl-α-L-rhamnopyranosyl-(1→3)-3-O-methyl-α-L-rhamnopyranose and the two secondary alcohol functions are esterified by Mycocerosic-Acids "mycocerosic acids" . PGL-1 inhibits the proliferation of T lymphocytes after stimulation with concanavalin A, seems to be associated with resistance to intracellular killing by macrophages, and promotes phagocytosis of TAX-1769 by macrophages and Schwann cells through binding to complement component C3 and laminin-2, respectively.

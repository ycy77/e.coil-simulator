## Enriched Summary

yfhM (gene.b2520) is a gene entity. It encodes yfhM (protein.P76578). Encoded protein function: FUNCTION: Protects the bacterial cell from host peptidases (PubMed:18697741, PubMed:26100869, PubMed:26143919). Acts by a 'trapping' mechanism. Cleavage of the bait-region domain by host peptidases leads to a global conformational change, which results in entrapment of the host peptidase and activation of the thioester bond that covalently binds the attacking host peptidase (PubMed:26100869, PubMed:26143919). Trapped peptidases are still active except against very large substrates (PubMed:26100869). May protect the entire periplam, including the lipoproteins anchored to the periplasmic side of the outer membrane, against intruding endopeptidases (PubMed:26100869). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18697741, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26100869, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26143919}. EcoCyc product frame: G7323-MONOMER. Genomic coordinates: 2647326-2652287. EcoCyc protein note: yfhM encodes an α2-macroglobulin which serves to protect the cell from host peptidases . YfhM functions as an irreversible, monomeric 'snap-trap'. Cleavage within the 'bait region' of YfhM by attacking host peptidases results in conformational rearrangment and exposure of an internal reactive thioester bond which covalently traps the peptidase and restricts its action...

## Enriched Summary

Maltodextrins are linear polymers of glucose molecules joined by an O-glycosidic (α-1->4) bond. The term is usally used to describe a mixture of chains that vary from 3-17 glucose units long. Maltodextrin is commonly used as a food additive, and is commercially produced from corn and wheat Starch by partial hydrolysis. This poly-6-phosphogluco-maltodextrin describes a maltodextrin that contains more than one α-linked glucosyl residue that is phosphorylated at the O6 position . While the phophorylation may occur at positions other than those shown in the attached structure, at least two phosphorylated residues exist in the molecule.

## Enriched Summary

Tryptophan tryptophylquinone (TTQ), the cofactor of CPLX-3247 (MADH), is formed by covalent cross-linking of two tryptophan residues and incorporation of two oxygen atoms into one of the indole rings to form a quinone. During the catalytic cycle, TTQ mediates electron transfer from substrate to a copper protein, amicyanin. These electrons are transferred to the respiratory chain via a c-type cytochrome .

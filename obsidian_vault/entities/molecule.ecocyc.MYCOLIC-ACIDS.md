Mycolic acids are the hallmark of the cell envelope of TAX-1773 and related species and genera. They comprise a homologous series of C60-C90 (2R)-alkyl-(3R)-hydroxy very long-chain fatty acids, and are found as either esters of Arabinogalactan or as free lipids in the form of trehalose dimycolate (TDM). Both forms contribute to forming the two leaflets of the mycobacterial outer membrane, also called the mycomembrane, which protects the organism from antibiotics and the host's immune system . There is a significant variability in the structure of mycolic acids among different species. The acids are formed by a Claisen-type condensation of two components - the meromycolic chain and a fatty acid. The meromycolic chain of TAX-1773 typically incorporates a hydroxy group at C-1, a cyclopropyl ring fused onto the C-16/C-17 bond (at either a cis or a trans configuration), a methoxyl or oxo group at C-35, and a methyl group at C-36. The modifications and their positions may change among strains and species. Several structural classes of mycolic acids exist.

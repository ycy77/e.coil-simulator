## Enriched Summary

Sulfones are a class of organosulfur compounds occurring widely in nature. They contain two carbon-sulfur bonds, with a hexavalent sulfur atom that is also linked to two oxygen atoms. All sulfones have the general structure: S(=O)2R2, where R2 represents linkage to two carbon atoms either within one (e.g. the heterocyclic ring in CPD-944) or two (e.g. the two methyl groups of CPD-10437) substituents.

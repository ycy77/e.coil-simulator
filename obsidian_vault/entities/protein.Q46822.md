## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Catalyzes the 1,3-allylic rearrangement of the homoallylic substrate isopentenyl (IPP) to its highly electrophilic allylic isomer, dimethylallyl diphosphate (DMAPP). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10099534, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10419945, ECO:0000269|PubMed:9603997}. Isopentenyl diphosphate (IPP) isomerase (Idi) catalyzes the reversible isomerization of isopentenyl diphosphate (IPP) to dimethylallyl diphosphate (DMAPP), a key step in the biosynthesis of isoprenoids. Idi catalyzes the reversible isomerization of IPP to DMAPP . The initial 5-6:1 ratio of IPP to DMAPP generated by CPLX0-7564 is adjusted to a final ratio of 3:7 by Idi . The enzyme requires two divalent cations for activity; a Zn2+ ion is located in a hexacoordinate His3Glu2 pocket and is part of the catalytic machinery, while the enzyme-substrate complex contains an additional Mg2+ ion that facilitates substrate binding. A wide variety of divalent metal ions can provide these functions . Crystal structures of free and metal-bound isopentenyl diphosphate isomerase enabled identification of the active site . Crystal structures of complexes with transition state analogues and irreversible inhibitors suggested a catalytic mechanism of protonation/deprotonation involving C67 and E116 , and crystal structures of site-directed mutants in C67 were obtained to confirm the proposed mechanism...

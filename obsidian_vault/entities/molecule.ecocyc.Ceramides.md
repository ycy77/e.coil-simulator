## Enriched Summary

A ceramide is a lipid molecule that is composed of Sphingoids and Fatty-Acids. Ceramides are found in high concentrations within the cell membrane and are one of the component lipids that make up Sphingomyelins sphingomyelin, one of the major lipids in the lipid bilayer.

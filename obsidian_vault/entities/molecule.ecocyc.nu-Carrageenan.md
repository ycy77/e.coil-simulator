The basic units of Carrageenans carrageenans are repeats of α-1,3 linked D-galactose and β-1,4- linked D-galactose units, which are substituted by sulfate. These bridges are responsible for the gelation that is typical to these compounds, by forming a double helix during the process. nu-Carrageenan is sulfated at position 4 of the β-1,4 linked D-galactose units and positions 2 and 6 of the α-1,3-linked galactose units.

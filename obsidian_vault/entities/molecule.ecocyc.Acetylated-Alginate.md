## Enriched Summary

Alginate is a major component of the cell walls of TAX-2870 (brown algae), and can make up to 20 to 30% of the total algal material. It is a linear polysaccharide of (1-4)-linked CPD-15205, with variable amounts of its C-5 epimer CPD-15204 . Alginate can absorb 200-300 times its own weight in water, forming a viscous gum. Algal alginate is not acetylated. Bacteria that belong to the TAX-286 and TAX-352 genera produce an acetylated form of alginate. The biological function of alginates differs between the two genera. In TAX-286 alginate is associated with biofilms, promoting bacterial adherence to target cell walls . Alginate production is a major contributing factor in chronic pulmonary infections by TAX-287, the causative agent of cystic fibrosis . In TAX-352 alginate is a structural element in the wall of the cyst, a desiccation-resistant capsule that is formed under adverse environmental conditions .

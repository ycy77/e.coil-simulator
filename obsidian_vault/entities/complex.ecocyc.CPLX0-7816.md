## Enriched Summary

YbaB is a widely conserved small protein. Like its ortholog from Borrelia burgdorferi, YbaB binds to DNA derived from the B. burgdorferi erpAB promoter. Binding can not be competed with poly(dI-dC) and is thus sequence- or topology-specific, although YbaB does not appear to bind to the same sequence as the B. burgdorferi protein. The structure of YbaB family of DNA-binding proteins is different from any other known DNA-binding protein . YbaB can be acetylated by the Salmonella enterica protein acetyltransferase . The protein was found to interact with EG11881-MONOMER HslU and can be degraded by the CPLX0-1163 . Expression of the ybaB gene is regulated by σE, suggesting involvement in the extracytoplasmic stress response . A ybaB deletion mutant is 976-fold more sensitive to UV-radiation and 643-fold more sensitive to X-radiation than wild type . A ybaB deletion mutant is more sensitive to kasugamycin than wild type . Overexpression of ybaB from the ASKA library plasmid allows increased expression of the human G protein-coupled receptor bradykinin receptor 2 (BR2) fused to GFP .

## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Toxic component of a type I toxin-antitoxin (TA) system. May be a toxic protein; overexpression causes cessation of growth and rapid membrane depolarization. Overexpression induces stress-response and a number of membrane protein genes. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18710431}. The small hydrophobic peptide ShoB is encoded within the small RNA sequence of ryfB . RyfB was identified in a cloning-based screen for small RNAs and shown to be expressed during exponential growth . Overexpression of ShoB is toxic to the cell by disrupting membrane integrity. Whole-genome gene expression analysis after induction of ShoB expression shows changes in expression of many stress response and membrane proteins . Overexpression of "RyfB" decreases swimming and swarming motility . ShoB is predicted to be a bitopic inner membrane protein .

## Enriched Summary

The peptide moiety of peptidoglycan, which is bound through its N terminus to the carboxyl group of muramate and contains L and D amino acids, reveals considerable variation. These peptides are synthesized as penetapeptides, but are often cleaved, resulting in a range in length of 2 to 5 amino acids. In addition, the peptide side-chain are often branched with additional amino acids. This compound class represnets the pentapeptide form. The amino acid linked directly to muramate is usually L-ALPHA-ALANINE, but in some cases can be replaced by GLY or SER . The most common amino acid in the second position is D-GLT, but in some cases (in a few coryneform bacteria) it is hydroxylated to CPD-48 . In some organisms the α-carboxyl group of D-GLT is modified by amidation or substituted by GLY , by GLYCINAMIDE , or by CPD0-1578 . The greatest variation occurs at position 3. The residue at this position is usually a diamino acid and is involved in cross-linking through the second amino group. The most common residue at this position is MESO-DIAMINOPIMELATE, which is present in probably all Gram-negative bacteria and in many other organisms, such as some species of bacilli, clostridia, lactobacilli, corynebacteria, propionibacteria, Actinomycetales, Myxobacteriales, Rickettsiae, and cyanobacteria (see PEPTIDOGLYCANSYN-PWY)...

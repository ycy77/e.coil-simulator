## Annotation

The MukBEF complex is the sole bacterial condensin known in E. coli. It functions as an ATPase-powered molecular machine that helps with global organization of the chromosome and its segregation during cell division. MukBEF is a SMC (structural maintenance of chromosomes) complex that is functionally and overall structurally similar to eukaryotic SMC complexes . MukBEF complex plays significant and positive roles in promoting mixing and relaxation of the chromosome . E. coli's MukBEF complex is a multimeric, ring-shaped structure that is present in multiple possible conformations both in vitro and in vivo. Electron microscopy studies indicate that the MukE and MukF subunits of the MukBEF complex associate with the terminal globular domains of the MukB homodimer . The MukEF complex appears to compete with DNA for binding to MukB; addition of MukEF to DNA-bound MukB displaces MukB from the DNA . Studies using real-time, single-molecule fluorescence imaging on live cells have determine various functional stoichiometric conformations of the MukBEF complex. One conformation, the half-saturated complex with a stoichiometry of B2(E2F)1 (i.e. MukB:E:F ratio = 2:2:1), is relatively stable and can bind DNA and ATP, while another conformation, the fully saturated MukBEF complex with a stoichiometry of B2(E2F)2 (i.e. MukB:E:F ratio = 2:4:2), is short-lived, putatively ATP-free and unable to bind DNA but able to form multimeric complexes. The minimal functional unit is a dimer of dimeric MukBEF (4:4:2) complexes with 8-10 of these complexes aggregating in 1-3 discrete chromosome-associated regions of the cell .

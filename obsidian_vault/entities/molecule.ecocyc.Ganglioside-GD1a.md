## Enriched Summary

A ganglioside is a molecule composed of Glycosphingolipids (Ceramides and Oligosaccharides) with one or more sialic acids (e.g. N-ACETYLNEURAMINATE) linked on the sugar chain. Ganglioside are components of the cell plasma membrane that modulates cell signal transduction events, and appear to concentrate in lipid rafts. The name ganglioside is derived from ganglion cells of the brain, from which the first gangliosides were isolated by the German scientist Ernst Klenk in 1942. More than 60 gangliosides are known, which differ from each other mainly in the position and number of N-ACETYLNEURAMINATE residues.

## Enriched Summary

The acid sugar KDO (known as Kdo for ketodeoxyoctonate) is a component of bacterial Lipopolysaccharides lipopolysaccharides (LPS) and of the CPD-14581 "rhamnogalacturonan II" (RG-II) pectin fraction of the primary cell walls of most higher plants, including dicots, monocots and gymnosperms . In bacteria Kdo is a part of the inner core of lipopolysaccharides. In the bacterium TAX-562 it is added to the lipid A precursor LIPID-IV-A , while in TAX-287 it is added directly to fully acylated Lipid-A "lipid A" . In most, but not all, bacteria two Kdo units are added to a lipid molecule (see KDOSYN-PWY). In some organisms such as TAX-727, TAX-666 and TAX-520 only one Kdo residue is incorporated (see PWY-7675), while lipopolysaccharides from members of the TAX-810 genus incorporate three Kdo units (see PWY-6467). TAX-22 species incorporate an CPD-18063 "aminated form of Kdo" (see PWY-7676), while in TAX-469, TAX-632 and TAX-615 one of the two Kdo units is replaced by CPD-18119 (D-glycero-D-talo-oct-2-ulopynosonate). When multiple Kdo units are incorporated, they are added by different linkages. The first unit is always added by a (2->6) glycosidic linkage, the second one by a (2->4) linkage, and the third one by a (2->8) linkage...

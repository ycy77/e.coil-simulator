yqjD (gene.b3098) is a gene entity. It encodes yqjD (protein.P64581). Encoded protein function: FUNCTION: Upon overexpression inhibits growth. EcoCyc product frame: G7612-MONOMER. Genomic coordinates: 3249375-3249680. EcoCyc protein note: YqjD is an inner membrane protein that associates with the 30S subunit of 70S and 100S ribosomes during stationary phase . The YqjD protein localizes to clusters of various sizes that are predominantly located at a cell pole, especially during stationary phase; the Min system is involved in localization of the protein . A YqjD-YFP fusion protein localizes mainly at the cell poles; the tail anchor domain of YqjD localizes to regions enriched in phosphatidic acid | Overexpression of yqjD inhibits cell growth . A yqjD mutant shows increased biofilm formation compared to wild type, but decreased biofilm formation when the derepressed conjugative plasmid R1drd19 is present . A ΔyqjD mutant also shows slightly decreased persister cell formation . yqjD is expressed during stationary phase, and expression is dependent on RpoS . Polyamines stimulate expression of yqjD . EG11447-MONOMER CsrA directly regulates yqjD expression post-transcriptionally; CsrA represses translation of yqjD during exponential growth .

## Enriched Summary

Cold shock-like protein CspF (CSP-F) CspF belongs to the family of cold shock proteins . Expression of cspF is highest during the mid-log growth phase in both defined rich and minimal medium at 37°C and is not cold-inducible . cspF belongs to a small set of genes whose expression is upregulated in long-term stationary phase (LTSP). Loss of cspF does not cause a competitive disadvantage in LTSP . The cspF gene is highly expressed in long-term stationary phase (LTSP) . Diverse expression responses of the cold shock proteins, CspA-I, were found under different stress conditions, specifically cold, heat, rich vs minimal media and oxidative stress caused by H2O2. Expression of cspF is significantly up in response to H2O2 stress conditions and to heat stress. Contrary to previous reports, the expression is down-regulated throughout the growth cycle in both minimal and rich media and does show significant up-regulation under conditions of cold shock . An in-silico comparative study of evolution, structure and function was carried out for the 9 CspA family of proteins in E. coli K-12.

## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: 1-Phosphatidyl-1D-myo-inositol 3,5-bisphosphate; 1,2-Diacyl-sn-glycero-3-phospho-(1'-myo-inositol-3',5'-bisphosphate) The phosphorylated derivatives of L-1-phosphatidyl-inositols "phosphatidylinositol" (PtdIns), collectively called phosphoinositides (PIs), are eukaryotic cell membrane phospholipids whose inositol head group is phosphorylated at position D3, D4, or D5, singly or in all combinations, to yield seven PI metabolites. The seven phosphoinositides found in animals include: Phosphatidylinositol monophosphates: CPD-177, CPD-1108 and 1-PHOSPHATIDYL-1D-MYO-INOSITOL-5-PHOSPHA. Phosphatidylinositol bisphophosphates: 1-PHOSPHATIDYL-1D-MYO-INOSITOL-34-BISPH, 1-PHOSPHATIDYL-1D-MYO-INOSITOL-35-BISPH, and PHOSPHATIDYL-MYO-INOSITOL-45-BISPHOSPHA. Phosphatidylinositol trisphophosphate: PHOSPHATIDYLINOSITOL-345-TRIPHOSPHATE. 1-PHOSPHATIDYL-1D-MYO-INOSITOL-35-BISPH "Phosphatidylinositol-3,5 bisphosphosphate" is vital in the differentiation and development of multicellular organisms. In mammals, this compound is synthesized from CPD-177 only by the very large enzyme, HS03825-MONOMER. A knockout of this enzyme or its associated regulator ENSG00000103043-MONOMER is lethal .

## Enriched Summary

Sulfolipids are a class of lipids that possess a sulfur-containing functional group. An abundant class of sulfolipids, found in many photosynthetic organisms, including all photosynthetic plants, algae, cyanobacteria, and purple sulfur and non-sulfur bacteria, is SULFOQUINOVOSYLDIACYLGLYCEROL "sulfoquinovosyl diacylglycerol" (SQDG), which is composed of a glycoside of CPD-10247 and 1-2-Diglycerides diacylglycerol. Other important sulfolipids include Cerebroside-3-sulfates sulfatide, which is abundant in myelin, and Seminolipids seminolipid, which is the most common glycolipid in mammalian testes.

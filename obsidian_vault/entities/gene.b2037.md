## Enriched Summary

rfbX (gene.b2037) is a gene entity. It encodes rfbX (protein.P37746). Encoded protein function: FUNCTION: May be involved in the translocation process of the nascent O-polysaccharide molecules and/or its ligation to lipid A core units. EcoCyc product frame: RFBX-MONOMER. EcoCyc synonyms: wzx, wzxB. Genomic coordinates: 2108337-2109584. EcoCyc protein note: RfbX, also known as Wzx, WzxB, and Wzx(O16), is the flippase that translocates undecaprenylpyrophosphate (Und-PP) linked O-antigen subunits across the inner membrane. The genes encoding the enzymes involved in the biosynthesis of O-specific polysaccharides are clustered in the rfb region. E. coli K-12 is phenotypically rough and does not express O-antigen due to mutations in the rfb region; E. coli K-12 MG1655 contains the rfb-50 mutation - an IS5 element which disrupts EG11986 wbbL encoding rhamnose transferase; an engineered strain of E. coli K-12 with all rfb genes intact synthesizes CPD0-2249 O16 antigen . An engineered strain of E. coli K-12 MG1655 which contains an intact wbbL (MG1655 wbbL+) displays increased resistance to phage P1 and complement-mediated killing and and an enhanced ability to colonize and kill Caenorhabditis elegans .

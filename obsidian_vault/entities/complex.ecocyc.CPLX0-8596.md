## Annotation

RecF is a recombination mediator protein in the RecF pathway for the homology-directed repair of post-replicative single-stranded DNA gaps. Early studies implicated RecF in a minor pathway of conjugational recombination that operates independently of the RECBCD RecBCD pathway and in post-replication DNA repair (also ). The recF143 mutation (RecFG286R ) confers UV sensitivity and a Rec- (recombination deficient) phenotype in a strain which lacks the major RecBC pathway of recombination (strain JC7623 recB- recC- sbcB-) . RecF, EG10834-MONOMER RecR, and EG10832-MONOMER RecO may function together as a complex . RecF, RecO, and RecR function at an early stage of recombinational repair and affect the interaction of EG10823-MONOMER RecA, CPLX0-8165 SSB, and ssDNA (also ). The RecF, RecO, and RecR proteins mediate the loading of RecA protein onto SSB-coated gapped DNA (and further ). RecFOR and RecOR may define two distinct RecA loading pathways in TAX-562 . The RecF pathway can promote recombinational repair of dsDNA breaks, and joint molecule formation between linear dsDNA and a homologous supercoiled recipient has been reconstituted in vitro with purified proteins (RecA, RecF, RecO, RecR, EG10830-MONOMER RecJ, EG10833-MONOMER RecQ, and SSB); omission of RecFOR abolishes joint molecule formation but use of the gain-of-function mutant RecA730 protein significantly restores formation when RecFOR is absent . RecF and RecR are required to reinitiate replication from a disrupted replication fork; the UV sensitivity of recF and recR null mutants is a result of the failure to resume replication at disrupted forks post irradiation (and see ).

## Enriched Summary

"Glycine cleavage repressor," GcvR, is an accessory protein that binds directly to GcvA, bending DNA to form a repression complex (GcvA/GcvR) in the regulatory region of the gcvT operon . Glycine binds directly to GcvR to disrupt or block the association of the GcvA/GcvR complex, whereas purines appear to promote the formation of the repression complex through an unknown mechanism . In the absence of glycine and the presence of GcvR, the GcvA/GcvR complex represses the operon involved in the glycine cleavage enzyme system . The GcvR binding landscape was globally mapped in vivo by standardized ChIP-seq and quantitatively modeled using BoltzNet, a biophysically informed neural network that infers binding energy from sequence data .

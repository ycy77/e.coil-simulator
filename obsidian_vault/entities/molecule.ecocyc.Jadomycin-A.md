## Enriched Summary

The jadomycins are a unique family of angucycline-derived antibiotics. Jadomycin A is produced by the organism TAX-953739 under stress conditions such as heat shock, phage infection, and particularly ethanol treatment. This compound is bioactive against Gram-positive (including the methicillin-resistant TAX-1280) and Gram-negative bacteria, but unlike Jadomycin-B, it is not active against yeast . The difference between the two compounds is that Jadomycin-B includes an CPD-11914 moiety . Jadomycin A exists as a diastereomeric mixture of CPD-12938 and CPD-21699 , with the former being the predominant form at a ratio of 10:1.

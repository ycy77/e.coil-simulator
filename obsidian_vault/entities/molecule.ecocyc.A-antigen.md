## Enriched Summary

The A antigen is an oligosaccharide that decorates proteins on the surface of red blood cells in people with A blood type. It is generated from H-antigen, which is found in people with O blood type, by the attachement of an CPD-12557 residue by a 1,3 linkage to one of the β-D-galactosyl residues of the H antigen. People with AB blood type contain both A-antigen "A antigens" and B-antigen "B antigens". The majority of the A, B and H antigens are linked to the ends of long polylactosamine chains attached mainly to band 3 protein (encoded by SLC4A1), the anion exchange protein of the red blood cells. A minor fraction of the antigens is attached to a ceramide moiety, forming neutral glycosphingolipids.

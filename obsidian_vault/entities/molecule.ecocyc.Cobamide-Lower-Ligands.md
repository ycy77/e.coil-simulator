## Enriched Summary

Cobamides "A cobamide" is a corrinoid molecule based on the structure of CPD-20843, a cobalt-corrinoid hexaamide that is formed from COBINAMIDE by the attachment of an α-D-ribofuranose 3-phosphate residue at the 2 position of the aminopropanol chain. Cobamides serve as cofactors for a number of important enzymes. A key structural feature of cobinamides is a corrin ring with a cobalt ion in its center. Four of the six coordination sites of the cobalt ion are provided by the corrin ring, while the other two are provided by Cobamide-Upper-Ligands upper (β) and Cobamide-Lower-Ligands lower (α) ligands, which are located above and below the ring plane, respectively. The best known cobamide, Cobalamins cobalamin, contains DIMETHYLBENZIMIDAZOLE as the lower ligand and either a methyl group or 5'-deoxyadenosyl group as the upper ligand. At least 16 different cobamides with structural variability in the lower ligand have been described. The 16 bases reported so far are BENZIMIDAZOLE, CPD-18320, CPD-18321, CPD-18497, CPD-18495, DIMETHYLBENZIMIDAZOLE, ADENINE, CPD-18323, GUANINE, HYPOXANTHINE, PHENOL, CPD-108, CPD-5723, CPD-20865, CPD-20864, and CPD-20885 . Different cobamides may not be functionally equivalent as cofactors for cobamide-dependent enzymes...

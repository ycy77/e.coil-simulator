## Enriched Summary

The EbgA-EbgC complex represents the second β-D-galactosidase enzyme in E. coli. The wild-type enzyme is too catalytically feeble to allow growth on lactose as the sole carbon source. Spontaneous mutations that enable growth on lactose occur under selective conditions in the absence of LacZ . The ebg and lac operons are homologous and probably descended from a common ancestor . The EbgAC enzyme is a heterooctamer . Catalytic activity of the large (EbgA) subunit alone suggests that the small subunit is associated with the optimal positioning of the electrophilic Mg2+ ions in the enzyme .

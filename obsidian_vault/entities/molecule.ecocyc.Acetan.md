## Enriched Summary

Acetan Acetan is an acidic heteropolysaccharide produced by the bacterium TAX-28448 (previously known as Acetobacter xylinum) that consists of a cellulose backbone with side-chains attached to alternate glucose units. The polymer is composed of repeating units of a heptasaccharide with the following structure: α-L-rhamnose-(1->6)-β-D-glucose-(1->6)-α-D-glucose-(1->4)-β-D-glucuronate-(1->2)-α-D-mannose-(1->3)-β-D-glucose-(1->4)-β-D-glucose. These repeating units are attached to each other via β(1->4) links between the terminal two glucose units, forming a cellulose backbone with alternating pentasaccharide branches . Acetan is structurally related to Xanthan.

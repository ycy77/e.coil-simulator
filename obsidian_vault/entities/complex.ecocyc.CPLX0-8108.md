## Annotation

IbpA belongs to the widely conserved family of small heat-shock proteins, molecular chaperones that contain a conserved α-crystallin domain flanked by variable N- and C-terminal tails. IbpA binds to aggregated proteins localized to the older cell poles and to inclusion bodies formed during heterologous protein expression . IbpA can suppress thermal aggregation of model proteins . IbpA/IbpB, ClpB, and the DnaK system form a functional triad of chaperones . The protein interaction network for chaperones has been mapped . The activities of IbpA and IbpB together promote disaggregation of protein aggregates by chaperones . IbpA reporter fusions with non-monomeric fluorescent proteins were found to overestimate of the prevalence of protein aggregates in the cell. Protein aggregates due to sublethal heat stress are asymmetrically inherited; cells that inherit the aggregates are more likely to survive subsequent heat stress . Conversely, the ability to eliminate aggregates of damaged proteins in minicells appears to decrease the doubling time of daughter cells . Localization of IbpB to the S fraction of aggregated proteins appears to be dependent on IbpA . Molecular dynamics simulation has been used to model heat activation of IbpA/IbpB heterooligomer formation . Presence of IbpA and IbpB together stabilizes protein substrates in a disaggregation-competent state . IbpA and IbpB appear to have different functions within protein aggregates . Under DnaK-DnaJ-GrpE (KJE)-limited conditions, assemblies of IbpA/B and denatured protein delay refolding of the aggregated proteins, while under KJE-replete conditions, refolding is accelerated. KJE dissociates IbpA/B from the protein aggregate, enabling refolding by ClpB . IbpA and denatured protein assemblies require higher amounts of KJE

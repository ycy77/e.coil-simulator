## Enriched Summary

ymgF (gene.b4520) is a gene entity. It encodes ymgF (protein.P58034). Encoded protein function: FUNCTION: Could be involved in cell division. May participate in the stabilization of the cell divisome under specific conditions. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:18978050}. EcoCyc product frame: MONOMER0-2141. Genomic coordinates: 1218983-1219201. EcoCyc protein note: YmgF is an inner membrane protein with two transmembrane domains; both the N- and the C-terminus are located in the cytoplasm. YmgF interacts with the cell division proteins FtsI, FtsN and FtsQ as well as other components of the cell division machinery , and it localizes to the septum during cell division. This localization is dependent on FtsZ, FtsA, FtsQ and FtsN . The N-terminal part of the protein may be required for dimerization and for its interactions with cell division proteins . YmgF is not essential for viability. In low osmolarity medium, overexpression of ymgF can partially rescue the growth defect of the ftsQ1(ts) mutant . Expression of ymgF has been detected under standard growth conditions . Expression of ymgF was significantly though oppositely affected by mioC and gidA, two genes which flank oriC .

## Enriched Summary

In E. coli K-12 the major proteins of a PWY-8147 "Type I cyctochrome c biogenesis" pathway are encoded in an 8-gene cluster (ccmABCDEFGH). Numerous studies have examined the interaction between Ccm proteins; various complexes have been reported and the possibility of a large 'maturase' complex has also been considered (see ). The CcmABCD complex represented here consists of the heme-binding inner membrane protein CcmC, the small inner membrane protein CcmD and the ATPase subcomplex CcmAB. During cytochrome c biosynthesis, heme is moved across the inner membrane concomitant with the formation of a CcmC-heme-CcmE-CcmD complex (CcmCDE-Complex-Heme "holo-CcmCDE"); subsequent release of holo-CcmE (for eventual heme transfer to apocytochromes c in the periplasm) requires CcmAB-Complex "CcmAB"-mediated ATP hydolysis (see and reviews by ). High resolution cryo-EM structures of CcmABCD in varying forms (ATP-bound, heme-bound, CcmE-bound etc) have been determined and a structural basis for heme binding and release explored .

## Enriched Summary

FUNCTION: Upon overexpression inhibits growth. YqjD is an inner membrane protein that associates with the 30S subunit of 70S and 100S ribosomes during stationary phase . The YqjD protein localizes to clusters of various sizes that are predominantly located at a cell pole, especially during stationary phase; the Min system is involved in localization of the protein . A YqjD-YFP fusion protein localizes mainly at the cell poles; the tail anchor domain of YqjD localizes to regions enriched in phosphatidic acid | Overexpression of yqjD inhibits cell growth . A yqjD mutant shows increased biofilm formation compared to wild type, but decreased biofilm formation when the derepressed conjugative plasmid R1drd19 is present . A ΔyqjD mutant also shows slightly decreased persister cell formation . yqjD is expressed during stationary phase, and expression is dependent on RpoS . Polyamines stimulate expression of yqjD . EG11447-MONOMER CsrA directly regulates yqjD expression post-transcriptionally; CsrA represses translation of yqjD during exponential growth . YqjD belongs to the heterogeneous group of C-tail-anchored membrane proteins . YqjD, G7173 ElaB, and B0899-MONOMER "YgaM" are paralogs .

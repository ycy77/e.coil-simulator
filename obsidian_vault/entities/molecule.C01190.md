Cerebrosides consist of Ceramides with a single sugar residue at the 1-hydroxyl moiety. The sugar residue can be either glucose or galactose, and the two major types are thus named Glucosyl-ceramides glucocerebrosides and D-galactosylceramide galactocerebrosides. Galactocerebrosides are typically found in neural tissue, while glucocerebrosides are found in other tissues. Based on the notation GlcCer(m:n)-p used to name subclasses of glucosylceramides, the total number of carbons present in the sphingoid base and the fatty amide is "m" while the total number of double bonds is "n." The total number of hydroxyl groups (not including the hydroxyl group used as the site of the head group attachment) is "p."

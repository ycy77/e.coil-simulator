## Enriched Summary

Flavin-N5-oxides are modified flavin cofactors that are used by some enzymes. Their production involves a two-electron oxidation of an oxidized flavin or four-electron oxidation of a reduced flavin. CPD-22329 was first observed in the EncM-catalyzed 1,3-diketone oxidation in CPD-12319 biosynthesis (see PWY-8063). Flavin-N5-oxides were later reported from the catabolic enzymes G6523 RutA (involved in URACIL catabolism of TAX-562) and G-494 DszA (from CPD-942 catabolism of Rhodococcus erythropolis) .

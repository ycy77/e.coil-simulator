## Enriched Summary

Uncharacterized HTH-type transcriptional regulator YgbI YgbI was predicted to be a DeoR-type transcription factor . It contains a helix-turn-helix motif to bind DNA in its N-terminal domain . YgbI DNA-binding activity was probed in vivo in glucose M9 minimal medium through chromatin immunoprecipitation assays (ChIP-exo), which identified a single binding region in between the ygbI and ygbJK divergent transcription units . By RNA-seq of the wild-type and ygbI knockout strains, it was determined that transcription of ygbJ and ygbK is negatively regulated by YgbI . In conjunction, ChIP-exo and RNA-seq studies indicated that ygbJK is a direct target of YgbI . Upstream of the ygbI gene, a sequence was found that could work as a transcriptional promoter, and the transcription of the ygbI gene is affected by glucose-lactose shift and heat shock . A ygbI knockout strain showed growth in M9 minimal medium with 20 mM L-tartrate, while the wild-type strain was unable to grow under these culture conditions . The deletion of ygbI substantially decreases viability in soil .

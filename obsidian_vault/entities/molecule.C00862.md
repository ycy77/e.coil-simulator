## Enriched Summary

KEGG compound: Methanofuran; Carbon dioxide reduction factor EcoCyc common name: a methanofuran. Methanofurans are a group of related carbon-carrier cofactors involved in the first step of the methanogenic reduction of CARBON-DIOXIDE (see METHANOGENESIS-PWY). Methanofurans react with CARBON-DIOXIDE to form the carbamate N-carboxymethanofuran, which is reduced to Formyl-methanofurans by EC-1.2.7.12. In addition, the cofactor also participates at a later stage of all methanogenesis pathways. These pathways converge at the compound Me-CoM, which is then disproportionated into CH4 and CARBON-DIOXIDE; the latter process involves methanofuran (see PWY-5209). Methanofuran is also used by methylotrophic bacteria as a coenzyme to oxidize formaldehyde to formate . Methanofurans are the only cofactors known to contain a furan moiety, although furans have been identified in polyketides and fatty acid derived natural products, and are often formed during the heating or oxidation of carbohydrates . The chemical structure of methanofuran varies among different methanogens. At this time (2014) five variants have been characterized: CPD-17181 (from TAX-145262) , CPD-7628 (from TAX-2208) , CPD-7629 (from TAX-2173) , methanofuran-d (from TAX-2190) and CPD-17182 (from TAX-39152, TAX-2187 and TAX-155863)...

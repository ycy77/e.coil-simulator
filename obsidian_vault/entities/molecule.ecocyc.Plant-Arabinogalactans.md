## Enriched Summary

Arabinogalactans are biopolymers consisting of arabinose and galactose residues. Two main classes of arabinogalactans are found in nature: Plant-Arabinogalactans "plant arabinogalactan" and Mycobacterial-Arabinogalactans "microbial arabinogalactan". Plant-Arabinogalactans "Plant arabinogalactans" (AGs) are widely spread throughout the plant kingdom. Many edible and inedible plants are rich sources of these polysaccharides. AGs are associated with the pectin cell-wall component by physical bonds, and some of them are covalently linked to the complex pectin molecule as neutral side chains . Two main classes of arabinogalactans are found in nature: Plant-Arabinogalactans "plant arabinogalactan" and Mycobacterial-Arabinogalactans "microbial arabinogalactan". Plant-Arabinogalactans "Plant arabinogalactans" (AGs) are widely spread throughout the plant kingdom. Many edible and inedible plants are rich sources of these polysaccharides. It is more widespread than the AG type I and occurs in cell walls of dicots and cereals often linked to proteins (known as arabinogalactan proteins).

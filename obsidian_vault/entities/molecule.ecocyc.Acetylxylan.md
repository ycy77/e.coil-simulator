## Enriched Summary

1-4-beta-Xylan (1→4)-β-D-xylan is present in some trees and other plants in a partially acetylated form. Angiosperm (hardwood) xylans (e.g. from beechwood) have a high rate of substitution (70-80%) by acetyl groups at position 2 and/or 3 of the β-D-xylopyranosyl residues, conferring on the xylan its partial solubility in water . 1-4-beta-Xylan (1→4)-β-D-xylan is present in some trees and other plants in a partially acetylated form.
